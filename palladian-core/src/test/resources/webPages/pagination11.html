      Review from John C.

      •  
      • 27 friends
      • 164 reviews
      • John C.

      Los Angeles, CA

      9/20/2009

      Looking for a notebook? Looking to drop that old Pentium POS and step up to something better, faster and lighter? Then don't go here.

      In the market for a notebook, I had gone to http://lenovo.com and configured a notebook. Just to give retailers a try, I stopped by a number of them including the http://Notebookshop.com Cerritos location. It was a waste of time.

      - The retail portion of the place is just the front office of a warehouse and it doesn't look presentable at all
      - The selection is poor, with an odd collection of brand offerings
      - The entire staff is Korean and even though I am Korean myself, I generally don't patronize Korean consumer good businesses - sans Korean restaurants and bars - because they exploit non-English speaking, not technically savvy Koreans into buying overpriced stuff they can normally get for less at mass market retailers
      - Their selection is overpriced... the sales person tried to pressure me into purchasing a notebook that had less features and an average reputation for notebooks (Fujitsu)
      - I told the salesperson that I was looking for a notebook with a built-in mobile broadband card... he misunderstood and pointed out the AGN card on the Fujitsu. No, buddy... I said wireless broadband / WAN, not a simple wireless card
      - There's no way to configure much of anything. Sure if you want to buy a preconfigured computer, that's great... but for anyone who has specific needs (such as a mobile broadband card), you want the flexibility. What if you don't want a 5,400rpm hard drive? What if you want 7,200rpm or a SSD (solid state drive) instead? Not possible here. (although I have to say that even Best Buy nor Fry's offers much customization save for HP, I believe... but HP computers are crap anyways so that's irrelevant)

      To cut a long story short, I ended up buying a Lenovo with the premium bells and whistles for $400 less than the Fujitsu the salesperson at http://Notebookshop.com pointed me to. Full retail on the Lenovo is actually higher, but with the current sale going on til the 29th of September, plus a discount code I found online, I ended up with a better computer for less money. The lesson here is buying direct from the manufacturer - which is the norm for computers nowadays - is more efficient, completely customizable and saves you money. That's not the case at this retailer.
