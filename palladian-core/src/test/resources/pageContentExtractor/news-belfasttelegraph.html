£750m IT system overhaul to change the face of education

Close

Pictured at the launch of the Education Authority (EA) Education Information Solutions (EdIS) Programme are pupils from St Patrick’s High School, Keady: Patrick Nugent (Senior Head Boy) and Niamh Carvill (Senior Head Girl) with (L-R) Michelle Rafferty, Teacher, St Patrick’s High School, Keady; Sara Long, EA Chief Executive; Dr Fionnuala Moore, Principal, St Patrick’s High School, Keady and EA Director of Education, Michele Corkey.

Schools in Northern Ireland are set to benefit from a new multi-functional online information system after an investment of £750m.

The Education Authority (EA) launched the Education Information Solutions (EdIS) Programme, a digital system which will support the learning experience for children and young people over the next 10 years.

Having worked on the network for the last five years, with the first big test getting a thumbs up from parents and schools in handling the application process for parents of next year’s P1 pupils last month, the EA is ready move the system into the wider educational environment.

The EA also said the system will be taking over the running of he NISTR substitute teacher register for the start of the next academic year.

EdIS will facilitate learning and teaching through technology with continuous improvement at its core and will transform children and young people’s learning experience, develop skills for the future, support programmes to improve educational outcomes and facilitate professional learning for school staff.

The introduction of the new system will be gradual, but will see a phasing out of the old C2K system as more applications are made available over the next few years.

EdIS will also deliver a rolling programme of devices for staff and pupils to support our children and young people to be the best that they can be and improve how school staff, parents, young people, stakeholders and customers access a range of important services. 20,000 state-of-the-art laptops have been distributed to teachers to date.

Pupils from St Patrick’s High School, Keady supported in the development of the EdIS brand, using the name of Thomas Edison in what they called a ‘light bulb’ moment, which also resulted in the new logo for the EA programme.

Sara Long, EA Chief Executive said the launch represented the beginning of an exciting journey.

“EdIS will be fundamental in providing modern, efficient and effective services and service delivery that will simplify school administration for school staff and wider education stakeholders and at the same time increase parental engagement and support teaching using technology,” she said.

“EdIS will deliver a joined up technology environment underpinned by a data analytics warehouse and platform that will provide analysis and reports to inform future policy making and support pupil centric learning to inspire and support our children and young people to be the best they can be.”

Michele Corkey, EA Director of Education, said the role technology played during Covid shows just what can be achieved.

“We were in a must do scenario when schools had to adapt to home learning. Schools jumped ten years in 18 months,” she said.

“The C2K contract was coming to an end. We had to plan. We asked everyone what they thought they needed and have taken everything on board.

“We needed to make it all more agile, more accessible, user friendly and up to date.

“Metamorphosis is the word that comes to mind. We want all users to be sitting down by 2024 and not realise they’re using a completely different system.

“All schools have been crying out for a one size fits all system, available to teachers, pupils, parents and governors and we’re delighted to be officially launching it.

The programme has been received continuous funding from Department of Education and Department of Finance since inception.

“We will continue to work with them to ensure our children have the best provision available. It’s just the start of a process,” said Ms Corkey.
