Acme Packet Net-Net session border controllers

Acme Packet, a provider of session border control, enables service providers to deliver high-quality, end-to-end interactive communications (voice, video and multimedia sessions) across multiple IP networks - just like a voice call today spans multiple circuit-switched PBX, local and long distance networks. The Net-Net family of session border controllers has been selected by 9 of the top 10, and 16 of the top 25 service providers in the world to satisfy critical security, service assurance and law enforcement requirements in wireline, cable and wireless networks.

This purpose-built network equipment (hardware and software) performs these critical roles at three types of IP-IP network boundaries: Peering border between service providers for PSTN termination/origination, IP transit, or IP origination/termination; Access-backbone network border for hosted consumer and business services such as voice & video, IP PBX transport, unified messaging, conferencing, multimedia collaboration, presence and IC, gaming, etc; Data center border for voice ASP retail or wholesale services such as voice, calling card, director services, messaging, customer care/contact centers, clearinghouse/exchanges.

Acme Packet's deployments support all applications - from trunking to hosted enterprise and residential services; all protocols - SIP, H.323, MGCP/NCS and H.248; and all border points - peering, access network and data center. The company has local presence in 12 countries and 17 cities.

Acterna's displays on BBWFE 2005

HST-3000

The Acterna HST-3000 is a modular, hand-held test tool that scales to address a broad spectrum of different access network technology testing needs, from the physical layer to the application layer. It was the first hand-held field test tool to provide basic turn-up and troubleshooting of VoIP deployment, supporting physical layer analysis through packet metrics, signaling analysis and voice quality determination. HST-3000 capabilities allow packet capture of live VoIP calls, including VoIP call storage and an integrated FTP capability to send captured VoIP calls to be analyzed by Acterna's PVA-1000 software. The HST was created to improve efficiency and lower the costs of installing, maintaining, and troubleshooting business and residential services and networks. The HST-3000 combines copper, voice and data service testing features with unparalleled process automation capabilities in a lightweight, battery-operated unit.

By adding ADSL2+ and IP Video test options, the HST-3000 breaks new ground in test support for triple-play service deployments and extends its capabilities for installation and maintenance crews who will be responsible for turn-up and troubleshooting these new services. The Acterna HST-3000 VoIP Tester was named Product of the Year for 2002 by INTERNET TELEPHONY magazine.

NetComplete

Service providers can maximize revenue while lowering costs by removing operational inefficiencies within and between these areas. ONMS

Residing on a server or desktop, Acterna's OFM software enables customers to address the state of the network from their fingertips. ADVA o display FSP family

The FSP 150 is a family of low cost Ethernet transport products for access networks. The family is comprised of a demarcation unit (FSP 150CP) located at the customer premises and a set of aggregation products (FSP 150MO/ME/MG), which can be deployed within the access infrastructure in order to make more effective usage of core network resources.

The unique packet aggregation scheme provides maximum bandwidth efficiency yet maintains the operational simplicity of a connection oriented transport solution. Thanks to the service agnostic nature of the FSP 150 long term protection of access investment is guaranteed.

The FSP 150 enables mass rollout of innovative Ethernet services by removing the complexity and indeterminacy of classical Ethernet switching from the access network.

FSP 2000

The ADVA FSP 2000 is a flexible Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) system for all high-speed data, storage, voice, and video applications.

The platform's new 4Gbit/s muxponder card enables service providers to further reduce cost-per-bit-transport in broadband backhaul environments. The unique design supports up to 4 Gigabit Ethernet streams on a single 4G wavelength. Or any combination of 1G Ethernet, 1G Fibre Channel (FC), and 2G FC signals.

Storage service providers are now able to offer the new 4G FC protocol in a card that is fully backward compatible with existing 1G and 2G FC interfaces. End users benefit from a non-disruptive upgrade of their storage area networks (SAN) to all Fibre Channel variants without delays or expensive equipment replacements.

FSP 3000

The FSP 3000 is a scalable Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) system that supports new revenue opportunities such as triple-play, high-speed Ethernet, and SAN services. The platform is highly competitive in DSL backhaul scenarios and has been deployed in several broadband rollout programs across Europe.

The unique optical layer design of the FSP 3000 combines lowest first-in cost with maximum scalability. The system supports in-service CWDM to DWDM migration as well as the hybrid implementation of static and dynamic photonic components including tunable lasers and Remote Optical Add Drop Multiplexers (ROADM).

The transparent fiber-in fiber-out interfaces support all protocols between the range of 8Mbit/s and 40Gbit/s, including Ethernet, SONET/SDH, G.709 OTN, ATM, video, and all common SAN protocols.
