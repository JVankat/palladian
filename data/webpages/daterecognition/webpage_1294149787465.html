Published Monday January 25, 2010

Prosecutor seeks re-election

By Jason Kuiper
WORLD-HERALD STAFF WRITER

« Crime/Courts
Share

Douglas County Attorney Don Kleine officially filed for re-election Monday to remain the county’s top law enforcement officer.

Kleine, 57, first was elected to the position in 2006. He had served as chief deputy county attorney for 12 years before becoming chief of the criminal bureau of the Nebraska Attorney General’s Office from 2003 to 2006.

So far, no one has filed to run against Kleine. The deadline for filing for the May primary election is March 1.

"I look forward to the opportunity of serving in this position for the people of Douglas County," Kleine said.

"I want to continue the progress we’ve made in bringing this office into the 21st century. The most important thing is we’ve run an office that has been efficient in the use of taxpayer dollars and yet has been successful in prosecuting the most serious cases that occur here in Douglas County. It’s a position I love and want to stay in."
