Will Temporary Census Worker Layoffs Affect the Market?

Two weeks ago I posted an article ("More than Half a Million Job Losses Coming") warning that a large reduction in employment would occur in the third quarter as temporary Census workers were laid off.

Calculated Risk points out that Census payroll numbers have already started to decline, as shown in the following graph.

For large image, click here.

This week (on Wednesday, June 23) the payroll number for temporary census workers for the week of June 6-12 will be released. This is important because the week containing the 12th of each month is the week that is used to determine the non-farms payroll number for the entire month. If the trend of the past three weeks is continued there could be a job loss from reduction of temporary Census workers of more than 150,000. This will be included in the next Employment Situation report from the U.S. Dept. of Labor for June (due 8:30am July 2).

The initial reaction to the headline number may be emotional, not logical, especially in front of a holiday weekend. But there is a logical element, as well, and that will influence market reaction as the day progresses on July 2. The expectation for recovery includes adding an average of at least 150,000 private sector jobs per month. This was discussed at TheStreet.com.

If the market is affected in the first half of July by the next employment report, the cause will be disappointing private sector employment numbers. It will not be the headline number containing the decline in temporary Census positions.

The fact that Weekly Initial Unemployment Claims are not behaving well (discussed ten days ago in a second article at TheStreet.com and continuing to degrade last week) is adding to the current negative environment for the effects of employment data on economic outlook and the stock market.

In recent months state and local government employment has been dropping and an increase from that source for June seems unlikely. That means that private non-farm payrolls must increase by at least the amount the Census payroll declines or June will show a loss of payroll jobs. Even if anticipated, a decline in non-farms payrolls for June could produce a negative reaction in the market. Investors may want to consider adding some puts or other hedges to prepare for that possibility.

Disclosure: Author is long several S&P 500 stocks.
