Worldwide Youth in Science and Engineering
 (WYSE) Academic Challenge
St. Louis regional
February 22, 2005
                                                

Approximately 100 juniors and seniors from Missouri high schools competed in the St. Louis regional competition in the Worldwide Youth in Science and Engineering (WYSE) Academic Challenge held at McKendree College Feb. 22.

The students hail from five high schools, including Trinity Catholic, Visitation, Chaminade, Maplewood-Richmond Heights, and St. Charles West. Trinity Catholic WYSE Coach Frank Cange said, �This competition is an excellent opportunity to challenge our best students.�  Visitation is an all-girl school, and Visitation Coach  Harold Gebhardt said the Academic challenge provides an outstanding venue for girls to prove they are "just as smart as boys in math and science.�

This was the second year that McKendree College hosted the St. Louis regional, and St. Charles West Coach Joan Twillman said, �My students loved this competition last year and really wanted to come back.  McKendree takes very good care of us. The facilities are great.�

The purpose of WYSE is to foster excellence in mathematics and sciences among the youth of the United States and the world. The immediate goal is to attract a greater number of talented and diverse students to careers in engineering and the sciences.

The WYSE Academic Challenge is a series of competitive tests in seven subjects designed to motivate high school students to higher achievement in the fields of math and science. Students compete in teams or as individuals in regional, sectional and state finals competitions in the following subjects: biology, chemistry, computer science, engineering graphics, English, mathematics, and physics.

McKendree College President James M. Dennis presented ribbons to the top performers.
