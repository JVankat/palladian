JOHN L. SMITH: Riviera publicist tries to make star come out for PR legend McDonald

Is there a star in the late Herb McDonald's future?

There might be if Riviera publicist John Neeland has his way.

Advertisement

McDonald was a legend in the local public relations business in the more than 50 years he spent promoting Las Vegas. When his pal Harvey Diederich recently suggested McDonald's memory be secured with a place of honor somewhere along the Strip that he surely helped create, I liked the idea enough to write a column about it.

Neeland, the affable PR veteran at the 50-year-old Riviera, is a member of the Las Vegas Walk of Stars committee. Although sidewalk stars are usually reserved for the entertainers who filled Strip showrooms, he says McDonald's importance to the development of Las Vegas is great enough to warrant an honor beside the likes of Wayne Newton and Elvis. He planned to introduce McDonald's name at this week's committee meeting.

"If not a statue, Herb deserves a humanitarian star," Neeland says.

Neeland can count on support from K.J. Howe and a number of other publicity veterans. Howe was responsible for promoting the Mint 400 off-road race. McDonald came through for Howe during the early days of the Mint, which for several years was one of the best-known off-road races in the world.

"Herb was always someone you could go to, to assist with a project or provide direction if you were a bit astray," Howe recalls. "He picked up the phone and called his contacts at the three networks in New York so I could make a presentation regarding TV rights for the Mint 400. He had me set up within five minutes."

THITCHENER JUDGMENT: District Judge Elizabeth Gonzalez has signed off on a $4.58 million judgment against Countrywide Home Loans, which inadvertently sold the condominium owned by Air Force serviceman Gerald Thitchener and his wife, Katrina.

A jury on Sept. 30 returned a verdict against the home mortgage giant after a four-day trial. The judgment includes more than $1.8 million in usual damages and interest and $2.5 million in punitive damages in a case argued on the plaintiffs' behalf by attorneys Terry Coffing and Terry Moore.

The judge's order promises to be a public relations nightmare for Countrywide. The judge wrote that the plaintiffs convincingly proved "conscious disregard" by the company.

TOPLESS, TIPLESS: Cab company sources say topless clubs at least temporarily have discontinued the lucrative and controversial practice of tipping drivers to drop off passengers at the nightclubs. The alcohol-free operations continue to tip drivers.

It might not last, but the drying up of the cash flow comes at a most inopportune time for the cabbies.

VIVA CARLOS: Carlos Ramirez, the stroke survivor who ran in Sunday's New Las Vegas Marathon, improved his usual last-place finish by 10 notches despite dragging his left side from the effects of the stroke he suffered nearly a decade ago.

Ramirez should have won a medal for inspiring the rest of us to try a little harder and complain a little less.

IRON GRANDMOTHER: "Barbara Vucanovich: From Nevada to Congress, and Back Again" is an interesting memoir from the state's first female member of Congress. Written with her daughter, Patricia Cafferata, Vucanovich's take on politics will please conservatives and even tickle liberal firebrand columnist Andrew "Barbwire" Barbano, a Vucanovich critic who takes a licking from the "tough grandmother" of Northern Nevada.

Expect a response from the always-entertaining Barbano shortly.

NAME GAME: With attorneys for Lance Malone and Dario Herrera heating up the preliminary phase of the Galardi-gate political corruption case, expect a long list of other names to surface in connection with the federal investigation. Although unindicted, their roles in the investigation are sure to make people think twice before doing business with them in the future.

ON THE BOULEVARD: Building giant KB Homes has stepped up in a big way to assist the annual Salvation Army Toy Drive. KB is collecting toys at its 40 local developments and has kicked in $10,000 in cash to make sure Christmas comes for thousands of local kids. ... Mobile Billboards of Las Vegas has signed a promotional deal with comedian Carrot Top. Question is, will the redheaded wild man distract drivers or cause mass road rage?
