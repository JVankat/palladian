  • Labor productivity in manufacturing continues to rise along with job increase (LABOR TODAY 729)
DATE: 2010-10-08 VIEW: 158
CATEGORY: ORIGIN: KOILAF
DOWNLOAD: LABOR729.pdf LABOR729.doc labor729(kor).hwp
13.6% up in the 2nd quarter year on year boosted by increasing domestic consumption and export

Amid the increasing expectation to the full economic recovery, it shows that the upward trend of labor productivity is on the track. In particular, the manufacturing industry and small & medium sized enterprises(SMEs) have been leading the virtuous circle of the productivity improvement and job increase. Along with this positive trend, nominal wage hike and collective agreement wage hike rates have marked a meaningful surge in the same period year on year.

According to the figures released by the Ministry of Knowledge Economy on Oct 7, the index of labor productivity in the manufacturing in the 2nd quarter this year marked 139.3, an increase of 13.6% in the same quarter year on year. The upward trend of labor productivity has been going on with the double-digit increase in the 4th quarter(17.3%) last year and the 1st quarter(19.5%) this year. The service sector also, albeit much weaker, improved its index reaching 113.8, up by 2.6% in the same period year on year.

It is analyzed that the manufacturing industry's labor productivity increase was boosted largely by the expansion of domestic demands and exports. The labor force input also increased 5.6% in the 2nd quarter in the same period year on year. Of significance, the numbers of labor force and work hours, which are two important variables to decide the labor force input, showed the increase of 5.1% and 0.5% respectively. This proves that new jobs were created, due to the expansion of production which was obtained by the increased input of labor force rather than longer work hours.

Another remarkable development appeared in the SMEs where the virtuous circle between productivity and employment is clearly visible. The labor productivity rate of the big corporations and SMEs in the 2nd quarter this year showed 17.5% and 12.9% respectively, where increase rate is higher in favor of the big corporations. However, the new labor force input in the SMEs increased 4.6%, which contributed higher to the job creation than 2.9% increase of the big corporations.

In the meantime, a coupling trend between labor productivity and wage increase is observed.

According to a survey result on 'wages and work hours' in the 2nd quarter released by the Ministry of Employment and Labor this year, the average monthly wage of the companies with 5 employees or more increased 6.2% compared to the same period of last year, which is a three consecutive quarterly increase. The survey also reveals that out of 7,816 work places which hire 100 employees or more, 2,689 companies reached the collective agreements on wages, whose average increase rate is 4.9%. This is 2.9% points increase, from 2.0% in the same period last year, which recovers to the similar wage hike level before the global financial crisis.
