Dispute Over Chrysler Sale Could Head to Supreme Court



Tweet

Fiat’s purchase of Chrysler isn’t finalized just yet.

A U.S. appeals court upheld the sale of Chrysler to a group including Fiat and the U.S. government today, but a group of Indiana pension funds is expected to appeal the sale to the U.S. top court, if necessary.

A lawyer for the group, Glenn Kurtz, told one of the three judges on a U.S. Court of Appeals panel he “would like to be successful in this court” but will take the dispute to the Supreme Court if the court doesn’t find in the favor of the pension funds, which represent about $42 million of Chrysler’s $6.9 billion in secured loans.

“There’s one more stop on this train,” said Tom Lauria, another lawyer for the Indiana pension funds to Reuters and other media after the decision.

Without a doubt, the court’s decision is being watched by General Motors, which is using a similar approach to its bankruptcy and quick sale.

If the sale is completed on Monday, Chrysler will have reorganized within the 60 days given by President Obama.

Source: Automotive News (Subscription required), Reuters
