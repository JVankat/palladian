Bonnie Byte: Make a Difference

Posted Monday October 18, 2010

Lo’ and Behold! There’s a National Holiday that I can really sink my teeth into and hang on! October 23rd is Make a Difference Day! It is an official holiday observed on the fourth Saturday in October. Oh how I howled with joy when I saw that many folks would be thinking about making their world a better place.

People have been brainstorming about all kind of activities that stem from a heart of service to improve and vitalize their communities. This day also functions as a way to raise awareness of our interconnectedness and our need for each other. Activities range from visiting shut-ins or serving in a program like Meals-on-Wheels to reading to friends or making refrigerator art to brighten the home of a new empty nester. Anything positive that contributes to another’s well being or joy can be considered a great way to celebrate.

Too often folks wait for a superhero or a facsimile of one - an empowered coach, a spiritual leader - to start the ball rolling. But this day encourages all of us to be a superhero, to take charge and make a change! No special costume needed, no mask or superpowers, all you need to be is willing to don a smile and arm yourself with a joyful, giving heart. Hey, now THAT’S power!

Come on, dear readers! Join the Celebration! And perhaps it will be so contagious that it will become part of all of us, part of what we put on each morning before we slip on our socks and shoes. Think of a world where when our feet hit the floor in the morning, one of our first thoughts is how can we make the world a better place, how can we make someone happy today, how can we show that we care for others!

So here I am, just plain ol’ Bon-Bon earnestly asking you all to turn October 23rd into a Make a Difference Day to remember. And I extend it into a challenge to keep the Love going all year. We all have it in us, all we have to do is awaken it. Once roused you may find it never going into sleep mode again!

Until next week,
Bonnie
