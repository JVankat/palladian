If you quoted lamp wattage is correct, then there must be a fault with the breaker.. No breaker should hold with that amount of current running through it..

The first thing I would check is that the wattage of your Par38's in fact 150 watt, although I think I have only seen 150watts (and maybe 120watts..) versions.

The best way to find out how much current your are using whilst the gig is in progress is with a clamp meter.. You can get AC or DC (or both) stand alone clamp meters although if you already have a decent multimeter, you can buy a clamp meter attachment. Basically you clamp the live wire, ideally near the breaker and it will show how much current is passing through the live. You can't clamp the L+N+Earth though. Only the live. I use clamp meters for testing the phase balance when using three phase (which I use quite a lot).

Good point about the strobes. They should only draw when they fire.

As to "bigger wire for smaller volts", thats because (as stated) amperage determines wire size. With low voltage wiring, wattage stays relatively constant. Voltage drops but amperage increases proportionaly.

cool, I assume this is a europe thing? in north Am. we use the ones alrealy mentioned. I have never seen or used anything above 250volt. Is this faily common in this area of the world ( I know your standard wall outlet is 240)?

It's a 240vac mains thing. 3 phase is 415 to 420 (ish) measured across any two of the three phases. Each phase to neutral measurement is anything from 220-250vac. This does vary though. I have seen over 260vac in some venues, which is bad for many generic or electronic fixtures.

The low voltage lighting you mention though, only requires suitably large cabling from the lamp to the transformer, which would naturally be quite close, due to voltage drop on low voltage cabling. The feed from the mains supply to the transformer would be rated by the wattage of the lamps.
