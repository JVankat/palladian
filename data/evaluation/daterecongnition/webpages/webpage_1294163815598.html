What is a volunteer?

An American Red Cross volunteer is an individual who, beyond the responsibilities of paid employment, freely and without expectation or receipt of compensation, and for personal, humanitarian or charitable reasons, contributes time and service to assist the American Red Cross in the accomplishment of its Mission - "a humanitarian organization led by volunteers, guided by its Congressional Charter and the Fundamental Principles of the International Red Cross Movement, will provide relief to victims of disasters and help people prevent, prepare for, and respond to emergencies."

Why volunteer?

The American Red Cross, a humanitarian organization led by volunteers and guided by its Congressional Charter and the Fundamental Principles of the International Red Cross Movement, will provide relief to victims of disasters and help people prevent, prepare for, and respond to emergencies. The very foundation of the American Red Cross is its volunteer work force. Today, more than 1.2 million people volunteer with the Red Cross nationwide, and the ratio of Red Cross volunteers to paid staff is 36 to 1. One in every 200 Americans is a Red Cross volunteer. Volunteering has long term benefits for both the volunteer and the community. Volunteers gain valuable work experience with an internationally known organization and maintain their proficiencies in specialized skills. The KMC community benefits in many ways. Volunteers help to enhance the services provided by many different agencies. Health and Safety volunteers train hundreds of adults and children every year in lifesaving skills, and Special Projects volunteers ensure the success of community events. For more information about volunteering in the KMC, Please look at our Volunteering section.

How do I volunteer?

Please contact your local American Red Cross office at Ramstein, Landstuhl or on Kleber Kaserne. We will provide information about scheduled volunteer orientations, and both long and short term volunteer opportunities. KMC volunteers are supported by one of the three offices depending on the volunteer activity. The Ramstein office administers volunteers placed on Air Force installations. The office on Kleber Kaserne oversees volunteers supporting the Army garrison. All Landstuhl Regional Medical Center volunteers report to the Landstuhl office.

To become a registered volunteer you must attend Red Cross Volunteer Orientation, complete a volunteer application, and watch a video about our services. These courses are offered twice a month and last a minimum of 2 hours. Orientations offered by the Kleber office are by appointment. Registered volunteers over the age of 18 are required to submit to our mandatory background check and must be cleared BEFORE volunteering. An agreed job description must be on file for each role. All volunteers are required to report volunteer hours each month. For more information, please look at our Contact Us section.

Where do I volunteer?

The KMC American Red Cross has volunteer opportunities throughout the area. We have specific Volunteer leadership and coordinator positions that will allow you to learn and practice human and program resource management skills. Our community volunteers work in medical settings, legal offices, schools, Post Offices and more. Health and Safety Volunteers teach CPR and First Aid Classes. Casework volunteers help deliver emergency messages to military members and their families. Join us and make a difference in your community.

How do I donate blood in Germany?

The Red Cross is not directly involved in collecting blood overseas. Blood collections in the KMC area are coordinated by the Armed Services Blood Bank Center - Europe at Landstuhl Regional Medical Center. In recognition of the vital importance of blood collection the Red Cross supports the Armed Services Blood Program (ASBP) by regularly sponsoring blood drives. Contact us to find out when the next Red Cross-sponsored drive is scheduled.

For more information about the ASBP and to check eligibility requirements visit www.militarylifeforce.com.

The Deutsches Rotes Kreuz (German Red Cross) also runs regular blutsplenden(blood drives). Visit www.drk-kl.de for information about upcoming blutsplende (blood drives).

What is the history of the Red Cross?

The American Red Cross was founded in 1881 by Clara Barton, a former schoolteacher who had helped care for the wounded during the Civil War. It is part of an international humanitarian movement that has its roots in 19th century war-torn Europe. For more information about the history of the American Red Cross, please look at the American Red Cross national site's history page.

Why does the American Red Cross serve the men and women of the Armed Forces?

The American Red Cross received its first congressional charter in June 1900 and was reincorporated by an act of Congress on January 5, 1905. Two purposes of the organization relate to activities with the armed forces. As specified by the charter, these purposes are (1) "to furnish volunteer aid to the sick and the wounded of the Armed Forces in time of war..." and (2) "to act in matters of voluntary relief and in accord with the military authorities as a medium of communication between the people of the United States of America and their Armed Forces."
