1.3 million Indian-British are the biggest luxury goods buyers and spenders in Britain, say surveys
Posted on Tuesday, September 13, 2005 (EST)
The survey reveals that ‘wedding gift lists’ are topped by one surname, which says a great deal about the new 21st century British society. The name is Patel – which means that ‘keeping up with the Patels’ could become the preferred mantra of British social life. By Vimla Patil
 
Print this page
Email this page

Mumbai: Sep 13, 2005/Sawf News Service/ -- UK’s 1.3 million cash rich Indians are the biggest buyers of luxury goods, says a recent survey done to check out Britain’s wedding gift preferences. The survey reveals that ‘wedding gift lists’ are topped by one surname, which says a great deal about the new 21st century British society. The name is Patel – which means that ‘keeping up with the Patels’ could become the preferred mantra of British social life. This revelation has brought to light a hitherto little known facet about British society – that a huge number of Indian-British people are millionaires or billionaires. Though famous rich people like 'curry king' Sir Ghulam Kader Noon – who won the Asian Of The Year Award some years ago –  are doubtful whether the results of the survey are accurate, the general agreement seen in the media, is that ‘the power list’ of Asians annually add the fabulous amount of Sterling Pound 103 billion to UK’s economy. Asians account for at least 10 per cent of Britain’s economic output at the present time. According to Lord Meghnad Desai, Asian professionals earn above average incomes and Indian Hindus are the richest whereas Bangladeshi Muslims the poorest in the Asian social graph. Whatever the truth, the survey of wedding gift buyers puts “Patels” at the top of luxury goods buyers in Britain!
