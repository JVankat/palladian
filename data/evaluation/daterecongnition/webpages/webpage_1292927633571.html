ESCONDIDO: Teen boy to face trial for rape

Teen girl says masked attacker had a gun

ESCONDIDO: Teen boy to face trial for rape

By TERI FIGUEROA - tfigueroa@nctimes.com North County Times - Californian | Posted: Wednesday, September 8, 2010 6:09 pm | Loading… | Print

A judge on Wednesday ordered a 17-year-old Escondido boy to face trial for allegedly raping and robbing a 16-year-old girl at gunpoint as she walked to school in July.

The teenage defendant, who is being tried as an adult, faces the potential of life in prison under the state's "one-strike" law for sex offenders.

The North County Times is not identifying the defendant because he is a juvenile. The alleged victim also is not being identified because of the nature of the crime.

The girl took the stand during a two-hour hearing in a Vista courtroom Wednesday and recounted the July 9 attack.

The teenager said she was headed to school, walking through a flood control channel off North Beech Street and East Valley Parkway at about 8 a.m. when she was accosted from behind by a man with a gun.

She said the masked assailant led her 80 yards from the channel to a spot behind a grocery store, where he pushed her to the dirt ground and got on top of her.

When Deputy District Attorney Patrick Espinoza asked her what happened next, the girl sighed heavily and paused for a long time. She eventually answered: "He violated me."

She said the attacker wore a mask that revealed only his eyes and mouth, and said he "was kissing me around my neck" during the rape.

As she testified, she kept her body turned away from the baby-faced teenage defendant seated across the room.

The accused rapist, dressed in an orange jail uniform, did not speak during the hearing.

After the rape, the assailant asked the victim where she lived. The victim said she lied about her address at first, then eventually she told him the truth when it was clear he did not believe her answer.

When the rapist fled, she said, he took her cash ---- $5 ---- and her iPod. The girl said she saw him take off his mask ---- giving her a look at his profile ---- as he was fleeing.

The victim said she got up from the ground, put her shorts on and ran to a nearby tire store for help.

The day after the attack, police released a sketch of the assailant.

Also taking the stand Wednesday was Escondido police Detective Juliette Barnes, who said the defendant was arrested the day the sketch was released. She said the teen had the iPod on him, but no gun has been found, she said.

The defendant's trial is tentatively set for October.

The state's one-strike law was passed by the Legislature in 1994 and expanded under voter-approved Jessica's Law in 2006. It allows child molesters and rapists to receive sentences of 15 years to life or 25 years to life if certain circumstances are present.

The one-strike law at issue in this case is not related to Chelsea's Law, which seeks to tighten penalties for sex offenders who sexually assault children.
