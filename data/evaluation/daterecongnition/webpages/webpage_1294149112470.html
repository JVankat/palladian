The TTL/ADI mode should work fine when you manually set the mode to [HSS] with the jogdial. You can do this when the TTL is blinking after using the Mode button. The LCD doesn't have an ADI display so the [HSS] is used as a substitute.
However, you DO need at least M5 SCA adapter to make it work correctly. If I remember correctly, there is supposed to be a button press combination that makes the flash display the SCA adapter version. I just can't remember what it is.

EDIT: According to some posts on dpr, the flash won't fire at all if the adapter isn't at least M5, so yours is probably updated to the latest version.

TTL with digital is a not as refined as with film. Due to the higher refectivity of the sensor as compared to film, they cannot do off the sensor reading of the light. As a result, most flashes actually fire a pre flash to measure the amount of light. Because of this, the subjects sometimes react to the first flash and the second flash catches them with there eyes partially closed.

Using the auto mode on the Metz eliminates this problem. HSS is supposed to work on the Metz.

I believe the Metz regarded as the best flash for the Minolta cameras, even considering the 5600.

The advantage of course, is if Sony changes the flash mount, you should be able to purchase an adapter and use your flash.

I got the Metz MZ54(i) with a SCA33302M5 adaptor with my 7D.

In order for this flash to work with P-TTL/ADI:
1) Set the Flash to TTL
2) Rotate the Jog/Dial until the display shows HSS

Known to cause over exposure in P-TTL mode
* Turning on the secondary reflector
* Mounting any kind of diffuser(softbox, stofen etc) or pulling out the build in wide angle diffuser
(* Shooting extremely close to your subject/combined with
very high ISOs)

You can check your SCA adaptor version when the flash unit is not mounted on the cam, by pressing both mode and select while turning the power on..

Good Luck

TTL and ADI do work on the Metz and my 7D.

Start in Program mode on camera and flash to make first test flash shots.

Make sure secondary flash on the Metz is switched off, to start of with.

If ADI is selected on camera and ADI is not possible the camera will automaticaly switch to TTL.

Make sure flash head is pointing straight ahead for ADI/TTL.

HSS is possible, do this outside in bright conditions to test shot.

I use my Metz mostly in Wireless mode, with very good results.

Always make sure the flash is mounted on camera when selecting camera & flash selections/settings.

The posting about setting flash to TTL + HSS and camera to ADI/TTL are correct.

Yes TTL on Metz + Camera ADI will give full blast of flash.

Flash on A + camera at ADI and P (full-auto program). To start with.

Its better to set the Metz to TTL/HSS = Metz TTL main menu, and HSS sub menu for the best ADI/TTL posabilities =

= You must set the Metz flash to TTL - and while its flashing 'TTL' turn the menu dial and within the sub menu select HSS, then ADI/TTL info works from the camera to the flash, why I dont know, thats how the Metz support tell you to set the Metz to get ADI on 7D, and it works for me and others.

Make sure the secondary flash is not switched on, the switch is on the side of the flash.

I assume you have the manual for the Metz and adapter (SCA 3302) , the SCA 'firm ware' needs an update to M5. My Metz worked OK without the update, but I had it done, its free, at least in the UK.

Try using the Metz in wireless mode (Remote). Of course the camera must be set to wireless and the flash must be on the camera to be able to set itself to Wireless - within the TTL mode, and sub menu will go to AD1, and 'SL' will show as 'slave'. Its in the manual.

You must use the Program full-auto mode on the camera to start off with or you will not know what is affecting the flash/camera exsposure. At least this is how I started out with the Metz, then used other modes as I went along.

Hopefuly your contacts are clean on camera and flash unit, and batteries are fully charged.

I hope this is of some help. Michael GE
