Former IT guru says sector's growth days are over

AFP , TOKYO

The former boss of Microsoft Japan has painted a pessimistic future for information technology (IT), declaring that the days of strong global growth for the sector are over.

"I resigned a year-and-a-half ago because I just got bored," Makoto Naruke, now president and chief executive of venture capital firm Inspire Corp told reporters at the Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan on Monday.

Long regarded as Bill Gates' right-hand man in Japan, Naruke, 47, decided to quit Microsoft Japan to set up Inspire.

He had been with Microsoft since 1981, when the company employed just 80 people worldwide, and only four of them in Japan, he recalled.

After nine years at the helm of the Japanese unit of the Seattle-based computer giant, Naruke said he wanted to strike out on his own and seek new challenges.

"I left the IT industry because I wanted to be involved in a new industry [which was] going up. The financial sector for me is the most growing industry," Naruke said.

"The financial sector in Japan is the most weak and most regulated -- somebody has to create new trends."

Whereas the need for restructuring in the Japanese financial sector offers a lot of scope for innovators and risk-takers, Naruke said the IT revolution had more or less run out of steam.

"In IT I see no future in terms of growth rate: some companies can win out through severe competition but it is only a battle for survival like in the auto industry or steel industry," he said.

Inspire has set up a range of investment funds primarily geared towards accepting investments from institutions. Its "Growth Strategic Fund," financed by capital from Sumitomo Bank, Yasuda Fire and Insurance Co Ltd, Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corp and 10 other companies, helps medium-sized listed companies which undergo modernization.

"When we accept investments, we ask the investors to help the company as industrial partners," Naruke said.

"But it is different from US venture capitalists: we don't send managers but we dispatch our investors to the companies."
