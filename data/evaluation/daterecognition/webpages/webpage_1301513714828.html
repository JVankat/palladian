Chicago man who threw puppy out of car window barred for a year from owning pets

By Associated Press

6:45 a.m. CDT, March 16, 2011

  • E-mail
  • Print

sns-ap-il--animalcruelty
WHEATON, Ill. (AP) — A Chicago man convicted of animal cruelty has been barred from owning a pet for a year.

Sergiu Muresan pleaded guilty Tuesday in DuPage County Circuit Court to misdemeanor animal cruelty. Judge Brian Diamond sentenced Muresan to one year of community supervision. While under supervision, the 26-year-old Muresan can't own a pet or have one in his possession.

Prosecutors say a witness saw Muresan throw a dog from a vehicle stopped in traffic in the Chicago suburb of Elmhurst on Dec. 11.

The witness rescued the uninjured 45-pound mixed breed dog and turned it over to employees of a nearby business.

Muresan turned himself in to police.

It isn't clear why Muresan threw the dog into traffic. The animal has been reunited with its owner.
