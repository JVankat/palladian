Baby boy loses legs and arm to meningitis as his identical twin brother escapes unscathed

By Daily Mail Reporter

Last updated at 11:58 AM on 16th October 2010

  • Comments (56)
  • Add to My Stories
  • Share

An identical twin baby boy has lost both his legs and an arm after contracting meningitis.

Healthy Richard and Jayden Hunter went to bed as usual one night, but within hours, Richard was fighting for his life.

Medical staff were forced to amputate the then 11-month-old's limbs in a desperate bid to save him.

His mother, Ashleigh Wilkinson has revealed her son's life or death battle with the killer disease to raise awareness of how quickly it strikes.  

Tragic: Richard Hunter recieves a kiss from his mother Ashleigh Wilkinson, shortly after having his legs and arm amputated

Now 15-months-old, Richard has had to undergo numerous operations as a result of contracting meningococcal septicaemia in June, an extremely severe form of meningitis.  

He was rushed to Newcastle General Hospital in the early hours of June 16 as a tell-tale rash began to spread over his body and Ms Wilkinson,18, was told to brace herself for the worst.  

She said: 'He was eating cake and was being his normal little self before we put him to bed.' 

The young mother from Newcastle had already been through an ordeal as Richard and Jayden were born three months premature and had been in and out of hospital  with numerous chest infections.  

She said: 'He was completely unrecognisable, he looked like he was black and blue because of the bad blood running through him. 

Brotherly love: Richard (right) with his twin brother Jayden before the killer disease struck

'The first 48 hours were critical for him and I stayed with him all night.'  

Ms Wilkinson, who comes from a family of seven, lives with her mother Dawn Geul,  49, a grandmother-of-five who discovered Richard's rash.  

She said: 'I woke up because he used to cry a lot through the night and he hadn't that night.  

'I looked at him and saw loads of spots and I just started running around the house shouting.  

'I could not stay calm at all. We were just trying to get him to stay awake.

'We thought he was teething so we had given him teething powder to help him before putting him down to bed.  

Brave: Baby Richard hopes to be home in time for Christmas, after spending his first birthday in hospital

'It's a good thing I woke up when I did. I was hysterical, it was just so quick.  

'If it had been during the day we would have noticed him getting worse and taken him to hospital straight way,' Ms Geul added.  

The family feared Jayden would also be struck down and he was kept in hospital for 48-hours but was given the all clear after the whole family had received precautionary treatment.  

'We got a message from the hospital the next day, which was the Thursday, asking us to come in because things did not look good.   

'We had him and Jayden baptised and everybody just talked to him to help and we prayed. I have never prayed so much in my life,' said Ms Geul.

The family's prayers were answered as Richard's heart rate began to pick up late that evening as the family played his favourite music to him.

'We got through that day and then made it to the weekend.  

'They said if he survived he would probably need to get limbs amputated and I just told them to try anything to help him,' Ms Wilkinson said.  

Two weeks after his brush with death Richard underwent procedures to amputate both legs, arm and the fingertips on his left hand.  

He is now undergoing extensive physiotherapy at Newcastle's Royal Victoria Infirmary where he has been since his first birthday in July.  

It is hoped the therapy will help him learn how to hold his head on his own and will teach him how to move.

Jayden can only visit his twin on rare occasions to stop the risk of infection but Richard is never far from his thoughts as the family frequently catch him kissing his treasured picture of his brother.  

'With Ashleigh being so young she has coped with it all very well, but he is going to need a lot of help when he comes out of hospital,' added Ms Geul.

Ms Wilkinson, who spends every other night at Richard's hospital bedside and the other at home with Jayden, is hoping to welcome him home before Christmas as he spent last Christmas in hospital with a chest infection.  

Now the family want to start raising awareness of the symptoms of meningitis so other families will not have to face the same trauma.  

Ms Wilkinson, added: 'We just want to let people know what happened to us so it  might not happen to them.  

'If your baby has a temperature or any symptoms, take them to the doctor.  

'If people can catch it early it might help save their child.'
