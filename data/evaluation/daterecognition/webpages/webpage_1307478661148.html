Data Warehousing

Inti provides Data Warehouse design, Data Modeling (logical and physical), for Operational Data Stores (ODSs) as well as Reporting Databases (Dimensional and Star Schema).

A data warehouse (DW) provides a central repository where all (most) of your business data is collected and stored and made available to business users and downstream consumer systems. In many cases, your DW contains master data - the single view of each business entity such as customer, security, organization, etc. Many operational systems do not intend to store historical data while the DW is specifically designed to store history.

Good DW design enables Business Intelligence tools to navigate the DW easily and quickly, providing dashboards, drill down capabilities, and understandable ad-hoc reporting.

A successful DW is supported by a Data Quality program, consistent and timely data flow into the DW as well as continuous improvement (i.e. addition of new business data and reclassification of data for current trends)

Data Governance

Inti can establish or expand your Data Governance program, including forming the Data Council, creating data standards and procedures, as well as formulating the documentation and communication strategy.

Data Governance is usually manages by the Data Architects (on the IT side), Data Stewards (on the business side) as well as a management representative. The concept is often met with resistance because it is viewed as an administrative duty. When successful meetings involving data governance issues take place that is the time to start introducing the concept, as already taking place - the formalization is just for visibility and continued focus.

One important are for Data Governance is onboarding of new data. The review process, how the data is to be structured, the data standards and quality are all important - quite often, once data is created, there is reduced emphasis on revising or reclassifying it. This is especially important with automated systems creating new business entities, from source data in many systems.

Publishing decisions, standards, policies shows that the process is working - and contributes to transparency.

Data Quality

Inti can build or expand your Data Quality program, by establishing strategy, data quality rules, quality targets and recording metrics to measure progress, either with commercial tools or proprietary solutions.

Understanding your data quality starts with data profiling and a data quality assessment. These will reveal how where the data issues really are, as compared to the frequency of reported issues. Each data quality issue found may have a different impact level associated with it - knowing this will help with prioritization.

Raising data quality to a targeted level can be challenging and frustrating. A Business Analyst and Data Architect will investigate the nature of the data issues and articulate the business/technical processes causing the issues. A further challenge is revising the upstream processes to prevent/avoid recurrence of data quality issues. Daily data quality rules should be run and stored, by date, so that data quality improvement metrics can be measured and reported.

Ideally bad/suspect data can be avoided at the source - data entry- but if it cannot, it can be checked before it proceeds to the next system. Or, when a DW is present, data quality can be measured in the Staging environment (pre-DW).

Data Management

Inti can lead or assist with your Master Data Management (MDM) effort. The challenge is in uncovering all instances of the same reference data used in the firm, and avoiding new satellite stores of data populated by the MDM solution!

The concept of MDM is relatively straightforward - it is the execution that presents challenges. Without a doubt, a successful MDM project requires subject matter experts (SMEs) who understand the meaning of the source systems data. ETL processes to load, transform and extract the data have to be flexible yet stable and highly efficient. The representation of the data has to be clear and understandable to business users otherwise it simply will not be used.

Matching of keys among supplier systems has to be robust to prevent duplicates and mismatches. Merging of data among multiple sources has to be pre-determined (i.e. layered, prioritized, rules based).

Ideally MDM requires active monitoring and continuous improvement so that is stays current and synchronized with the needs of the business users.

Data Integration

Inti brings extensive experience with integrating systems and data. Inti's approach involves meta-data driven solutions that are adaptable and sustainable over the long term.

The Data Integration Strategy is often the most important aspect of a Data Management program. In many instances, the chosen tool will greatly influence initial completion time, future support costs and the ability to quickly upgrade/adjust interfaces.

Consider an environment with hundreds of interfaces - chances are that one or more interfaces are undergoing creation, decommissioning, upgrade or adjustment at any given time. It is advisable to strive for meta data driven interfaces with a minimal application layer so that changes can applied easily and swiftly.

Sometime the ETL component is related to the "coding" portion of the project when in fact, it can benefit from a data driven solution, resulting in flexibility. Inti has been fortunate to recommend and apply data driven approaches to data integration, utilizing the data warehouse as it's rules/transformations library.

Vendor Analyses

When a firm is comparing data management solutions from vendors, a template of criteria can be utilized, where each criteria has weightings and each vendor has scores for each criteria. Ideally the weights and scores should be numerical so that a calculated score can be produced. It is important, at the outset, to determine how close the ratings have to be when the differences are considering insignificant.
