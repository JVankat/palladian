Hali Worthington talks with her coach, Mark Holanchock, before the final fight that will determine the gold at the Tae Kwon Do Junior Olympics in Orlando on July 8.

Area youth takes silver medal

By DIANE RODGERS

Assignment Editor

When Hali Worthington was in kindergarten, she knew she wanted to train in the martial arts.

By second grade, she had narrowed her specialty down to Tae Kwon Do, and had successfully bugged her parents into letting her take lessons.

On July 8, the 9-year-old St. Augustine resident took the silver medal for sparring in the Tae Kwon Do Junior Olympics, held in Orlando.

??I had always had a goal to go to Tae Kwon Do,?? Hali said.

??She pretty much knows what?s she?s wanted,?? said Donna Worthington, her mother.

Hali had competed in five tournaments before and thought it would be interesting to try something other than her normal competitions. Hali said she will use her experience with this Junior Olympics to prepare for next year?s event.

??It was fun and educational,?? she said. ??If I do get a chance to go to the next Junior Olympics ... this Junior Olympics was a great experience.??

That experience included learning other styles from other parts of the country.

??They all knew different moves,?? she said of the forms competition.

But sparring, or fighting, wasn?t much different, and that?s where Hali excelled. In her first fight, she won by three points. Points are garnered by striking the chest or head. She said her round kicks got her the most points. It?s also her favorite kick.

??It?s sneaky and when they don?t even know it you can spin into a spin back kick,?? she said.

That win put her into the finals. And some tougher competition.

Before the match that would determine gold and silver, she got some one-on-one instruction from her coach, Mark Holanchock.

He reminded her that her goal was the gold.

??That is what we came for,?? he told her. ??You?re going to use some indomitable spirit.??

Hali came close to achieving that goal. She lost by one point and took the silver, but she is proud of the medal.

??I worked my hardest and I got a lot of compliments on my work,?? she said.

??We?re just extremely, extremely proud of her,?? said Donna Worthington. ??This was the granddaddy for us.??

Donna said she is pleased that Hali chose Tae Kwon Do as a sport. Not just for self-defense, but for discipline, poise and the way it has affected her thinking process.

When Hali makes a decision, she has control and weighs out her options, Donna said.

There were about 8,000 competitors at the Junior Olympics, 800 of which were in the 8- and 9-year-old group.

Other St. Augustine residents who competed include: Chris Taylor, Chris Hogan, Adam Chapman, Paul Bradley, Jacob Bradley and Josh Forest.
