SPORTS: Is Kevin Durant Better Than LeBron James?

  • April 27, 2010 10:06:06
  • Read all 11 opinions
  • +4 Rave
  • share this Share
Looking back on it, it seems crazy that there was any debate about the premier player in the 2007 NBA Draft. In the months leading up to that draft, fans, scouts and general managers were going back and forth every hour on the hour, arguing the merits of Ohio State center Greg Oden in contrast to Texas forward Kevin Durant.

Well, that debate has been settled. While Oden missed his entire rookie season with the Portland Trail Blazers and almost all of this past season with various injuries, Durant has blown up. In his third season, Durant won the NBA scoring title by averaging 30.1 points per game, beating out NBA royalty such as LeBron James, Kobe Bryant, Carmelo Anthony and Dwyane Wade.




He also helped lead an Oklahoma City Thunder team whose core is barely older than the Duke team that just won the NCAA championship to a 50-win season and a berth in the playoffs. Now they are giving the defending NBA champion Los Angeles Lakers and Bryant all they can handle in the first round, as the Thunder and Lakers are tied 2-2 going into tonight's Game 5 at Staples Center.

I think at this point its fair to say that Durant and the team formerly known as the Seattle Supersonics won the Oden-Durant debate in a first-round TKO. While Oden is young and still could avoid a Sam Bowie-like fate, he is never going to get the star power that Durant already has. And to be quite frank, it's only the beginning for the 21-year old Durant.

So with Oden in his dust, Durant needs a new opponent for water-cooler talks (I don't think people actually talk at the water cooler anymore, but that's a blog for another day). With that said, I figure that Durant fans should aim high and gun for King James.

That may seem presumptuous, as LeBron hasn't fully even taken the throne of "World's Best Basketball Player" from Kobe. While the 31-year-old Bryant is heading toward the downside of his career - as hard as it is to fathom that a guy that's fresh off his fourth NBA championship and who averaged 27 points per game this season could possibly be backsliding - he still has a chance at a couple more rings. But you have to think that 14 regular seasons plus six postseason runs that have taken the Lakers to the NBA Finals are bound to take their toll on Kobe sooner rather than later.

Presuming that the Black Mamba isn't Superman - which could be quite a loose presumption considering his brilliance - the NBA is going to be LeBron's league. But as with any other organization, competition makes for a better, more exciting product. And I think that Durant will take Kobe's place as LeBron's foil a lot quicker than one would think.

Durant has a game that makes a basketball purist drool. At 6-foot-9, Durant shoots nearly 37 percent from 3-point range, which forces defenders to have to pay attention to him from 25 feet in. That allows him to drive and create off the dribble. He's impossible for power forwards to guard because of his quickness and slippery low-post moves and a nightmare for small forwards because of his height and length.

Unlike the LeBron-Kobe debate, which involved two different players with two different overall skill sets playing two different positions, LeBron-Durant is compelling because it involves two players playing similar positions with different methods. James, at 6-foot-8 and a listed 250 pounds (probably closer to 270) is a scoring Magic Johnson with elite athleticism and strength, as he can use his football-like stature to bull through his defender and get anywhere on the court he wants.

Durant is a lot more of a finesse player, relying on crafty low-post moves, a deadly jump shot and limitless energy to free himself for open looks. And he can rebound as well, outrebounding LeBron by .3 rebounds per game (7.6 to 7.3) this past season. It's hard to really describe his game - almost a combo of Reggie Miller and Kevin McHale with Michael Jordan's athletic ability.

Accompanied by a roster that will mature and become deadly with and one of the best young general managers in the game in Sam Presti, Durant will be making long NBA playoff runs in the near future. So Kobe and LeBron had better watch out, there's a new star coming and he'll be here pretty quickly - maybe before he gets a catchy nickname.
