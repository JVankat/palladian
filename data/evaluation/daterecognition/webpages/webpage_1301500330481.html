William Hill to launch £250m high yield bond

William Hill was under pressure amid chatter it is about to sell high yield bonds to help refinance its existing £1bn debt pile.

By Ben Harrington 8:56PM GMT 05 Nov 2009

Comments

Traders said the embattled bookmaker is looking to raise £250m by issuing a seven-year bond yielding between 7.3pc and 8pc.

Bankers from Barclays Capital, Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS) and Lloyds Banking Group are said to have been mandated to carry out the fund-raising.

It is not clear whether the bonds will be classed as “investment grade” or “junk” by the rating agencies. One market participant believed Standard & Poor’s will put a BBB+ rating on the new bonds.

The gossip, though, is that investors are not keen on the bond offering.

The move to raise fresh cash comes after William Hill, led by Ralph Topping, carried out a £350m rights issue earlier in the year. Some observers questioned why the company needs to raise extra capital from the bond markets. William Hill slipped 2½ to 173.8p.

Elsewhere in the sector, Ladbrokes fell 2.8 to 123.7p as dealers began to speculate it may follow suit. However, people familiar with the company played down the likelihood of Ladbrokes tapping the bond markets for more funds so soon after its own rights issue.

Overall, better-than-expected US unemployment figures boosted sentiment and helped the blue-chip index recover morning losses. The FTSE 100 eventually closed up 17.75 points to 5125.64 and the FTSE 250 gained 27.65 points to 9020.40. The Dow Jones was up 165.88 points at 9968.02 in late trading.

Tim Hughes, head of sales trading at IG Index, said: “It was the strength on Wall Street that continued to drive the FTSE 100 higher during the afternoon, with the index hitting its best levels for the month so far.

“This should give traders confidence that the 5000 area has once again proved to be a line in the sand for markets on the downside.”

Rentokil Initial put on 3.2 to 112p, amid renewed chatter it is about to land a huge contract with Transport for London.

Next perked up 44p to £19.56

as analysts raised their profit forecasts following a positive trading update earlier in the week.

Caroline Gulliver, an analyst at Execution, said: “Next raised profit guidance [on Wednesday] for the third time this year on the back of good third-quarter sales growth.

“Management has indicated that it expects consensus profit-before-tax estimates to increase by approximately 7pc to £472m for 2010. We have taken a slightly more bullish stance and raised our profit-before-tax estimate by 8pc to £485m.”

Double-digit profit growth at MillerCoors boosted SABMiller, which put on 24p to £16.40.

UBS analysts also argued that SABMiller will be able to achieve more cost savings than the company highlighted at yesterday’s third-quarter results. “We believe there could be even further upside to the $700m [£422m] savings target,” said analyst Jason DeRise.

Oil companies continued to be in demand as the price of crude flirted with $80 a barrel. Royal Dutch Shell ticked up 23½ to £17.66 and BP rose 5.7 to 587.1p. Tullow Oil also moved 23 higher to £12.33 as UBS raised its price target to £13.56.

On the downside, Cable & Wireless fell 9.1 to 138.9p after it reduced its earnings guidance for the full year.

BHP Billiton slipped 5 to £17.00 as Morgan Stanley analysts argued that the mining giant should splash out on petroleum acquisitions. “Despite reports of M&A in potash, we think petroleum offers upside that could have an immediate impact, including the ability to sell into unconstrained markets,” said analyst Craig Campbell.

Banks were under pressure amid waning risk appetite. Lloyds Banking Group shed 3.3 to 82.9p and Royal Bank of Scotland fell 1.2 to 35.2p.

Among the second-liners, BBA Aviation gained 6.9 to 156.2p after Oliver Wynne-James, analyst at Panmure Gordon, placed a “buy” rating on the company and gave it a target price of 190p.

“US business jet activity started to decline in late-2007. Two years on and, following a 35pc contraction in the market, it now looks as if a long-awaited broader-based recovery has set in,” said Mr Wynne-James. The talk was that US funds were buying the stock yesterday.

ITV jumped to the top of the FTSE 250 leaderboard following its better-than-expected trading update. The shares climbed 4.2 to 48.8p.

However, Yell slipped 1.7 to 42.9p as Cazenove said the company could launch its £500m equity fund-raising at its interim results next Tuesday.

Bowleven slipped 1¼ to 88p despite support from RBS traders.
