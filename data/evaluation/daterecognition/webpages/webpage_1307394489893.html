Hanninen holds slender lead

Eurosport - Fri, 16 Jul 09:54:00 2010

Intercontinental Rally Challenge standings leader Juho Hanninen heads the field at Sata Rallye Acores after the first leg.

More Stories

  • Sata Rallye Acores: calendar & results
  • IRC standings

The Finn holds a 0.2-second advantage over Bruno Magalhaes and Andreas Mikkelsen after the opening three stages.

All three won one stage after the opening day proper of round seven of the IRC season.

Magalhaes took the first stage before Hanninen secured SS2 and Mikkelsen the third.

Reigning champion Kris Meeke of Britain described driving first on the road through the loose coating of surface gravel as "impossible".

"You could see the times of the guys running further back were much faster than those running up front and that was definitely the case for me," said Meeke, in sixth spot but well-placed for Friday.

"It was so easy to make a mistake because it was just so slippery."

Jan Kopecky is fourth overnight and will hope that his Skoda Motorsport mechanics will be able to plug a small hole in his Fabia S2000, which ingested clouds of dust during the opening stage, as well as repairing a broken clutch.

Bernardo Sousa, the current Portuguese championship leader, completed leg one in fifth overall and will therefore start first on Friday's second leg after the top five places are reversed.

Friday's leg consists of nine stages.
