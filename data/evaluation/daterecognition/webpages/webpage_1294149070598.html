DO NOT get an All-in-Wonder card as these will not not work with MythTV. I would highly suggest a PVR-250/150 or you could even go with the PVR-350 but thats for advanced users and another topic. Yes the PVR-150 has audio out

Submitted by d00dtv on September 22, 2005 - 12:41pm.

Yes the PVR-150 has audio out. It does not however have video out. The PVR-350 has that but it's a big PIA to set up. The video out would be up to your video card. I would suggest an Nvidia GeForce of some sort that has both S-Video and Composite. Here's a link for the 150 card that shows the inputs/outputs:
http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/customer-images/B0006FS2IQ/ref=cm_ciu_pdp_images_0/103-3103399-8271812?%5Fencoding=UTF8&s=electronics&index=0#gallery

You can see the 1/8" mini-din plug for the audio. You should get the audio cable for this but if not then Radio Shack has these for sure. And here is a link for a descent video Nvidia card.
http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16814139146

Of course I'm not sure how your gonna hook things up. You may want DVI, I don't know.

Well its not actually two vid

Submitted by d00dtv on September 22, 2005 - 1:54pm.

Well its not actually two video cards. Its one capture card and one video card. Thats pretty standard in any computer for the exception of the capture card. Yes the 350 is more expensive but I'm telling you, you will be very disappointed in the outcome if you went that route. Its not worth it IMHO. Yes a Geforce card will work. Just make sure it has either S-Video or Composite out. Whichever your TV accepts.

This isn't a real cheap hobby just depends on what your wanting to do.
