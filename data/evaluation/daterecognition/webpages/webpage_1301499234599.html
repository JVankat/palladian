Poston-McGee

Thursday, Jan 22 2009, 2:55 pm

Mrs. Jeff Poston
Holly McGee

Poston-McGee
Holly Elizabeth McGee and Jeffrey Michael Poston were married Saturday, Dec. 6, 2008, at Anthony Grove Baptist Church in Crouse.
The Rev. Dennis Bean officiated the 6 p.m. ceremony.
About the bride: Holly is the daughter of George W. McGee of Casar and Denise R. Hamrick of Lincolnton. She is a the granddaughter of the Rev. Earsal and Sue Robinson of Lincolnton and the late Pearl McGee of Lincolnton. She is a 1996 graduate of Burns High School, a 2000 graduate of N.C. State University with a BS in zoology, a 2002 graduate of the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with a BS in nursing and a 2007 graduate of the University of North Carolina at Greensboro and the Wake Forest University Baptist Medical Center Nurse Anesthesia Program with a MS in nursing, specializing in nurse anesthesia. She is a CRNA at UNC Hospitals in Chapel Hill.
About the bridegroom: Jeff is the son of Don and Joyce Dye of Shelby. He is the grandson of Bill Conner of Blacksburg, S.C., Shirley Conner of Gastonia, Jack and Mary Farless of Shelby and Maude Poston of Shelby. He is a 1991 graduate of Crest High School and served in the US Navy on the USS Spadefish.
The ceremony: Music included a variety of the couple's favorite Christmas carols. During the ceremony, a pictorial interlude of the couple's 11 years of courtship was played to Alison Krauss' "When You Say Nothing At All." The bride was given in marriage by her family.
The bride wore: An Alfred Angelo white satin princess-line gown with pearl and crystal beading, featuring a lace-up corset back and semi-cathedral train. The strapless neckline featured a bodice band trimmed in claret with white embroidery that wrapped around to the corset and extended down the train inset. She carried a bouquet, made by her mother, featuring red and white poinsettias and holly, trimmed with pearl and silver accents.
The bride's attendants: Matron of honor was Terri Qualls of Burlington. Bridesmaids were Kelly Bivins of Lawndale, Angel Poston of Shelby, Emily Sain of Lincolnton and Amy Spizzo of Hickory. Flower girls were Kylee Spizzo and Reagan Spizzo, both of Hickory.
The bridegroom's attendants: The bridegroom chose Jeremy Poston of Shelby as best man. Groomsmen were Chad Conner of Casar and Jon Conner, Rodney Poston and Josh Turner, all of Shelby. Ring bearers were Triston Bivins and Brackston Bivins, both of Lawndale.
Other attendants: Jeanne Smith of Burlington attended the register. Miniature bride and bridegroom were Jaycee Bivins of Lawndale and Payton Sain of Lincolnton. Junior bridesmaids were Megan Poston of Shelby and Harley Sain of Lincolnton, and junior groomsmen were Anthony Poston of Shelby and Austin Sain of Crouse. Wedding director was Kristy Warlick.
The reception: Hosted by the bridal couple at Cherryville Country Club. Music was provided by Sounds Unlimited Entertainment. The Christmas-themed reception featured Christmas lights and candles, green and red table linens and chair sashes. Centerpieces were ornament-filled vases and Christmas trees. The wedding cake was topped with a red bow and the place settings featured a candy cane and cookie cutter wedding favor. Guests were served a soup bar, carving bar, potato bar and apple cider and a chocolate fountain.
The rehearsal dinner: Hosted by the bridegroom's parents and the special events committee of Anthony Grove Baptist Church at the church. The football-themed barbecue featured the Pittsburg Steelers colors of black and gold.
After the wedding: The couple took a trip to Adventura Spa Palace Resort in Riviera Maya, Mexico, and live in Graham.
