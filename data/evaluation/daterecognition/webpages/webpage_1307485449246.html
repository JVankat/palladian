Will be fifth consecutive bowl appearance for Scarlet Knights

For the first time in school history, Rutgers will appear in a bowl game for the fifth consecutive season as the Scarlet Knights officially accepted a bid to the St. Petersburg Bowl on December 19 versus Conference USA's University of Central Florida. Kickoff is set for 8 p.m. at Tropicana Field.

ESPN2 will televise the St. Petersburg Bowl in HD to a national audience. It marks Rutgers' seventh appearance on ESPN or ESPN2 in 2009.

The Scarlet Knights are just one of nine teams from a BCS conference to win a bowl game in each of the previous three seasons. In total, Rutgers is one of 30 teams nationally to play in five consecutive bowls, including one of 23 teams from a BCS conference to appear in five straight bowl games.

Rutgers and Central Florida will meet for the first time in the St. Petersburg Bowl. The game versus Central Florida marks the second Conference USA opponent Rutgers will face on the gridiron. Rutgers has also played C-USA member Tulane four times in school history.

Tickets and bowl information are available at RutgersBowl.com.

Greg Schiano on the St. Petersburg Bowl

Opening Statement:

"Thanks for coming out. We accepted a bid to go down to St. Petersburg and play in the bowl game and I am about ready to meet with the team in 15 minutes to inform them of a decision that is probably already out there and to tell them what the agenda is. It is a little different than in past years because it really amounts to about a bye week of preparation. We are going to be playing in no time flat. When the decision came, we have to change some things that are planned academically. There are some challenges with final exams and papers and things. Recruiting... we are finishing up a recruiting weekend. We actually have recruits here right now. We have coaches that are scheduled to go on the road this week so we are going to have to make some adjustments there. We are going to be playing a very good Central Florida football team. An 8-4 Central Florida football team that is, just looking at the stats having not seen them on tape, they are fourth against the run on defense. It is going to be quite a challenge for us. We certainly have to be really, really efficient with our time right now because we have a lot going between now and the 19th."

On if it is disappointing not having a lot of practice time:

"Yeah it is. Quite frankly it is very disappointing. I cherish that time with the under classmen to really bring them along. This date doesn't work so well for that. But on the same token, we are blessed to be going to a bowl game for the fifth straight year. I think people need to take a deep breath and realize that. There are a lot of football teams in the country that would like to go to five straight bowl games. Some would call it not at the level they expect, well I apologize for that but the reality is five straight bowls is five straight bowls and we are going to get to the top but this is part of the process. I remember going to our first bowl game and everybody was giddy. Don't take things for granted. I know I don't, I know I players don't."

On the impact of final exams:

"We finish exams the 23rd. You remember going to college. If we finish exams on the 23rd that means we start somewhere around a week earlier... the 16th and the game is the 19th so it is not going to be easy. The administration is going to work with us. We are going to ask our players to really buckle down. They don't know that yet, I am going to tell them that now. This is not the normal situation and we are going to make the best of it. We are going to play a football game against a very good football team and we are hopefully going to play our best football game of the year."

On whether bowls are a reward with tight time frames like this:

"It will be a reward. It is going to be a great location. Our guys are going to be treated very well. Sometimes things are a little harder than others, that's all. It is going to be a little harder this time."

On the academic schedule with the bowl:

"All the details have not been worked out yet. There has been some preliminary work in case. I didn't think that that is where it was going to go so I didn't spend a lot of time on it. I know our academic people have and our administration has. I have these recruits here tonight and I have my team here in about 15 minutes. I have to work all that and then in between I have to make sure we are all set up academically. We already have the video exchange going. Kevin MacConnell and Todd Greineder and those guys are heading down to get the logistics set up tomorrow morning first thing. It is ‘gotta go' and that's fine. We are going to go and get ready and get down to a great city and play a really good football team and see if we can get to nine wins, that's our goal."

On the hectic process of different bowl scenarios:

"It is kind of hectic. I think sometimes we all kid ourselves that we have more control than we do. You would like to think that you are really working to get it done. It is going to be what it is going to be. If we had won more games than there wouldn't be a situation where you have to worry. We didn't. Right now, we are who we are and I am not ashamed of who we are. We are growing and we are going to become great. But right now, we are not great. So, you know what, we have to deal with it and we will. We have to get ready to play a big time football game in St. Petersburg and that is what I am looking forward to."

On the schedule this week:

"I did not see this coming so to be frank I do not have a schedule yet. I will be up late tonight. I have all different bowl scenarios, this is one we are going to have to pull pieces from... some different preparations and figure out how we are going to do it. Our coaches right now are in between recruits working on scheduling what they were supposed to do this week, who we have to get in, game planning, getting ready for an opponent. I have a binder on my desk with all the information but I haven't been able to get to it because I have been meeting with recruits and their families all day. It is a juggling act."

On if you have had a turnaround like this for a bowl before:

"No, never."

On what changed in terms of bowl scenarios since yesterday:

"I didn't say it changed. I said I didn't anticipate it going the way it went. It didn't go the way I anticipated. I didn't make the right anticipation, I guess."

On an opportunity to lobby for a bowl game:

"There is always an opportunity. We have the right to say we are not going. I am not ready to do that. I would never think of doing that unless the administration said you can't go. This is a bowl game. We are excited to go to a bowl game. I am excited."

On how the league works for bowl game placement:

"Let them comment on that. That is the inner workings of the Big East Conference. We are part of the Big East Conference. I think you let them explain that. I know where we got invited and Tim [Pernetti] and I discussed it and we accepted the invitation. That is what we do, we make a decision, do you accept or not. Tim is on the phone and today I was on the phone, but in the end there is a lot of moving parts and the puzzles have to fit together just right and sometimes you are not the guy moving the pieces. You live with what you get."

On the number of practices you are allowed:

"There is no rule on how many you could have."

On the possibility of practicing after the bowl game:

"You trying getting young people to try and practice after the bowl. I don't know if it has been done in football. I can't comment on that."

On how your recruiting weekend is going:

"It is going well. I can't obviously comment on particular recruits but we have a great group here and we are enjoying the time with them and their families. Quite frankly we are not a school that does recruiting weekends on game weekends so there are a lot of new experiences I am going through. I am 43 and you think you have seen a lot and you haven't seen it. This is a good growth experience for me. Unfortunately the game didn't go the way we wanted, the way it ended. You have to get up and get going. That is what we did. We have great energy with these recruits and our players. I am excited about where we are going with this bowl game. A lot of our Florida kids get to go back home. I am sure it will be a great opportunity for their families to get on up to the games. We are really looking forward to taking our team to its fifth straight bowl game and all the things that go with it and to a great city. We have a great group of kids. I think they value this. They don't take this stuff for granted. I am going to go up there now and see them and give them the abbreviated plan. Quite frankly it is hard to give them what you don't know. I can tell them they better get cracking on their books tonight because it is coming down faster, maybe faster than they had thought, academically. Again, I am excited and it will be really a challenge, the opponent we play. I can't wait to get going."
