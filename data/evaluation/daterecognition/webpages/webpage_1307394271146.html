Women's shelter increases capacity

CTV Calgary News

Updated: Tue Oct. 26 2010 09:55:30

ctvcalgary.ca

Increasing demand has forced the Calgary Women's Emergency Shelter to add more beds.

Ten more beds have been added, to bring the total to 50 beds.

It was part of a renovation at the shelter.

"We've had to be innovative to meet the increasing demand for secure accommodation and the wide range of support services we provide for women and children in crisis," says Executive Director Lisa Falkowsky.

"We know 62 per cent of Calgarians have experienced or know someone who has experienced family violence, making this issue the most prevalent social issue facing our community today. We will continue to work hard to support women and children, youth and men on their journeys towards lives free from abuse."

The new bedroom configurations and upgraded counselling and common living spaces better meet the needs of women with three or more children who often have a difficult time finding suitable crisis support.

Calgary Women's Emergency Shelter supports women and children, youth and men experiencing family violence or using abusive behaviours.
