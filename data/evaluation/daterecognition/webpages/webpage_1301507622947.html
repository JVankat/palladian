Huffington Post going local, coming to Denver

By Cory Casciato, Mon., Jun. 22 2009 @ 12:51PM
Categories: Media, Tech

Arianna has her eyes on Denver.

The latest trend on the Internet is local, and new media juggernaut The Huffington Post is wasting no time leading the charge into desirable local markets. Not too long ago, the site launched its first local foray with the Chicago edition of the Huffington Post (around the same time that ESPN launched its Chicago sports site). Today, the New York version launched and, in a video interview with All Things Digital, Huffington Post founder Ariana Huffington revealed her next target is none other than our beloved Denver.

So what do you think, Denver? Exciting new development on the new media frontier, or a total waste of bandwidth?

  • Brad Jones

    If the Denver version emulates the strategy for HuffPo and its Chicago version, the question will be: what will their site offer news consumers that isn't already available elsewhere? HuffPo is an interesting concept but its main business - news aggregation - is highly dependent on other news outlets. How hard is it to surf around to about a dozen highly-relevant Denver news sites and link to their content? Face The State does some news aggregation - but it's state-wide, and not a core product offering. Who will blog for Arianna's new site, and what will they write about? There will be plenty of out-of-work journalists willing to pen opinion for the Denver micro-site, but if history is any indication they'll be encouraged to toe the liberal line. That's easy to do against a corrupt political establishment like in Chicago, but it's harder when the highest-profile men in Denver are media darling John Hickenlooper and the uber-vulnerable Bill Ritter. Why would the left spend their resources targeting either of them? There's also the matter of cannibalizing your core audience: politically-savvy activists and insiders. The left already has an action tank (ProgressNow), a news shop (Colorado Independent) and couldn't justify the continued existence of Colorado Media Matters. How does HuffPo fit in, and how do they grow the audience? HuffPo recently announced the creation of a spin-off non-profit to pursue investigative journalism, but that kind of work doesn't appear to be at the core of the site that will appear in Denver. More talking heads and blogger-pundits are always welcome (many times I'm one of them) but we also need a steady stream of original reporting to feed the opinion leaders. With the death of the Rocky, I'd put my money on reporting - not opinion. And there is a question of money. At the recent Investigative Reporters and Editors conference in Baltimore, a HuffPo non-profit exec declined to elaborate on the corporate entity's budget, but indicated the company wasn't exactly wildly profitable. How will Huffington's company make money in Denver when the national site hasn't yet been able to reliably produce? I'd love to meet the folks behind this new endeavor and ask them some of these questions - drinks are on me, guys. Brad
