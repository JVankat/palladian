EXPERIENCE AND EXCELLENCE IN SOCIAL RESEARCH 

CURS has over 18 years of experience in conducting social research in Romania. In what follows, we briefly present some of the most recent surveys on large samples, exit polls, and impact evaluation studies. 

 

 

1. Surveys on Large Samples and Public Opinion Polls

 

CURS has a long experience with excellent results in conducting studies on large and very large, nationwide samples. The latter type of sample ranges from 2,000 to 37,000 cases. The studies presented below were conducted on random samples, which are representative at national and regional level. In all of the studies/polls discussed in this subsection, the interviews were conducted face-to-face (in-person, in-home interviews).

 

 

“EUREQUAL 2007: Social Inequality and Why it Matters for the Economic and Democratic Development of Europe and its Citizens: Post-Communist Central and Eastern Europe in Comparative Perspective.” This comparative study was conducted in 13 East European states; it was funded by the European Commission and coordinated by the University of Oxford. CURS conducted the fieldwork for EUREQUAL in Romania at the request of Median Research Center. Specifically, within this project CURS conducted a survey on a nationally representative sample of 1492 respondents, aged 15 years and older.

 

 

“The European Social Survey – Round 3 in Romania” 2006 conducted in cooperation with the National School for Political and Administrative Studies (Bucharest, Romania). The ESS is an academically-driven social survey designed to chart and explain the interaction between Europe's changing institutions and the attitudes, beliefs and behavior patterns of its diverse populations. Now moving into its fourth round, the survey covers over 30 nations and employs the most rigorous methodologies. The fieldwork has been funded through the European Commission’s fifth and sixth Framework Programme, the European Science Foundation and national funding bodies in each country. In 2005 the ESS was awarded Europe’s top annual science award, the Descartes Prize. The ESS was conducted by CURS for the first time in Romania in 2006 (Round 3) on a nationally representative (random) sample of 2,139 respondents aged 15 years and older.

 

“The Cultural Consumption Barometers (2005 and 2007)”; these two survey were conducted by CURS for the Center for Studies and Researches in the Field of Culture (of the Romanian Ministry of Culture). Inspired by Pierre Bourdieu's approach to cultural preferences, consumption, and social class, these surveys conducted on nationally representative samples of 1636 respondents and 1605, respectively, aged 15 years and older.

  

“The New Europe Barometer”. In 2001 and 2004, CURS conducted two nationwide surveys on two samples of 1100 adult respondents each, within the New Europe Barometer Program (NEB). The fall of the Berlin Wall created great uncertainties and opportunities across half of Europe. The Centre for the Study of Public Policy and the Paul Lazarsfeld Society, Vienna, cooperated in launching a major multi-national survey, the New Democracies Barometer (NDB), to monitor the response of people caught up in the transformation of their polity, economy, society and often state boundaries too.  Coordinated by Professor Richard Rose (currently at the University of Aberdeen’s Centre for the Study of Public Policy) NEB has been conducted in Bulgaria, Croatia, the Czech Republic, Estonia, Lithuania, Poland, Slovakia, Slovenia, Romania, Belarus, Russia, Ukraine, and Hungary. NEB has benefited from the generous support of national funding bodies, governmental agencies, and NGOs from Austria, France, Germany, Italy, Hungary, Sweden, U.K., and the U.S.A. The World Bank and the Council of Europe have also supported this program. NDB focuses on respondents’ evaluations of the current political and economic system by comparison with the past--and future expectations of democratic or undemocratic governance. Health is also a special concern for NEB.

 

  

“Life Styles and Values in Contemporary Romania: Cultural Consumption Practices, Cultural Identities, Work Ethics, Health”. This study, which was commissioned by Intact Publishing House, aimed at describing and analyzing Romanians’ life-styles during transition. For this study, CURS conducted 4 nationwide surveys; each survey was carried out on a sample of 2000 adult respondents. The results of this study have been published in the Jurnalul National daily newspaper.

 

  

“Media Consumption among Romanian Teenagers” (July 2005). This study was commissioned by the National Audio-Visual Council (CNA) and was conducted on a nationwide sample of 4,600 respondents. Specifically, we interviewed 2,300 respondents aged 11 to 14 years and 2,300 respondents aged 15 to 18 years.

 

   

“The Analysis of Cultural Consumption of Audio-Visual Programming among Students aged 7 to 11 years” (November 2005). This study was conducted for the National Audio-Visual Council (CNA) on a nationally representative sample of 2,100 respondents aged 7 to 11 years.

 

  

“Mass Media’s Impact on Civic and Voting Behaviors” (2004). For this study, which was commissioned by the National Audio-Visual Council (CNA), we conducted a nationwide survey on a sample of 5,000 individuals, aged 15 years and older.

 

  

“Media Consumption and Socio-Political Issues” (June-July 2003) – a study conducted for the private TV station Antena 1 on a nationally representative sample of 35,000 adult respondents (18  years and over), with sub-samples of 800 respondents for each county.

 

  

In 2003, at the request of the National Audio-Visual Council (CNA), CURS conducted a survey on a sample of 5,989 respondents within the project “The Establishment of a National Service for the Measurement of TV Audience.” The survey resulted in the selection of a panel sample of households. The households in this sample were subsequently equipped with “people meters.” This panel sample is currently used by CNA to measure the audience of TV stations in Romania.

 

  

November-December 2003, “Survey on Socio-Political Issues” (in cooperation with the survey firm CSOP). This study was commissioned by the National Liberal Party (PNL) and the Democratic Party (PD) and it was conducted on a nationally representative sample of 35,000 adult respondents, with sub-samples of 800 respondents in each county.

 

  

“Survey on Social, Political, and Economic Themes” (November-December 1999) for the Romanian Association for Freedom and Development (ARLD). The total sample included 37,000 eligible voters, with sub-samples of 800 respondents in each county.

 

  

“The Public Opinion Barometer”. Between 1994 and 2003, CURS conducted 14 out of the 22 surveys within the Open Society (Soros) Foundation’s program “The Public Opinion Barometer”. At the request of Soros Foundation – Moldova, CURS also conducted a Public Opinion Barometer in the Republic of Moldova. Each survey in this program was conducted on a nationally representative sample of 1,100 adult respondents.

 

 

 

2. Exit Polls

2009 - Exit polls - Presidential Elections. CURS conducted two exit polls for TVR (the National Television Station) as follows: 1) an exit poll for the first round of presidential elections (the total sample included 18,540 voters); 2) an exit poll for the run-off presidential election (the total sample included 19,620 voters); 

2007 – Exit Poll: May 19 Referendum on Dismissing the President of Romania; the exit poll was conducted for the national/state television station TVR1 and for the private TV station Antena 1; the total sample included 12,000 voters.

 

2004 – General and Presidential Elections Exit Poll, November 2004. The exit poll was conducted for the private TV station Antena 1 on a sample of 17,000 voters.

 

2004 – Presidential Elections run-off Exit Poll, December 2004. The exit poll was conducted for the private TV station Antena 1 on a sample of 18,000 voters.

 

2004 – Bucharest Mayoral Election Exit Poll. The exit poll was conducted for the private TV station Antena 1 on a sample of 13,000 voters from Bucharest (the capital of Romania).

 

2000 – General and Presidential Elections Exit Poll. The exit poll was conducted for the private TV station ProTV on a sample of 16,000 voters.

 

3. Impact Evaluation Studies

“Mine Closure and Social Impact Mitigation: Social Impact Monitoring (SIM)”, 2001-2005. This World Bank financed evaluation study was conducted by CURS in association with the Hart Group (U.K.) and Vienna Survey Center (Austria) for the National Agency for Development and Implementation Reconstruction Programs for the Mining Regions. The social impact monitoring focused on collecting and analyzing socio-demographic and economic data regarding the changes resulting from the mining sector's restructuring and the formal closing uneconomic mines. The evaluation of the social impact was done through (a) repeated social surveys on the population of 14 mining areas in Romania; and (b) in-depth interviews with individuals (miners and their families) directly affected by the economic restructuring, local businesspersons who might provide employment opportunities to laid-off workers, and local authorities. We also conducted training activities for the personnel of the National Agency for Development and Implementation Reconstruction Programs for the Mining Regions. The goal of our training activities in the fields of economics and social research methodology was to teach the employees of this state agency how to measure, monitor, and evaluate the social and economic consequences of the mining sector’s restructuring.

1) Baseline Survey (2001). Specifically, the survey was conducted on two samples as follows: 1) the treatment sample – this sample included communes from the programme areas, which were supposed to receive financial support for infrastructure works (water supply and roads); 2) the control sample, which consisted of communes in the neighboring counties that were not included in the RDP. In each of the communes included in the two samples we conducted household-level interviews, interviews with public officials at the commune level, and interviews with informal leaders at the village level. The households included in the Baseline Survey formed the panel sample on which two sub-sequent studies were conducted (see below). In addition to carrying out surveys in the two groups of communes, CURS conducted focus-groups with citizens of target (or RDP) communes.

  

2) Mid-term Evaluation Study (2004-2005) was conducted by surveys in the treatment and control groups (RDP and non-RDP communes), using a panel sample. We also conducted focus-groups with the local authorities and citizens from the RDP communes.

  

3) Follow-up Survey 2006 aimed to evaluate the impact of the Rural Development Project in the communes included in this programme. The F-US was conducted in the households from RDP and non-RDP communes included in the panel sample. We also conducted focus-groups with RDP’s beneficiaries (local authorities and citizens in the RDP communes).

 

“Evaluation Study of the Employment and Training Incentive Scheme (ETIS) for Unemployed Workers and their Families from the Mining Areas in Romania” 2000-2004. This World Bank-financed evaluation study was conducted by CURS in association with the Hart Group (U.K.) and Vienna Survey Center (Austria) for the National Agency for Development and Implementation Reconstruction Programs for the Mining Regions. Our study evaluated the results of ETIS (e.g., how many laid-off miners found long-term employment through ETIS, the socio-psychological effects of ETIS, changes in the taxpaying culture etc.). For this impact evaluation study, we conducted surveys among laid-off miners that found employment through ETIS and surveys among laid-off miners that did not benefit from ETIS. We also conducted interviews with (a) representatives of firms that applied for and obtained ETIS funds to hire workers, (b) representatives of firms that initially agreed to cooperate with ETIS but subsequently rennouced their cooperation with ETIS, (c) interviews with representatives of firms that applied for ETIS funds to hire workers but did not sign a contract with ETIS and the National Agency for Development and Implementation Reconstruction Programs for the Mining Regions.
