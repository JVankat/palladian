Top Ten Games to play on the Wii (3rd & 2nd party games)

by Kevin on Apr.21, 2010, under Games to play on the Wii (3rd & 2nd party games)

There are many great Nintendo games for the Wii but the ones well known are Nintendo games such as Mario, Metroid, Kirby and Zelda, but what about games that aren’t well known but are worth a shot.  We present the Top Ten Games to play on the Wii (3rd party)

10. Endless Ocean

I want to be, under the sea, in an octopus’s garden…in the shade.  Now you can with Endless Ocean for the Nintendo Wii. Although this game lacks any kind of story, it’s the equivalent of having a fire place in your house. Stressed? Don’t smoke, but instead pop this little bugger in your Wii. Having the best fish graphics out there (humans look terrible) the game teaches you about the different types of fish. It’s not exciting by any means but it is relaxing very relaxing, so relaxing that I’m going to stop writing about it…….now

9. Trauma Center: New Blood

Dammit nerds I’m a writer not a doctor. Seriously I am, but you can feel like a doctor in Trauma Center. Grab that forceps with the A and B button and carve somebody open to defeat cancer, or in this case, little alien things. Either way this game is the closest to a doctor simulator with out it getting all too damn real. I’m glad they chose a nice cute anime style rather then a realistic art direction. Although this game is great on the Nintendo Ds it gets better on the Wii, holding up the Nunchuk and Wii Remote like a defibrillator and shocking the air in front of us while looking like a tool, never gets old.

8. A Boy and his Blob

This game is a must play on the Nintendo Wii, No Wii gimmicks here, but good ole puzzle side scrolling love. Again lack of a story line but the quality of the art is worth every penny. A Boy and his Blob is one of those games that you play and smile, because it’s so damn cute! You can make the boy hug his blob and they both smile with glee. This game has some amazing puzzles that get harder later and the amount of jelly beans you have to transform your blob is pretty outrageous as well. Where else can you play a game about a boy and his blob?
7. Deadly Creatures

I don’t get it; I really don’t understand why this game flew under the radar. You play as a Scorpion and a Spider, I mean, do I have to say any more? That’s bad ass, sure the camera sucks and sure the controls are a little awkward, but the overall presentation is unique. For a Wii title the creepy critters look identical to their real life counterpart. The in game story involving two guys (one voiced by Billy Bob Thornton mind you) is not really interesting but being able to dive that manly stinger into a rats head makes up for it.

6. The Conduit


Hearing about The Conduit before I owned either an Xbox 360 or a Playstation 3 it promised to have graphics that are up to par to both consoles……of course that is not true, but they did try (only with the blur effect , everything else is pretty dull.) Sure the game is nothing original meaning you have to stop aliens…yup.Although the game is not anything spectacular the multiplayer is. If you want a multiplayer that is online and is a first person game for the Wii this is on to pick up. Hell it’s the only game besides Animal Crossing that uses that awesome bull plop Wii Speak. It’s worth a play through at least once.

 

5. De blob 

Hey De Blob, how are you? Feeling blue? Feeling green? Feeling every single colour in the world!!!!!?? Well alright then. De Blob is one of the most interesting games on the Nintendo Wii; originally a Demo for Windows became a great long free roaming game for the Nintendo Wii, one that involves multiple colors to gouge your eyes out. You can soak up colours and transform the city from dull grey to rainbow road. The music really add to the beauty of the game with Jazz being for red, Blues being for Blues and wikkity wiki record scratches for purple. Play IT!!

4. Dead Space: Extraction

Well this is awkward, a prequel to an Xbox 360 game, how queer. Dead Space: Extraction was random but is really really good. This game is a First Person Rail Shooter, which I must say is the only great use for the Wii remotes (when it comes to pointing and what not).  Being a prequel and all you play as Sam Caldwell who does something. I’m not going to go over the whole story, but I can say that this game is scary; well it will make you jump. The graphics are very impressive and the wavy camera make you feel like your suffering from Vertigo, but it’s all part of the experience. A very very very bloody experience. 

3.House of the Dead: Overkill 

“Mother Fu..er!! That SH.T ain’t right!” is what Good ole Detective Isaac Washington  says when he sees a head rolling down the stairs and he’s right, Sh.t ain’ right. House of the Dead has always been a favorite arcade rail shooter of mine, and owning both HOTD: 2 & 3 was a treat, but not only did Sega release a game ONLY for the Nintendo Wii but they made one hell of tongue in cheek game, Well aware of how god awful the voice acting and story was in the past House of the Dead games Sega went the Grind house route instead and broke the Guinness World Record for the most times the word “Fu.k” was used in a video game, 189 times to be exact. Good job, besides that this game is hilarious throughout, Agent G and Isaac Washington play great off each other and it’s one of those rare times where you will actually care about the storyline in a House of the Dead game.

2.Mad World

Black and white sin city esque drawing? Check. Main protagonist named Jack? Check. Guy has a chainsaw for an arm? Hell yah! Mad World is a 13 year old boy little creepy dream (or at least it would be mine, if I was a 13…and a boy…) you play as Jack who does something for someone, who cares about the story line there’s blood! There’s also Wii Motions that WORK! The game like House of the Dead: Overkill is hilarious and just flat out crazy. The commentators add a lot of character to this hell hole of a city with the voices of John DiMaggio (Bender from Futurama) and Greg Proops ( um…Whose line is it Anyways?). They play off each other like their actually sitting down next to each other and watching the whole thing go down. Mad World is like every Mortal Kombat fatality without the fighting. It’s a fun free
roaming game with Tires, garbage cans and even subways being your tools of destruction. Sega has really tried new experiments on the Nintendo Wii and they have been M rated and more for the “hardcore” fans, but for some reason this game didn’t make that big of a dent, it really is a Mad World.

1. No More Heroes 

Now this is what I’m talking about.  A unique Wii titles (although as I write this it is rumoured to be re-released for the Playstation 3) that is actually good! You play as Travis Touchdown a guy with a really sweet name who kind of looks like Johnny Knoxville, you’re a Wrestling loving, kitty playing, anime crazy nerd…. You’ll feel right at home. Sure the game has some odd “duties” like cutting the grass and cleaning a window, but as weird as it is, you will actually have fun doing it. No More Heroes is exactly what I’m talking about when I want a game that is fun and colourful, it also uses the Wii controls properly, and the artistic style is outstanding, they didn’t try to go for realism but went with a well thought out cell shaded look. There are a lot of 8 bit nods as well, and the game is well aware at how cheesy it is which  is great, it does not take itself seriously. Not only is this game funny, but it is very fun to play through and there is blood…lots and lots of blood.  If you haven’t played any game on this list, you must at least play No More Heroes.
- Max
