Company Growth

The company began with early projects for refurbishment works for Southend Council and Waymade Health Care. Confidence in the company grew and new build was ventured into, a new factory for Waymade Health Care in Basildon was undertaken. From that experience a new bowling alley in Ashford was built for a private client, during which time their reputation grew and Southend Council appointed the company for larger projects including new build for schools. Now growing from strength to strength, they became accepted as an approved contractor for Essex Police Authority and subsequently were in urgent need of larger premises in Leigh. The company began to start their own development including the Victoria Business Park, Southend. Their reputation for reliability and quality continued to grow and they began to build houses and apartments for their existing private clients, including sixteen luxury apartments along the Esplanade, Southend; two luxury detached houses in Benfleet; industrial and warehouse units at Burnt Mills, Basildon; Essex Police Firing Range; Pitsea Police Station; Greenways Junior School, Southend; and the luxury & award winning project at The Leas, Southend.

The company's services include being the approved responsive maintenance contractor for Southend Borough Council, Essex Police and Essex County Fire Brigade. Current projects are diverse from eighteen apartments at Wier Pond Road, Rochford to a luxury mansion and swimming pool in South Benfleet. Current projects also include a ten storey development of forty-five luxury apartments along Southend Seafront - a contemporary modern design concept building using brick and natural stone, glass, and copper and stainless steel, and two new luxury detached five bedroom houses at Badgers Hall, Benfleet.

More recently the company have sympathetically renovated a derelict barn in Rawreth and it is here where their prestigious offices now can be found. The building both stunning inside and out is the outcome of the meticulous conversion of a dilapidated 19th century barn. Many features of the original building have been restored and retained together with the restoration of the original pond and ditch network.
