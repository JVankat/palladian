Fashion Merchandising Masters Degrees: Online and Campus-Based Program FAQs

Do you have a passion for fashion? Are you a creative thinker who loves color and design? Fashion merchandisers work to increase sales through the creative display and promotion of fashion items. If this sounds like fun, then a master's degree in fashion merchandising may be right for you. Read on to learn more about this degree.

What Courses Will I Take in a Fashion Merchandising Master's Degree Program?

You will study retailing and consumer behavior through intensive studio and academic courses. Fashion merchandising core courses will typically focus on marketing communications and market development, including direct response, social networking and word-of-mouth marketing strategies. You will also focus on image and branding concepts and visual displays. Business management courses may also make up part of the curriculum.

Some programs also include fashion design courses where you'll learn how to sketch a live model or select the colors and fabric for a fashion collection. You may also have to create and market your own brand, developing skills in market and trend analysis, promotion and fashion forecasting. Typical coursework includes the following:

  • Competitive strategy creation
  • Textile products
  • Consumer behavior
  • Fashion theory
  • Fashion retailing
  • Apparel design
  • Textile markets
  • Design conceptualization
  • Marketing and promotion
  • Merchandising principles

How Do Online Programs Work?

Online master's degree programs in fashion merchandising are available as specialties within a fashion, merchandising or human sciences program. Coursework is typically the same as on-campus programs and is delivered in multimedia presentations via the Internet. You will need reliable Internet access and a recommended Web browser.

Some online studio courses may require additional image editing or presentation software, such as Adobe Photoshop or PowerPoint. Hardware requirements may include digital cameras, color printers and scanners. As with on-campus programs, you will be responsible for creating a fashion merchandising thesis project prior to being granted a degree. Some programs may require you to present your finished project on campus.

What Can I Do With My Degree?

A career in fashion merchandising requires a blend of business expertise and fashion sense in order to reach and influence consumers. Master's degree program graduates may have a career as any one of the following:

  • Visual display manager
  • Product designer
  • Fashion product developer
  • Costumer
  • Textile evaluation specialist
