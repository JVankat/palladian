WestConn baseball team hammers Lehman

DANBURY -- A strong outing by starter Connor Nolan and a pair of home runs by junior slugger Zach Dandrea propelled the Western Connecticut State University baseball team to a lopsided 24-0 victory over the visitors from Lehman College at Westside Field on Wednesday evening.

Both teams went quietly in the first inning, and after another quiet inning for Lehman (5-20) in the second, junior Kyle Savo led off the WestConn half of the second with a triple over the head of the center fielder. Junior Cody Sestito followed with an RBI double to get the Colonials on the board. After back to back strike outs, freshman Jacob Szyndlar singled in Sestito from second and the Colonials had a 2-0 lead after two.

The Lehman bats remained silent in the third and fourth as Nolan continued to pitch well for the Colonials. In the bottom of the fourth, WestConn used seven singles and a base on balls to take command on the scoreboard. Sestito and freshman Xavier Ortiz got things going for the Colonials with back to back singles, and a failed pick-off attempt by Lehman pitcher Justin Moises moved the runners to second and third. Freshman Austin Howard singled in both runners, and the WestConn lead was 4-0. A walk and a bunt single by leadoff hitter Anthony Ruffo loaded the bases for WestConn (12-12), but a fielder's choice and three more singles pushed the lead to 9-0 before two pop-ups to short ended the inning.

The Colonials added five more runs in the bottom, behind five more singles, and error, and a walk, for a 14-0 lead.
