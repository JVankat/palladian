Senatorial candidate Jim Norman removed from ballot after judge's ruling

By Laurie Davison, Reporter
Last Updated:
More Info
  • Jim Norman
  • Judge's ruling against Norman
HILLSBOROUGH COUNTY -- 

On Friday, a judgeÂ ruled that Jim NormanÂ should be disqualified andÂ removed from the ballot for the upcoming election on Nov. 2.

Norman is currently a Hillsborough County Commissioner running for the State Senate District 12 seat, which is comprised of northern Hillsborough and much of Pasco counties.

Kevin Ambler ran against Norman in the primary election and lost by 12 percentage points. Following his loss, he filed a lawsuit against Norman stating that he wasn't forthcoming with his all of his assets.

In Florida, there is a law that requires candidates to allow the state full access to their financial reports.

Investigators determined Norman had declined to disclose two boats and a vacation home, which are listed in his wife's name.

During trial, Norman denied that he had any knowledge of his wife's spending and where or who she got the money from.

His wife told the court she used about $100,000 from a joint account between her and Norman and also received funds from the late Republican activist Ralph Hughes.

Following his wife's disclosure, Norman disclosed that he and Hughes were good friends, but still had no idea that his wife received any money from Hughes.

The FBI is now investigating Norman's purchase of the lakefront vacation home in Arkansas.

Norman's attorney, Frank Winkles, released a statement saying:

"We think the judge overstepped her authority and didn't have the jurisdiction to make this decision. We believe it's not a matter for the judicial branch to decide, but rather for the legislative branch to make a determination."

Winkles said they are planning to appeal the decision immediately and will ask for a quick decision since the election is justÂ a little more than two weeks away.

Ambler testified against Norman in court, sayingÂ Norman told him about the lakefront home before he purchased it.

"I think the judge's ruling goes a long way to restoring public trust in not just the judicial process, but in the electoral process," Ambler said, following the judge'sÂ ruling.Â "The thing is, I think that if the voters had had all the facts that were exposed during this trial available to them then, they most certainly would not have voted for Jim Norman."

Ambler could now replace Norman on the ballot, but it's up to the judge to ultimately make that decision.

There are no Democrats running for the Senate State District 12 seat There are two other write-in candidates, neither of whom have a highly-visible campaign.
