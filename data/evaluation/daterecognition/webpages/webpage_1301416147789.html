Weekly Film Listings for July 31

Posted By: Monday Magazine Staff
07/30/2008 12:00 AM

Please call theatres to confirm titles and times.

Opening

The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor - (Odeon/SilverCity/Caprice, 2:00, 7:00, 9:25) Brendan Fraser is back in mummy-bashing mode, in a sequel set in China that involves zillions of warriors back from the dead. Co-starring Jet Li and Michelle Yeoh. Starts Friday.

Swing Vote - (Odeon) Kevin Costner stars in a genial comedy about an easy-going slacker whose single vote will determine the outcome of the next U.S. election. Co-starring Dennis Hopper, Stanley Tucci and Nathan Lane. Starts Friday.

Brideshead Revisited - (Capitol, 12:30, 3:30, 6:30, 9:20) Evelyn Waugh’s revered novel about an aristocratic English family was evocatively set between the wars and explored themes of Catholicism, guilt, art and privilege. It was made into a landmark, 12-hour series in 1981, so this revisit has its work cut out for it. Starring Emma Thompson as the icy Lady Marchmain. Those upper lips start to stiffen this Friday.

Pineapple Express - (Capitol, 12:30, 3:50, 7:10, 9:40) Seth Rogen not only stars in this new stoner comedy, but he's co-written it with his buddy Judd Apatow (40-Year-Old Virgin, Superbad) and Evan Goldberg. Co-starring James Franco, Nora Dunn and Rosie Perez. Starts Wednesday.

Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2 - (Star, Wed., 2:00, 7:15/Capitol, Wed., 1:10, 4:00, 7:00, 9:30) Based on Ann Brashares’ best-selling series of novels, the four friends are back, but going there separate ways as they grow up and move on in life. Starts Wednesday.

Continuing

***  Before the Rains - (Odeon) The setting for this 1930s melodrama is southern India, which takes place during the declining years of the British raj. Personal ambition, culture clash and a forbidden interracial love affair add lots of spice to a well-acted and beautifully filmed movie, the first English language film from the East Indian director of The Terrorist.

*** The Dark Knight - (Capitol, 12:20, 1:00, 3:20, 4:10, 6:40, 8:00, 9:50/SilverCity/Uni 4/Caprice, 1:30, 6:30, 9:30) The dark death of Heath Ledger casts a shadow over the latest Batman tale. Although initially gripping, this is an A-list film trying way too hard to find thematic depth in comic-book material. With Christian Bale, Gary Oldman and Maggie Gyllenhaal.

Dinosaurs: Giants of Patagonia - (IMAX) Donald Sutherland narrates this speculative account of the life of a dinosaur in the Argentina of hundreds of millions of years ago.

**½ Get Smart - (Roxy, 7:15) One of the highlights of ’60s TV was the sitcom about bumbling spy Maxwell Smart. Steve Carell puts on the tux and the befuddled expression for this amiable, reasonably entertaining big-screen adaptation, co-starring Anne Hathaway, Alan Arkin and Terence Stamp.

**¾ Hancock - (Capitol, 1:10, 4:00, 7:10, 9:40 *Ends Tues./SilverCity) Will Smith stars in a quirky comedy about a dissolute, widely despised superhero who tries to reform himself and win back the respect of ordinary citizens.

**½ Hellboy II: The Golden Army - (Odeon) Go-to fantasy stylist Guillermo del Toro (Pan’s Labyrinth) directed and wrote this disappointing sequel, which has an overly-dense bunch of malarkey for a plot, and so many weird creatures that scrappy (and mouthy) underdog Hellboy (Ron Perlman) gets upstaged by an excess of latex and CGI.

***½ Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull - (Odeon) Everyone’s favourite whip-cracking archeologist-adventurer returns to the screen after a 19-year sabbatical. Welcome back!

** Journey to the Center of the Earth - (Capitol, 12:50, 3:50, 7:00, 9:30 *Ends Tues./SilverCity) The action may be in 3D but the characters sure aren’t, in a lame retelling of the Jules Verne classic about finding a lost world in the bowels of the earth.

** Mamma Mia! - (Star, Fri.-Tue., 7:15; Sat.-Sun., Tue., 2:00/Odeon/SilverCity/Uni 4) Everyone’s favourite ABBA-based musical comes to the screen, starring Meryl Streep, Colin Firth and Pierce Brosnan (who sings like a wounded water buffalo). Weirdly edited and cheesier than feta pie, this is the most fun you’ll have at a bad movie all year.

***½ Shine A Light - (IMAX) Martin Scorsese filmed the Rolling Bones over two nights in a small Manhattan theatre in 2006; the resulting concert film is an intimately ear-throbbing tribute that captures Mick, Keith et al. in top form—after an amazing 45 years of being the bad boys of rock ‘n’ roll.

Space Chimps - (Odeon) A trio of chimpanzee astronauts blasts off into outer space and much monkey business ensues. I’m betting boys will laugh until mucus roars out their nose.

*** Step Brothers - (Odeon/SilverCity/Caprice, 1:45, 7:10, 9:20) John C. Reilly and Will Ferrell star in a shamelessly lowbrow comedy about two woefully immature stay-at-home 40-year-olds who start to behave very badly when their single parents get married and merge households. Co-starring Richard Jenkins and Mary Steenburgen. See review.

****½ WALL-E - (Star, 7:00; Sat.-Sun., Tue.-Wed., 2:15/Capitol, 12:40, 3:40, 6:50, 9:10/SilverCity/Uni 4/Caprice) The newest Pixar smash stars a lonely trash-collecting robot left behind on a depopulated earth for 700 years. When a cute “search robot” named EVE lands on earth to check things out, WALL-E gets one look at her and his circuits start to smoke. Subtle, wildly imaginative and poignant, this masterwork is like intergalactic Kubrick for kids.

***½ Wild Ocean - (IMAX) Although a bit preachy, this gorgeously photographed account of South Africa’s massive herring migration—and the thousands of predators it attracts—is an awesome portrait of nature’s brutal magnificence.

** The X-Files: I Want to Believe - (Odeon/SilverCity/Uni 4) The truth is out there—someone abducted Scully and Mulder and made a crappy movie. Co-starring Billy Connolly and Amanda Peet. See review.

**¼ You Don’t Mess With The Zohan - (Roxy, 9:15) Adam Sandler stars as a ridiculously lethal Israeli counter-terrorism agent who moves to New York to become a hairstylist. But after he's spotted by a vengeful Arab, the humus hits the fan. This very silly movie has a heart and a droll premise—it’s a Borat for the hard-of-thinking—but too much of the comedy is a vulgar misfire.

Leaving

*** Wanted - (Odeon)

**½ Get Smart - (Capitol/SilverCity)

*** Kung Fu Panda - (Odeon)

Screenings

Reel Dance Film Fest - MediaNet presents this fourth annual event featuring new, experimental and eclectic works created by participants of this year’s Dance for the Camera workshop.7:30pm SATURDAY at Victoria Arts Connection, 2750 Quadra. $5 suggested donation. 381-4428.

Movie Monday - Showing B.C. Day. In honour of our 150th anniversary there will be a medley of amusing social-history docs. Up first are some government-made shorts from the ’40s, ’50s and ’60s that were aimed at tourists. Then there are some droll moments from the psychedelic era, including footage of Janis Joplin in Vancouver and an interview with redneck mayor Tom Campbell denouncing “hippie types.” Should be good! 6:30pm at Eric Martin Pavillion, 2326 Trent. By donation. 595-FLIC. moviemonday.ca

Cinecenta

UVic’s Cinecenta film society screens its films in the Student Union Building. Tickets are available 40 minutes prior to showtime. Info: 721-8365. cinecenta.com

Silent Light - (Wed.-Thurs., July 30-31: 7:15) This winner of the Cannes “jury prize” hails from Mexico and tells a poignant tale of love and faith set in a Mennonite community near Chihuahua.

**** Antonio Gaudi - (Fri.-Thurs., Aug. 1-7: 7:10, 8:45) After a week of sellouts they had to bring this one back! Spain’s legendary architect—most famous for the eccentric and sublime Sagrada Familia cathedral in Barcelona—is profiled in this essentially wordless documentary that is deeply in tune with its subject.
