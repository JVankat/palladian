St. Rose Heath Centre
Peka, Lesotho

St. Rose Health Centre was opened in 1935 by the Sisters of the Holy Names. Since that time it has given comprehensive health care to the people in Leribe area and its outskirts.



New expectant mothers, well-baby clinics and a maternity section where women come to have their babies delivered are some of the services the health centre provides. In one six month period 141 babies were delivered.

Since 1998 the number of patients, especially young mothers, who are dying of HIV/AIDS continues to increase. The number of orphans are gradually increasing as well. The staff of St. Rose health Centre feel a strong call to support our people during this HIV and AIDS epidemic. The staff is exploring ways of expanding the services and support offered to patients and families suffering from this disease.
