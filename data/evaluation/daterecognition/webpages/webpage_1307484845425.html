USA|Kansas Legislative Update (12.9.2008)
By Dodie Wellshear, Government Relations Consultant to USA|Kansas
Shake-up in Senate Committee Leadership

The Senate announced its new lineup of committee chairs, vice-chairs, and ranking Democrats for the 2009 Kansas legislative session. Among these are a number of notable changes, including Sen. Dwayne Umbarger (R-Thayer) moving from chairman of the powerful Ways and Means Committee, to chairman of the Transportation Committee. There will be increased focus on the Transportation Committee in 2009, as the legislature considers a third multi-billion-dollar, ten-year Comprehensive Transportation Plan.

Also notable is that all of the most conservative Republican members of the Senate have lost their chairmanships. Unlike four years ago, only moderate Republicans—with the exception of Sen. Les Donovan (R-Wichita)—received committee chairmanships.

Following is a list of Senate committee leadership for, presumably, the next four years:
  • Agriculture—Chair, Mark Taddiken (R-Clifton); Vice-Chair, Ralph Ostmeyer (R-Grinnell); Ranking Democrat, Janis Lee (D-Kensington)

  • Assessment & Taxation—Chair, Les Donovan (R-Wichita); Vice-Chairs, Derek Schmidt (R-Independence) and Julia Lynn (R-Olathe); Ranking Democrat, Tom Holland (D-Baldwin City)

  • Commerce—Chair, David Wysong (R-Shawnee Mission); Vice-Chair, Julia Lynn (R-Olathe); Ranking Democrat, Tom Holland (D-Baldwin City)

  • Education—Chair, Jean Schodorf (R-Wichita); Vice-Chair, John Vratil (R-Leawood); and Ranking Democrat, Anthony Hensley (D-Topeka)

  • Ethics & Elections—Chair, Vicki Schmidt (R-Topeka); Vice-Chair, Pat Apple (R-Louisburg); Ranking Democrat, Oletha Faust-Goudeau (D-Wichita)

  • Federal & State Affairs—Chair, Pete Brungardt (R-Salina); Vice-Chair, Roger Reitz (R-Manhattan); Ranking Democrat, Oletha Faust-Goudeau (D-Wichita)

  • Financial Institutions & Insurance—Chair, Ruth Teichman (R-Stafford); Vice-Chair, Karin Brownlee (R-Olathe); Ranking Democrat, Chris Steineger (D-Kansas City)

  • Judiciary—Chair, Tim Owens (R-Overland Park); Vice-Chair, Derek Schmidt (R-Independence); Ranking Democrat, David Haley (D-Kansas City)

  • Local Government—Chair, Roger Reitz (R-Manhattan); Vice-Chair, Susan Wagle (R-Wichita); Ranking Democrat, Kelly Kultala (D-Kansas City)

  • Natural Resources—Chair, Carolyn McGinn (R-Sedgwick); Vice-Chair, Ruth Teichman (R-Stafford); Ranking Democrat, Marci Francisco (D-Lawrence)

  • Public Health & Welfare—Chair, Jim Barnett (R-Emporia); Vice-Chair, Vicki Schmidt (R-Topeka); Ranking Democrat, David Haley (D-Kansas City)

  • Transportation—Chair, Dwayne Umbarger (R-Thayer); Vice-Chair, Bob Marshall (R-Ft. Scott); Ranking Democrat, Kelly Kultala (D-Kansas City)

  • Utilities—Chair, Pat Apple (R-Louisburg); Vice-Chair, Mike Petersen (R-Wichita); Ranking Democrat, Janis Lee (D-Kensington)

  • Ways & Means—Chair, Jay Emler (R-Lindsborg); Vice-Chairs, John Vratil (R-Leawood) and Carolyn McGinn (R-Sedgwick); Ranking Democrat, Laura Kelly (D-Topeka)
    • The Senate’s Organization, Calendar, and Rules Committee is scheduled to meet December 19th to finalize committee assignments for the upcoming session.

    The House is expected to announce its committee leadership lineup yet this week. USA|Kansas will update members when that occurs.
