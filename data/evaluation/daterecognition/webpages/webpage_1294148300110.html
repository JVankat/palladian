Florida Receiver Frankie Hammond Suspended

Written by playerpress.com, Sunday June 13 2010

Florida Gators receiver Frankie Hammond Jr., who in the teams’ spring game caught four passes and scored a touchdown, has been suspended from all team activities following his arrest shortly after 2 a.m. on Sunday for driving under the influence and possession.

 

According to school spokesman Steve McClain, Hammond has been suspended indefinitely from all team activities. Hammond is the 28th Florida Gator football player to have a legal issue since Urban Meyer became Florida's head coach in 2005.

 

University of Florida police say that Hammond, a 20-year-old sophomore receiver who saw limited time a year ago and was expected to be part of Florida's revamped receiving corps this season, was speeding in a 20 mph speed zone on campus, swerving and he allegedly had two open bottles of whiskey in his car early Sunday morning.

 

Officer Samuel Delucca says Hammond was driving faster than 45 miles per hour in a 20-mph speed zone while swerving on Gale Lemerand Drive on Florida's campus at 2:09 a.m., according to a police report provided by Alachua County Sheriff's Office.

 

After an initial appearance in front of a judge, Hammond is expected to be released from the Alachua County Jail on Sunday.

 

Hammond, who would be a redshirt sophomore in the fall, caught just four passes for 57 yards last season with one touchdown but was expected to compete for a starting job in 2010.
