Stocks

   

TOKYO | Thu Feb 5, 2009 11:04am IST

TOKYO (Reuters) - Japan's Nikkei average .N225 dipped 0.5 percent in choppy trade on Thursday as defensive stocks such as drugmakers lost steam after recent gains, but steelmakers and shippers jumped on hopes for Chinese economic steps.

In the United States, poor earnings from Kraft Foods (KFT.N) and forecasts after the bell of a sharp loss for tech bellwether Cisco Systems Inc (CSCO.O) frayed worries that the recession may be deepening even as an economic stimulus bill remains unpassed.

"The market sank early in the day in response to losses in U.S. stocks but rebounded as it eventually ran low on bearish factors," said Yoshinori Nagano, chief strategist at Daiwa Asset Management.

"Stocks may have found a near-term bottom as investors have digested the string of downbeat corporate and economic news," Nagano said.

After moving in and out of positive territory, the benchmark Nikkei .N225 was down 41.87 points at 7,997.07 while the broader Topix .TOPX fell 0.3 percent to 790.37.

Drug firms weighed on the market. Chugai Pharmaceutical Co (4519.T) fell 1.8 percent to 1,622 yen after Nikko Citigroup cut its rating on the drugmaker to "Sell/Medium Risk" from "Hold/Medium Risk" and lowered its target price to 1,600 yen from 1,700 yen, citing an increase in research and development costs.

But market players said a glimmer of hope toward the Chinese economy helped underpin stocks.

"Stocks related to demand from China such as machinery makers have gained recently on hopes that the Chinese economy will recover relatively soon as its government economic spending accounts for 4 or 5 percent of GDP," said Takahiko Murai, general manager at Nozomi Securities.

A rise in China's official manufacturing index, coupled with a surge in bank lending, has fed optimism that the world's third-largest economy may soon be on the path to recovery.

Shipping firms surged after the key Baltic Dry freight index .BADI soared nearly 15 percent, which some market players attributed to growth in demand from China or hopes this will soon result, with Mitsui O.S.K. Lines (9104.T) climbing 6.6 percent to 647 yen.

Kawasaki Kisen (9107.T) soared 7.9 percent to 396 yen and Nippon Yusen (9101.T), Japan's biggest shipping firm, climbed 5.1 percent to 495 yen.

China hopes buoyed steel firms as well, with JFE Holdings (5411.T) up 3.6 percent at 2,595 yen and Nippon Steel (5401.T) up 1.1 percent at 283 yen.

(Reporting by Shinichi Saoshiro, Aiko Hayashi and Elaine Lies; Editing by Michael Watson)
