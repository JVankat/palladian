Govt announces new licence for IPLC services

Wednesday, September 24, 2008, 00:00
Tags:
0  Tweet
New Delhi, Sept 24: To promote competition and make bandwidth-based services like BPO and call centre operations more affordable, the government today announced a new category of licence for International Private Leased Circuits (IPLC) services.

The new category of licence called Resale of IPLC Service would available with immediate effect, the Department of Telecommunication said in a statement.

BPO units, software exporters, call centers, banks and other small and medium enterprises will immensely benefit from the move, as they are the major users of leased line services.

As per the new licence, the reseller should provide end-to-end IPLC between India and country of destination.

Resellers should be permitted to enter into an arrangement for leased line with Access Providers, National Long Distance Service Providers and International Long Distance Service Providers for provision of IPLC to end customers.

Only Indian company, registered under the Companies Act, 1956 can apply for resale of IPLC Service Licence.

The networth as well as paid-up capital of the company should be minimum Rs 2.5 crore.

The entry fee shall be Rs 1 crore and the annual licence fee shall be 6 per cent of Adjusted Gross Revenue (AGR) or Rs 5 lakh whichever is higher.

The initial financial bank guarantee shall be Rs 1 crore.

The Lawful Interception and Monitoring of Resellers circuits shall take place at the Gateway/Monitoring system of the ILD operator from whom the IPLC has been taken by the Reseller.
