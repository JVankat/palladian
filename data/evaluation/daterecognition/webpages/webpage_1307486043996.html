By Jim Aughney

Tuesday September 21 2004

SHARES in Aminex, the Irish oil and gas explorer, rose to their highest level in seven weeks after the company signed a 20-year agreement with North Korea to develop its oil reserves.

The shares jumped 3.5p, or 36pc, to 13.25p in London, the highest level since early August.

Aminex, which has oil and gas production in the US and exploration interests in Tanzania, will initially provide technical assistance for onshore and offshore drilling projects, and will earn royalties on production from new wells. North Korea is developing its oil reserves as it seeks to reduce dependence on its much-publicised nuclear industry.

"Relations between North Korea and the outside world are strained, but the important relationship with South Korea appears to be improving and commercial co-operation is on the increase," Brian Hall, Aminex chairman said.

"It is the nature of the oil industry that you need to operate in some of the remotest parts of the world and North Korea is referred to as the 'hermit regime' because of the secrecy," Mr Hall said. "We have been talking to the North Koreans for five years, and have found them relatively easy to deal with despite President Bush's 'axis of evil' speech," he added.

Aminex will have limited financial exposure and will initially provide technical assistance through locating two staff in Pyongyang.

Animex will have a prior right to explore in its own name anywhere in the country as part of North Korea's drive to develop an indigenous energy industry.

North Korea has an existing oil industry and several wells have been drilled onshore and offshore over a 25-year period, resulting in limited discoveries of oil.

Aminex believes that the country is highly prospective for new discoveries, but lack of resources has so far restricted progress.

Under the agreement, Aminex will initially provide technical assistance to the country through assessing existing data, obtaining new data, assisting with drawing up a framework for licensing exploration areas, and for marketing the country's potential to the international oil industry.

- Jim Aughney
