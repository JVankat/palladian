Jayhawk Conference well represented at volleyball national championships

Proving to be one of the toughest volleyball conferences in the country, the Jayhawk Conference recently had three teams place in the top-10 of their respective national tournaments.

Both Cowley College (national runner-up) and Johnson County (sixth place) had stellar showings at the NJCAA Division II National Championships held in Wisconsin Dells, WI Nov. 19-21. While, Hutchinson placed ninth at the NJCAA Division I National Championships held in Council Bluffs, IA.

Jayhawk Conference Commissioner Bryce Roderick enjoyed watching these three schools blossom towards the end of the season.

�It was exciting to have three teams representing the conference and region so well at the national tournament,� Roderick said. �It was really reflective as to how good volleyball is in the Jayhawk Conference and was a great way to cap the season.�
Roderick praised the coaching jobs done by Jenifer Bahner (Cowley), Jenny Ei (Johnson County), and Ronda Shirley (Hutchinson).

For the Tigers, this marked the second time in the past five years that the team has finished as the national runner-up. The last time coming in 2005 when they lost the national championship to Johnson County.

After winning its first three matches at the national tournament against Monroe College (N.Y.) 25-18, 25-17, 25-16, Des Moines (IA) Area Community College (25-19, 21-25, 25-21, 25-17), and Parkland College (20-25, 25-20, 25-22, 19-25, 15-13) to advance to the title match against Illinois Central College, Cowley fell behind two games to none to the Lady Cougars.

Indicative of its season, Cowley would not go down without a fight. The Lady Tigers rallied to win the next two games (25-23, 25-22) to force a fifth and final set.

Having to expend so much energy to fight back in the match seemed to catch up to the Lady Tigers in the final game as the Lady Cougars won by a score of 15-7 to claim the national title.

�Going five games and being so close makes it tough, but we need to look at all the great things we did,� Bahner said. �I am very proud of them to fight back and get to a fifth game.�

The second place finish matches the program�s highest ever finish at the national tournament. Cowley ends the season with a record of 32-5 and had the highest winning percentage (86.5%) in the program�s history.
Bahner had a season to remember as she helped guide Cowley to conference and region titles, a 19-match win streak, and a runner-up national finish.

Johnson County, which finished in a tie for second place behind Cowley in the Jayhawk Conference Eastern Division, rallied to defeat Iowa Lakes in five games (22-25, 22-25, 25-19, 25-15, 15-9) before losing its second round match against Kishwaukee (IL) College (23-25, 23-25, 19-25).

The Lady Cavaliers would then defeat Kalamazoo (MI) Valley Community College in three games (25-17, 25-19, 25-20) to advance to the fifth/sixth place match, which they dropped to Des Moines (IA) Area Community College (17-25, 19-25, 22-25). 

Still, it was another stellar season for the Lady Cavaliers as they finished with a record of 27-9. Ei, who has accumulated a record of 57-13 in her two seasons as head coach at Johnson County, believes the conference was much tougher from top to bottom.

�We had a stronger conference this year than in years past,� Ei said. �Our conference is one that is very competitive. We were well represented at the national tournament and Cowley did an excellent job.�

Both Cowley and Johnson County had only three returning players on its roster, meaning next year�s teams should once again be in the hunt for a national championship.

�We are really looking forward to next year,� Ei said. �Our freshmen did a nice job of progressing and gained the experience they needed.�

At the NJCAA Division I National Championships, Hutchinson put an opening round loss (21-25, 17-25, 18-25) to Hillsborough Community College behind them as they came back to win three matches in a row, including the ninth place match against San Jacinto College by a score of 25-23, 24-26, 25-19, 26-24.

Hutchinson also defeated Spartanburg Methodist College (25-15, 25-8, 25-10), and Panola College (25-19, 25-20, 17-25, 25-21) en route to its ninth place finish. The Lady Blue Dragons, which placed second in the Jayhawk West, finished the season with a record of 33-9.

Hutchinson head coach Ronda Shirley, the school�s all-time leader in coaching victories, was proud of her team for fighting back from the opening round loss.

�Unfortunately, we lost at the wrong time,� Shirley said. �We could have checked it in, but I instilled in them to take pride in representing us and the region.�

With Hutchinson finishing one game behind Seward County in the Jayhawk West, and Pratt (8-4) and Barton County (7-5) posting winning records in the conference, Shirley was impressed with the level of competition her team faced during the season.

�I think the conference was more level than I have seen it in the 12 years I have been here,� Shirley said. �Any four of us (Hutchinson, Seward, Pratt, and Barton County) could have gone to the national tournament.�

Like Cowley and Johnson County, Hutchinson will also return a strong team next season. Don�t be surprised to see the Lady Blue Dragons making another run at conference and regional titles, as well as a national championship.
