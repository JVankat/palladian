New Loveland Supercenter Eases Shopping for Area Residents

Alpine timber exterior designed to compliment natural beauty

LOVELAND, Colo., Sept. 12, 2007 – Shopping will soon be easier and more convenient for residents in Loveland and surrounding areas, when the newly relocated Wal-Mart Supercenter opens Sept. 19. With everything from fresh produce and sushi to the latest home electronics, the store offers the Loveland community more than Wal-Mart values and one-stop shopping. Before stepping in the doors, customers will notice the unique lodge-like exterior themed to blend in with local architecture and landscaping.

“We are a store of the community and the attractive exterior features are one way that we can demonstrate that,” said Store Manager Rick Wilms.

The store will mark its opening with a grand opening ceremony at 7:30 a.m. Sept. 19. Manager Wilms and local chamber officials will join members of the community in the official ribbon cutting celebration.

Same Great Service with Added Conveniences, Greater Selection
The new 207,489-square-foot store features appealing new elements evident from the outside, in. Inside shoppers will find stained concrete and wood laminate flooring and shopper-friendly features such as wider aisles, lowered shelving and navigational signing.

“Now, more than ever, we’ll be able to provide our customers with large selections of merchandise, services and value,” Store Manager Rick Wilms said. “A Supercenter also provides a convenience to shopping that’s bar none. The addition of groceries and time-saving services truly makes us a one-stop shopping destination.”

The store’s general merchandise areas include a wide range of infant apparel and products, toys, fine jewelry, an expanded lawn and garden center with separate side entrance, expanded home entertainment and electronics department and large health and beauty section.

The store’s grocery department offers a fresh produce section, baked goods, a delicatessen with fresh sushi bar, a frozen food section and meat and dairy sections as well as beer sales. The full deli offers rotisserie chicken and other hot foods and a wide variety of fresh prepared foods including sandwiches and salads.

The convenience continues with services including a Tire & Lube Express, vision center, a pharmacy with two drive-through lanes and a one-hour photo lab. Leased space and services within the store include a Regal Nails Salon, a SmartStyle Family Hair Salon, a McDonald’s restaurant, and a branch of Academy Bank. The store will be open to customers 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

New Associates Join Wal-Mart Team
Approximately 300 new associates will join Wal-Mart’s team with the opening of the new Supercenter bringing the total planned associate count to 500. As of early September the store had received more than 2,000 applications. Two of those associates have worked for the store since it originally opened in 1987.

“There are always opportunities for new careers and growth at Wal-Mart,” Wilms said. “Working for Wal-Mart provides a number of job opportunities and choices for so many people, from seniors looking for supplemental income to young people just starting their work experience.” Wilms is an example of the career growth Wal-Mart offers. More than 76 percent of Wal-Mart store managers began their career with the company as hourly associates. Wilms began his career in 1987 working in receiving.

Community Support Continues
Wal-Mart will continue to support organizations that are important to the area and their customers with numerous community grants. In keeping with company’s philosophy of giving back locally, the new store will announce $26,000 in charitable donations to community groups as part of the grand opening ceremony.

“We are committed and invested in our local communities.” Wilms said. “I am proud to be part of a company that gives back to the communities it serves.”

The store will also have a budget to give donations away locally throughout the year. Organizations can contact the store to inquire about grants, matching grants and opportunities to raise dollars outside store locations throughout the year. EDITOR’S NOTE: Grant recipients are listed on the fact sheet on page three.

Grand Opening Celebration 7:30 a.m., Sept. 19
• An invitation-only open house for VIPs, Wal-Mart associates and their families is scheduled for Monday, Sept. 17, from 6 p.m. to 8 p.m. Preview tours will be provided and checks will be presented to representatives from community organizations. Reporters and photographers are welcome. The community is invited to attend a grand opening celebration at 7:30 a.m., Wednesday, Sept. 19. Store Associate Christine Lesterance will lead the invocation. The Associated Veterans of Loveland will present the colors and the Best Regards Quartet will sing the national anthem. Chamber officials and store associates will help Wilms with the official ribbon cutting. Throughout the day, customers will be able to enjoy numerous product samples, giveaways and character appearances.
