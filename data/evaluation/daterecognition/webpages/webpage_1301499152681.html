Spartans sweep through round robin

By: Uncredited

11/12/2010 1:00 AM | Comments: 0

  • Print
  • E–mail
  • Report Error

Enlarge Image

Vincent Massey Viking Iain Cowie lays the ball in for an easy two points following a turnover against Roblin during the Brandon Sun Spartan Invitational basketball tournament at Vincent Massey High School on Friday. Massey won the game 83-30. (BRUCE BUMSTEAD/BRANDON SUN)

Enlarge Image

Vincent Massey Vikings' Harrison Robb shoots from the three-point line against Roblin's Goose Lake Raiders during the Brandon Sun Spartan Invitational basketball tournament at Massey on Friday. The Vikings won 83-30. (BRUCE BUMSTEAD/BRANDON SUN)

The Neelin Spartans qualified for the championship bracket of the second tier at the Brandon Sun Spartan Invitational, while two former champions are back in the final four in the top tier.

The Spartans completed a 3-0 round robin with a pair of wins Friday in the Tri-Star Screen Printing Division, while the Oak Park Raiders and Garden City Fighting Gophers will meet in a semifinal of first-place finishers in the Victoria Inn Division.

Here's a round-up of Day 2 of the seventh annual 32-team event:

TRI-STAR DIVISION

Neelin 62 Swan Valley 32

Mark Jacobson's 15 points paced the Spartans, while Nyle Williams had 13 for the Tigers.

Neelin 75 Winston Knoll 46

Theo Farough had 18 points in the Spartans final game in pool play and Connor Enion had 12 for Winston Knoll.

Killarney 60 Swan Valley 51

Jordan Dyck led Killarney with 13 points and Swan Valley got 16 from Williams.

In another game from this pool, Killarney beat Winston Knoll 56-48. Top scorers were unavailable.

Elmwood 95 Crocus 94

Crocus' Justin Arthurson dropped a Tier 2 single-game BSSI record 48 points in a losing cause. Ryan Griffiths scored 34 for the Giants.

R.D. Parker 85 Crocus 63

Matt Gillis had 27 points to lead R.D. Parker while Arthurson added 17.

Sanford 72 Elmwood 71

Sanford's Nathan Warburton had a game-high 29 points and Justin Canseco replied with 21 for Elmwood.

R.D. Parker 63 Sanford 59

Arnav Sud's 18 points led the Trojans, who won the pool.

Portage 90 Holy Rosary 62

Portage got a game-high 26 from Logan Lambert while Lloydminster, Alta.'s Raiders were led by Brett Spenrath's 19 points.

St. John's 57 Stonewall 55

Dylan Blind led all scorers with 27 for the Tigers, while Stonewall got 16 each from Zach Stasynec and Ryley Holod.

St. John's 79 Portage 58

Angelo Domondon had 10 points for 10 and Lambert chipped in 21 for Portage.

Stonewall 56

Holy Rosary 27

Holod had 17 for the Rams, Bretton Greening 10 for Holy Rosary.

Dauphin 76 Goose Lake 37

Tyler Cook scored a game-high 30 for Dauphin and Jesse Gensorek replied with 11 for the Raiders.

Selkirk 66 Dauphin 45

DeRooy had 22 to top the Royals, who finished 3-0. Michael Belinsky had 12 for the Clippers.

VICTORIA INN DIVISION

Daniel McIntyre 92

Churchill 49

Jeyson Saceda had 15 points for the Maroons and Matt Lazzari had 21 for Churchill.

Daniel McIntyre 78

Fort Richmond 55

Saceda scored 15 in a battle of unbeatens and put the Maroons into the first-place bracket today. Bosko Zorcic had 15 for the Centurions.

River East 80 Churchill 57

Josh Hutton had 17 points to lead the Bulldogs.

In another game, Fort Richmond beat River East 64-61 in overtime.

Garden City 89 Dakota 74

Jordan Kelly had 25 points for GC, which won the 2007 BSSI. Riley Deavau topped Dakota, the defending champion, with 15 points.

Superior 70

Sheldon Williams 68

Alex Robichaud had 26 points as Thunder Bay's Superior won a battle of out-of-province teams. Caillin McMurtry had 18 to top Regina's Spartans.

Garden City 96

Sheldon Williams 65

The Gophers pulled away lade led by Kelly's 40 points. Addison Richards had 14 for the Spartans.

Superior 73 Dakota 67

Jordan Abraham hit for 31 for Superior and Josh McKay led the Lancers with 13.

Oak Park 74 Westgate 23

Guard Cash Blanca had 15 for the Raiders, the only team to have won the tournament twice. Ross Armstrong had six for Thunder Bay's Westgate.

Kild. East 95 J.H. Bruns 73

John Deng scored 21 for the Rievers and Tyler Powell had eight for Bruns.

Oak Park 106 J.H. Bruns 36

Junior Sesay had 16 points to lead the 3-0 Raiders and Matt Choi had 11 for Bruns.

Kelvin 113 MBCI 59

A big second half led the Clippers to their third win and second straight output of 100-plus points. Michael Thorne-Finch had 20 points while MBCI was led by Jeff Stafeckis, who had 15.

MBCI 90 Miles Mac 84

The Hawks had 27 points from Grade 12 guard Noah Derksen while Jordan Barendregt led the Buckeyes with 21.

Miles Mac 87 G. Valley 68

Jamar Farley had 30 to top the Buckeyes, who got their first win. Toby Milne had 24 for the Zodiacs.
