The terrorism  in Keralam.

Kerala’s contribution to arts, science, technology, literature, and so on are  memorable.  Sree Sankaracharya united this nation   by establishing four  matts  in the four corners of Bharath……these  are all  becoming the story of the past. Now Kerala is becoming  the source for many negatives…..the muscle power of  minority is  spreading through out the country from Kerala…the terrorism messages are going from Keralam……. The bombs  used by the   terrorists  are stored in this Parasurama Kshetra. All the political nuisances, hartals, bandhs, destruction of  temples and aasrams   have become the part of the day today  news  in Kerala.  We request all Indians  never  try to copy Keralam  which, once upon a time  was an ideal model for  all other states of India….

Let the media  not produce   the reflection of the what is  happening in the society. Let the media  produce and re-produce what should happen in the  society and nation…. Let them become the guiding force  for one  and all independent of the  caste, religion, political lenience  and regional partiality.

Without far sightedness, the politicians  created  many problems  which can never be  solved  by anyone at any time.  In the name of  nationality  and secularism our politicians  have  destroyed (are destroying) our nation. They are/ were doing the same  thing for the  last six decades….

The background sad story of Chicago Lecture of Swami Vivekananda.
Swami wanted to visit  the west…he expressed his desire to  attend the parliament of religion to be held in Chicago in 1893……..He reviewed his experience of the past , present and  future of  mother India  and the causes of her downfall…..The picture of  ancient India appeared before his eyes  vividly in all its grandeur  and glory  and the contrast   was unbearable to the saint….some enlightened people in different part of India had previously advised him to go to the west…. he took  the decision to go to west for uplifting Bharath… the money received by begging and also  received as the blessings from the Maharajas was used for the journey……Swami reached Chicago at the end of July  via Colombo……. He was shocked to learn that  the  parliament of religion will commence only  in September…  and no delegate would be admitted without proper  credentials from organizations….he came to know the final selection of the delegates  was also over…. Swami did not foresee all these problems…he knew living in Chicago is costly  that too without money in his hand…. He left to Boston… on the way he met a lady  Miss Katherine Abbot Sanborn a co traveler in  the train…. She invited  Vivekananda to her home  and introduced  to a Greek Professor of Harvard University……. After six hours discussion, the professor could understand  the depth of knowledge of  swami and he said “ asking a credential to you is like asking the sun to state  its right to shine”…… he recommended  to the chairman of the  parliament…..” here is  a man  who is more learned than all our professors put together “  Unfortunately when  he reached Chicago  on 9th September, he found he had mislaid the address of the Committee…. The chilling September started  and he had to  find shelter  in an empty boxcar  in the railroad… he slept there trusting in the guidance of  Lord… after that he walked  and came to  the corner of rich quarters of the city  and sought  help from house to house begging… many shut their doors  seeing  his  dirty clothes  many treated him rudely   and some slammed the  doors too… he sat down on the road side. Just then the door of a fashionable residence opposite him opened and a lady of regal appearance came out and spoke to him in soft voice…. She invited   him and  gave food…. She took him to the office of the  Parliament of religions  and he could became a delegate  and was lodged with the oriental guests. .. his deliverer  was Mrs. George W Hale …. Swami was convinced that  the Lord was with him…… behind every achievement and recognition  there are stories  of pain and sufferings…

PINGALA UPANISHAD
Pingala Upanishad is the  11th among the Sukla Yajurvedic Upanishads. It is presented as the discussion between Maharshi yajnavalkhya and his disciple Pingala, in four chapters. In the first chapter  following points are described. 1. The cosmic power and its relation with brahma chaitanya. 2. The colors of  the Brahmam.3. saathwika, raajasika and thaamasika  gunas present in this. 4.   The brahma chaitanyam is said to be filled in universe hence explained  as brahmaanda. Ie. Brahma + anda = brahmaanda. 5.  The awareness and consciousness existing in the universe is explained as the manifestation of the Brahmam itself : prajnaaanam brahma.  6.   From that  prajnaan ( awareness and consciousness) the  creation of the  ahamkaaram  (aham + kaaram) = swabhodham  takes place. 7. It is the  awareness and consciousness with correct direction (pathway)! So as to follow the manifestation and manipulation of that Brahmam ( awareness and consciousness) in all living and non living beings. 8. Defining and explaining this cosmic energy  as  prapancha purusha chaitanya  for symbolically presenting the brahmaanda concept.  9. Further presenting that cosmic energy  symbolically as Mahavishnu . 10. The formation of the pancha bhootha  from awareness and consciousness as pruthvi, ap, theja., vaayu, akaasa, + ahankaara  from macrocosm. ( the last one is the consciousness in microcosm). 11. Formation of  the elements  from  pancha bhootha and  from that the materials/compounds. 12. The formation of the material world  from these molecules. 12. From those  molecules  formed  each and every living and non living beings, including animals and plants with jeeva chaitanya (praana) in them. 13. From the molecules, the  cells, from the cells the tissues including the  sensory organs and  functional organs were formed  says the first chapter of the Pingala Upanishad.  ( this  explanation has  great similarity to the  evolution theory  and  beyond that). The second chapter of the Pingala Upanishad  is  the  answers given by Maharshi Yajnavalkhya  for the question “ how  the  divine power  manifested  as jeevaathma ?” asked by his disciple. Yajnavalkhya said 1.  The formation of pancha bhootha has taken place first.  2. In this panchabhootha based  body  the feelings  were  created/ generated  after the creation of the organs from pancha boothas. 3. Then formed the feelings like hunger, thirst, heat , cold, etc.  4. These are  created from the  agni/ theja part of the pancha bhootha. 5. All the body movements,  oxygen and air  transfer, etc  are linked  with the  vaayu part of the panchabhootha. 6. The desire, anger, jealousy,  and all the mental attitudes were generated from aakaasa ( part of the pancha bhootha)7. Then the five  state of praana  were  created  as praana – apaana – vyaana – udaana – samaana.  8. Each part of the body / organ has been attributed to one or more of the divine natural forces. 9. Each organ has  five  major foundation parameters  as annamaya kosha- praana maya kosha – manomaya kosha – vijanaanamaya kosha – aanandamaya kosha.  10. Through these pancha bhootha constituents and pancha praanas  the divinity of the nature gets manifested in body, said Yajnavalkhya Maharshi. 11. In the 3rd  chapter excellent explanation of four  mahavakyaas are given.  These  mahavakyaas are connected with the Vedas. From Rig-Veda  prajnaanam brahma, from Yajurveda, aham brahmasmi, from Sama Veda thathwam asi and from  Atharva Veda  the ayam athma brahma , (four) mahavakyaas were taught (these four mahavakyas are from the four  Upanishads  belonging respectively to the four Vedas)  12. In the 4th  chapter  the  sanyaasa charyas (acharas) and the qualifications of the  Vedic scholars who has practically implemented the Vedic messages in their life are explained.  13. The state at which the  aathma and paramaathma become one is explained as parama pada praapthi of the jeevaathma.

Two types of Tin :   There are two types of tin known as khurakam  and  misrakam. The tin which is white, soft, flexible,  melts  easily , denser  and without metallic sound is known as khurakam. Misrakam has  low quality and  it is blackish   white in color.  The former is  beta tin  and the latter is alpha tin according to modern science. ( Rasaratna samuccha 5/153 – 154  book written in 800 AD)

Bhikshuka Upanishad:
This is a small and simple Upanishad which discusses  the bhikshaatana rules for the sanyaasies  who  accept sanyaasa  directly ( as Sankaracharya did) or  after passing through brahmacharya, gruhastha, sanyaasa /  vaanaprastha. ( Do not translate the bhiskhaatana word as begging. accepting bhiskhsa and giving bhiksha are two holiest dharma). 1. There are  four levels for the bhikshus known as kuteechika bhikshus, Bahoodaka bhikshus, Hamsa bhikshus, Paramahamsa bhikshus. Gouthama, Bharadwaaja, Yajnavalkhya  and Vasishta  Maharshies are respectively  included in the  four  classes, in that order. 3. Kuteeechika bhikshus satisfy with eight hand full of annam / food 4. Bhahoodaka bhikshus take little food  but they  should have a three feet long bamboo piece+ kamandalu+ yajnopaveetha + saafron cloth  .  5. Hamsa bhikshus live with little  milk products and stay not more than a day in a village, five days in a town and seven days in holy places. 6. For parama Hamsa bhikshus nothing is  a criteria they live in a state where nothing is important and no  rules . 7. For  the parama Hamsa bhikshus the gold and stone are alike. 8. They  don’t have any attachment or feelings like happiness, unhappiness, fear, sorrow and so on. 9. Parama Hamsa bhikshus may live naked or half naked  in forests or a places similar to that. 10. They eat food just to maintain life/ jeevaathma in the body.
