Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

The Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster (Scorpious pinchius) is a foul and loathsome creature that tastes great with beer and melted butter. This conjecture is totally hypothetical, of course, since nobody has ever managed to capture a Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster, much less cook one.

Edit Origination of the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

Non-killer non-Africanized normal-sized pacifist underwater lobsters were taken away from their native radioactive waters of the Sea of Japan in the early 1950's and brought to Africa in a desperate attempt to take over the bulk of the continent from the vicious machete-wielding Tutsi warriors. However, the local natives became friendly with the scorpion-like creatures, inbred them for pure evilness, and taught them to pinch the "white man" instead.

The lobsters reach almost 5 feet in length, 3 feet in width, standing (in some cases) 24 inches at the top of their backs. There have been a small number of reports that a particularly large Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Lobster wanders about the desert, only moving at night, but these reports are mostly unfounded. There was a case where a safari wagon was found looking as if it had been pinched in half by one truly huge lobster claw, but authorities concluded that the sheer size of the beast would have to have been something close to that of two full grown elephants. And nothing with that ungodly size could possibly wander about without being seen. Therefore the report was written up as the vehicle being destroyed by many claws of regular sized Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Lobsters.

Edit The Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster Stalks its Prey

The lobsters are very fast, moving at speeds up to 35 miles per hour (56.32704 kph). One of their primary tactics involves lying still in mounds of sand until tourists on safari inadvertently go near. The lobster then latches upside down from the bottom of the vehicle and punctures the fuel tank with its poisonous stinger, injecting a delicious sugary venom that stalls the vehicle. Having rendered the safari vehicle helpless, it attacks the people inside and devours them whole. Head first in most cases, although one poor bastard was reportedly ate from both ends by two lobsters re-enacting the spaghetti scene from Lady and the Tramp.

Edit How to Avoid the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

Fortunately, if the tourists are near a tree, they can climb it to relative safety, since the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster cannot climb trees with its large claws and stubby legs. However, there are no trees in the African desert, so what good does this do the tourists? Well, if they happen to have a portable tree brought along on their safari by either themselves or their tour guide, climbing it would be the best act of self preservation possible.

Unfortunately, since the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster has recently been declared an endangered species, it is illegal for non-native tourists to harm it in any way. If a tourist does hurt a Killer Lobster, the lobster will rear up on its massive 58 inch hind legs and climb the tree, as a testament to the powers of evolution.

Edit How to Prepare the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

A famous African explorer [1] once said that you-know-whats were easy to catch and prepare for food. The famous explorer also realized that he brought an imitation turkey egg to use for cooking and was subsequently devoured by imitation zombified crustaceans.

Currently, there is no known method of capturing a Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster but just in case a lucky tourist manages to snare one, we have included a copy of the explorer's recipe:

  • 35 tons of Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster
  • 1 turkey egg
  • Beer and melted butter
  1. Stir lobster until nicely browned.
  2. Cook lobster over an open bonfire, preferably made of burning lobsters.
  3. Place 100% certified turkey egg on top.
  4. Eat with a dab of beer and melted butter.*
  • We are not sure if adding the legendary liquor-dairy condiment would be suitable for Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster, so it may be wise to first ask yourself, “Would ‘’’I’’’ taste good with beer and melted butter after being roasted over a batch of burning lobsters?” [2]

Edit Anatomy of the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

Due to the nature of the mutations concerning the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster, various anatomical differences have been noted. Firstly, the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster's skin is laced with pure fire, increasing its ability to murder senselessly. Its claws have also been tipped with laser-cut diamond, allowing the Killer Lobster to not only rip apart newly married tourists in an ironic manner, but present human-embedded claws to their mate(s) of choice. Finally, a claw can reach over 6 feet long when extended, thus proving that the Anatomy of the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster can hold its own in a fight with Wolverine. It's been proven that Wolverine was based on the Anatomy of the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster, but the name wasn't "catchy" enough, and thus he is known by a different alias.

It is also worth noting that the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster has adapted extremely well to the desert environment. Their characteristics allow them to live without water for over seven years, since they have the ability to thrive on human fear alone. In a last resort, Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobsters will enact ancient tribal rain dances to bring on thunderstorms, but being lobsters, the thunderstorms usually evolve into something like the picture to the right.

Edit Communication with the Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster

There have been several attempts at talking to the lobsters, with varying degrees of success. The closest attempt occurred when a journalist violently wrestled one to the ground until it passed out. Being a journalist who knew the dangers of passed-out Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobsters, the journalist stood eight feet away while electing to communicate via obsolete versions of Yeniseian [3] Sign Language. When asked for an interview, the lobster, however, ran away into the sunset on its massive 26 feet hind legs, shouting anti-establishment slogans like only a Killer Africanized Giant Fighting Desert Lobster could.
