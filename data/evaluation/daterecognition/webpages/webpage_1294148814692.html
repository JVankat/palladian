The FDA is pushing for a "BlackBox" warning on the diabetes drug Avandia. The drug came under pressure in May when a U.S. analysis linked Avandia to a 43% higher risk of heart attack.
 
An advisory panel to the FDA recommended that Avandia stay on the market, but said it should have increase warnings.
 
Avandia posted global sales of $3.24 billion last year making it the company's second best seller. But sales have plummeted since the May study linking it to increased risk of heart attack.
