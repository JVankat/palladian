UPDATED: Destin man arrested in boating accident that killed three (PHOTO GALLERY, ARREST REPORT)

Comments 0

December 10, 2009 6:15 PM
Angel McCurdy
Daily News

OKALOOSA ISLAND — A Destin man has been charged with boating under the influence manslaughter in connection to an accident Sept. 4 that killed an Alabama couple and one of their relatives from Texas.

Click here to view a photo gallery of the two boats involved in the collision »

Augusta Frederick Kennedy, 41, was arrested Wednesday on three counts of BUI manslaughter. He was also charged with boating under the influence, causing damage to property and five counts of violation of navigational rules resulting in an accident.

According to Michele Nicholson, spokeswoman for the Okaloosa County Sheriff’s Office, while being arrested, Kennedy attempted to use a trapped door installed in the second story of his home leading to his first floor to elude deputies. Charges on Kennedy are pending with the Sheriff’s Office.

Read the arrest report »

Kennedy was operating an 18-foot Bayliner at around 8 p.m. east of Marler Park in the Choctawhatchee Bay when it collided with a 21-foot Sea Hunter.

Three passengers on the Sea Hunter were killed in the accident. Donna Brenner, 78, of Daphne, Ala., and Jeane Crowson, 70, of San Antonio, Texas and Donna’s 79-year-old husband, George Brenner, who was pulled from the water, all died at the scene.

None of the passengers had on a life jacket.

The Brenners' son, Bill, was operating the Sea Hunter. Crowson was his aunt.

Kennedy’s blood alcohol level was tested and came back to 0.19; more than twice the legal limit, according to Stan Kirkland, with the Florida Fish and Wildlife Commission.

Brenner was also found responsible for part of the accident breaking violations on action to avoid a collision and responsibility of being a safe seaman. Brenner’s blood alcohol test showed he had a blood alcohol content of 0.069.

“Both operators judgment was impaired to some extent and contributed to the collision,” the report said.

The report went on to say that if Kennedy had corrected his course, the nearly head-on collision would not have occurred.

Kennedy had three passengers aboard his boat. One passenger, James L. Fernoff of Destin, told investigators he had mixed Kennedy several drinks including Vodka. Fernoff said he told Kennedy repeatedly to slow down on his drinking, but Kennedy would reply that he was fine, said a Florida boating accident investigation report by the FWC.

Several empty bottles were found in Kennedy’s boat, along with an empty bottle of Vodka.

Fernoff also told investigators he asked him to slow down as he was driving the boat and said he was scared of Kennedy’s driving.

Before the wreck, Kennedy dropped his three passengers off at Helen Back restaurant on Okaloosa Island. The passengers considered getting taking a cab back to Destin because of Kennedy’s driving, but went with him because they didn’t want to hurt his feelings, the report said.

Just prior to the collision, Fernoff told investigators he yelled “boat” to Kennedy.

Reports from the FWC found the two vessels collided almost head on, causing George to be ejected from the boat and hit his head on the skeg, which is the sternward extension of the keel of the boat.

Donna hit her head on the bow area of the boat causing internal fatal injuries. Crowson was thrown into the center console area causing internal injuries to the chest, the report said.

Kennedy’s violations from the FWC were listed as a head on collision violation, failure to maintain proper lookout, failure to operate a vessel at a safe speed, failure to take action for a collision and responsibility rule – taking responsibility for actions on vessel.

Kennedy made his first appearance at the Okaloosa County Jail Thursday. He will not be released on bond.
