Irish Beauty Brand Voya Gives Skin Natural TLC

by Jane Shin Park April 29, 2011

Photo by Jane Park.

From time to time, I still daydream about a heavenly spa treatment I got a couple of months ago while on vacay in Kauai. Called Voyage From The Sea, it’s a four-handed massage-plus-facial combo (doesn’t just the thought of it make your body go ahhhh...?) offered at the St. Regis’s brand-new (and very posh) Halele’a Spa, and I swear, my muscles never felt so Jell-o-like and happy. Since coming back home, my face has been totally freaking out from the temperature changes—very oily one minute and then superdry post-cleanse—and I’m kicking myself for not stocking up on the skin-care products that were used in the facial! Voya is a seaweed-based organic line from Ireland that’s hugely popular there but still under the radar in the U.S. I certainly had never heard of the brand before, but its My Little Hero face serum (packed with a blend of organic oils) and Maskerade face mask (made with seaweed and soothing chamomile extracts) performed a beauty miracle: They brought balance to my skin. No more ricocheting back and forth between extreme shininess and flaky dryness—FINALLY! Voya is currently carried in a limited number of places in the U.S. (for the full list, go here. If there isn’t one near you, though, don’t stress—trust me, this brand will blow up stateside soon enough.

Countdown to Prom: Healthy Foods for Healthy Skin

by Laurel Pantin March 14, 2011

Image by Horacio Salinas

We've all been told by some adult figure at one point to eat our fruits and veggies. But did you know that eating your greens has some impressive beauty benefits? According to Junelle H. Lupiani, the Nutrition Supervisor at Miraval Arizona, eating right can mean anything from an energy boost to an improved skin tone! See what she had to say, here:

To improve skin tone:
Eat lots of antioxidant-rich foods--in other words, fruits and vegetables! Also include omega-3 fatty acids in your diet; they can be found in foods like wild Alaskan salmon, walnuts, and freshly ground flaxseeds. These fats will help reduce and calm puffy or inflamed skin. Drink lots of water to keep the skin hydrated and healthy. Limit processed and refined foods, and opt for whole, healthful foods instead. Don't be afraid to eat a piece of dark chocolate now and then, since there is no link between chocolate and acne--and dark chocolate actually has antioxidant benefits!

To boost your energy:

Be sure to get plenty of B vitamins every day. Most B vitamins are found in fruits and vegetables, so be sure to get at least 3 servings of fruits and 5 servings of vegetables each day. This ensures that you get plenty of folate and other important B vitamins that increase our physical and mental energy. Vitamin B12 is one B vitamin not found in fruits and veggies; it is in meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy products. So make sure to include a few of these foods in your diet as well.

Make Your School Go Green with Pureology

by Phillip Picardi March 01, 2011

Pureology is a brand known for its sulfate-free formulas (which means a more eco-friendly lather), and now it is fully committed to spreading its green philosophy outside of the beauty world . . . and to your classroom!

The hair pros pledged a donation of $250,000 to Global Green USA's School Makeover Competition, which helps local schools (like yours) be more environmentally conscious. Whether it's solar panels, energy-efficient lighting, recycling programs, or an organic edible garden, the possibilities are endless!

Sure, it's not exactly what we had in mind when we heard "Pureology" and "makeover" . . .  but it's even better! Head over to the Pureology website to nominate your school! And while you're at it, you might as well nab their chic new Hydrate shampoo and conditioner!

Pamper Sensitive Skin with VMV Hypoallergenics

by Jane Shin Park January 12, 2011

If you feel like you've gotten the beauty shaft because you have very sensitive skin or allergies to ingredients commonly found in cleansers, lotions, and makeup, VMV Hypoallergenics is going to change your life! The entire product line has not only been scientifically validated as hypoallergenic through extensive clinical studies, but it's also comprehensive (they've got everything from face scrubs and sunscreens to acne treatments and makeup) and totally effective. Since the frigid weather has been majorly irritating my skin, I recently stopped by the new VMV boutique near NYC's Union Square and picked up a few items with the help of their in-house aesthetician. She recommended an amazing product called Grandma Minnie's Oil's Well Nurturing Do-It-Oil--a combo of organic virgin coconut oil and coconut-derived monolaurin (a gentle antibacterial agent). You can use it for everything from cleaning cuts to detangling hair, but I love applying a tiny amount to my face to help repair damaged skin cells and deeply moisturize. (Don't get freaked out by the idea of putting this oil on your face--100% virgin coconut oil absolutely won't clog pores!) My skin hasn't felt this soft, soothed, and nourished in months! If you're in the NYC area, check out VMV's products in person at their store/spa on 17th and Broadway--or just go online to www.vmvhypoallergenics.com.

Naked Juice Goes Even More Eco-Friendly With The ReNEWabottle

by Laurel Pantin November 15, 2010
Wahoo! My favorite juicy treat just got even more eco! As of today, Naked Juice is launching its reNEWabottle bottle, which is made of 100 percent post-consumer recycled (rPET) plastic. Not impressed? This little tweak will reduce plastic consumption by 7.4 MILLION pounds per year, and the new bottle will be entirely recyclable itself. The old bottles are slowly being phased out of stores (you can recognize them by their frosted texture) and will be totally replaced by the clear, smooth, rPET bottles by Earth Day 2011. Hooray!

Hair Stylist Brandon Martinez Wants a Cleaner Ocean!

by Phillip Picardi September 27, 2010

Haircare expert Brandon Martinez recently launched his own line of products, but that didn't stop the stylist from launching another project that is close to his heart -- the ocean. A devout surfer, Martinez grew frustrated with the way people were treating the waters he dwells in so frequently. "For my body, no other workout has made me feel the way surfing does. The ocean is the fountain of youth," he revealed.

The t-shirts, which are made from organic cotton, raise money for his B. Ocean Awareness Foundation, which helps save marine life and cleans up our oceans. Though the beach may be somewhat of a second home for him, Martinez still sticks to his stylist sensibilities. "After a day at the beach, it is always good to run a deep conditioner through the hair." He recommends a Vitamin B-based product, like the one from his namesake line.
