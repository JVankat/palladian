Feb 5 & 12
The Scarring
by Geoff Page
adapted for radio by Mike Ladd
Set in Geoff Page's home territory of The Clarence River region in Northern New South Wales, The Scarring is a novel in verse which tells the story of a cattle farming couple, Hal and Sally Mitchell. Part 1 begins in the 1920's with Hal's childhood and takes us up to the Second World War and an incident which blights Hal and Sally's relationship for the rest of their lives.
Narrator Syd Brisbane
Sally Kate Roberts
Hal Andrew Martin
Other roles played by Don Barker, Barbara West, Audine Leith, Adrian Shirley, Roger Newcombe, Geoff Revell, Peter Raymond Powell, and Michaela Cantwell.
Sound engineer Stuart Hall
Producer/director Mike Ladd

Feb 19
The Great Zoo
by Nicolas Guillen
translated by Robert Marquez
Performed by Paul Capsis, with music composed by Justo Diaz
Nicolas Guillen was born on 10 July 1902 in Camaguey, Cuba. He is known as Cuba's national poet. In his collection The Great Zoo, he sees humanity as a vast and diverse menagerie. In poetry ranging from the absurd to the far too real, he explores an enormous range of issues, including crime in New York, the disappearance of our natural wonders, whaling, and human rights.
Paul Capsis is a Sydney performer with an extraordinary voice. He has appeared on stage many times with his one man shows Whole Lotta Capsis and Alive. He is also an actor, and appeared in the recent feature film Head On.
Justo Diaz is a Sydney composer and performer. With his collection of more than two hundred musical instruments from Latin America, he has composed a very eclectic soundtrack for The Great Zoo.
With Paul Capsis, five musicians from Argentina, Brazil and beyond playing instruments with names like Quenacho, Ocarinas and Bombo Leguero, The Great Zoo is a PoeticA to get you on your feet.
Sound engineer Russell Stapleton
Producer/director Libby Douglas

Feb 26
Mal Morgan
Mal Morgan began writing his latest book Beautiful Veins shortly after being diagnosed with cancer in 1998. Many of the poems are dedicated to individual people, though his intention was to communicate with more than just those who were closest to him. The poems, written with his usual wry tenderness, are a celebration of life and love. In his introduction to the reader, Mal Morgan says:
"Today is July 27th, 1998. It is every day. It is a good day, one in which love is stronger than death."
This program includes selected poems from Beautiful Veins read by Roger Newcombe as well as recordings of Mal Morgan reading his own work.
Sound engineer Wayne Baker
Producer/ director Krystyna Kubiak
