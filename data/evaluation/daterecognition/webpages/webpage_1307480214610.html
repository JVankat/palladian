Mayor answers questions about library expansion

By LOIS EVEZICH
Laguna Niguel News
Story Highlights
Work should begin early next year to add 3,045 square feet to the building.
Next Article »
Tweet
Share
  
  • Article
  • Videos

The city of Laguna Niguel will fund the expansion of the Laguna Niguel Library to include more room for books, computers, restrooms, offices and meeting space. Mayor Cathryn DeYoung talked with the Laguna Niguel News about the plans.

Q: Why is the city expanding the library?

A: In December the Citizen's Satisfaction Survey told the City Council that our library is too small. That survey helped get the council's attention. It was a key issue with community members.

Q: What will be added to the library when the project is done?

A: We'll have a quiet study room, 30 percent more shelf space and more study pavilions. Storage rooms will take care of those boxes in the community room, and the community room will be enlarged and can be divided into two for special programs and story times. Part of that room can be used for a homework center. Offices will be expanded and men's and women's restrooms will be remodeled. We'll make the entrance comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and add fire sprinklers to the entire building.

Q: How many additional square feet will there be?

A: Plans call for 3,045 additional square feet.

Q: How much will it cost?

A: The City Council has budgeted $3 million dollars to the project. The library is owned by the county, but the county has no money to pay for these major projects. We're expecting to pay about $250 per square foot.

Q: When will the project start and how long will it take?

A: We'll probably break ground in early 2007. The working drawings are finished, and the council has approved them. But there are environmental studies and final construction plans before we start. Those plans could take four to six months to complete. We expect the new library will be finished in late summer or fall of 2008.

Q: Will the library be open during construction?

A: We'd like it to be but the building will be gutted.

Q: What other changes will residents see?

A: We're planning natural fibers instead of synthetic for carpets. That's important for people with allergies. The floor-to-ceiling windows will let in natural light, and we're building a quiet area where the open patio is now. The exterior will look different, as well. We have had an increase in the circulation of books so there'll be more shelves to accommodate more books. And the Friends of the Library will have more space where the community room is now.
