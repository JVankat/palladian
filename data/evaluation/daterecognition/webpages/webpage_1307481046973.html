Series Preview: Philadelphia Phillies (24-12) at Atlanta Braves (21-18)

May 13th

Posted by Meghan P. in Posts

No comments

After a 2-1 series win against the Florida Marlins, the Phillies will make their second trip of 2011 to Turner Field to face the Atlanta Braves for a three game set. The Phillies and Braves will also play in the Civil Rights Game Sunday afternoon. This series will also mark the return of catch Carlos Ruiz after a brief stint on the disabled list. This season, the Braves and Phillies’ season series is tied with each having won three games a apiece. 

GAME ONE: Cole Hamels (4-2, 2.83) at Brandon Beachy (1-1, 2.98)
(Friday, May 13th 7:35 PM — MYPHL17, 1210 AM)

Hamels will make his second consecutive start against Atlanta. In his last start, Hamels went seven innings and allowed three runs on five hits with nine strikeouts and one walk in the loss to Atlanta. It will be Hamels’ third start against Atlanta this season; in 2011 the Braves are batting a mere .188 against him with nine hits in 48 at bats with three hits for extra bases, including two home runs. Freddie Freeman and Alex Gonzalez account for five of those hits and each have a home run against Hamels. Brian McCann and Chipper Jones are a combined 0-12 this season with five strikeouts between them. Beachy is coming off a solid start against Milwaukee; going six shutout innings while allowing just four hits with nine strikeouts and a walk. In his April start against the Phillies, Beachy earned a loss and allowed four runs on seven hits with just two strikeouts over six innings of work. This season, the Phillies are batting .300 against Beachy with six hits in 20 at bats with four of those hits for extra bases.

GAME TWO: Joe Blanton (1-1, 5.83) at Jair Jurrjens (4-0, 1.50)
(Saturday, May 14th 1:10 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

Blanton will make his second start after his return to the rotation; going five innings while allowing three runs on hits, Blanton earned his first win of the season. It will be his first start of 2011 against Atlanta. For his career Blanton is 1-3 with a 5.67 ERA in 46 innings pitched with 29 strikeouts. Batting .270 against Blanton, the Braves have 37 hits in 137 at bats, with 12 hits for extra bases. Brian McCann is batting .400 off of Blanton with eight hits in 20 at bats including two home runs and six RBIs with three walks. Jurrjens’ last start was Sunday against the Phillies; going seven innings and holding the Phillies offense to one run on eight hits with just two strikeouts in the win. In 2011, the Phillies offense is batting .320 against Jurrjens with only two of the eight hits against Jurrjens are for extra bases.

GAME THREE: Roy Halladay (5-2, 2.05) at Tim Hudson (4-3, 3.09)
(Sunday, May 15th 1:35 PM, Civil Rights Game — MYPHL17, 1210 AM)

Halladay was masterful in his last outing against Florida, but an error by Jimmy Rollins allowed the sole run (unearned) of the game to score and the Phillies offense was unable to muster up much of an effort behind Halladay. Halladay who went eight innings while allowing one unearned run on five hits with nine strikeouts and two walks in hist last start will be making his first start against Atlanta this season. In 35.2 innings, Halladay is 4-0 against the Braves with 1.26 ERA and 25 strikeouts. Atlanta is batting .258 against Halladay with 33 hits in 128 at bats with 13 hits for extra bases. Dan Uggla is 7-19 against Halladay, including a triple and two home runs with four RBIs and two strikeouts. Chipper Jones also likes to hit Halladay; he is 5-10 with two doubles and two home runs. Hudson is coming off his third loss of the season while facing Washington; going just five innings and allowing three runs (two solo home runs) on five hits with three strikeouts and two walks. In Hudson’s other start against the Phillies in April, Hudson lasted seven and two-thirds innings in the win allowing just two runs on eight hits while only striking out two. Batting .222 against Hudson in 2011, the Phillies are 6-27 with just one hit for extra bases and three RBIs.

 UP NEXT: The Phillies will conclude their road trip with a brief stay in St. Louis for a two game series against the Cardinals.

“Cocks in the Hen House” is a weekly column by friends of the Chicks that aren’t, well, chicks.  Stop by every Wednesday to see what the men-folk have to say about the weeks biggest baseball stories.

By James (@PhuturePhillies)

Hi everyone, please allow me to introduce myself. I’m James, and for the last 4+ years, I’ve maintained the website phuturephillies.com. When Michelle was looking for a volunteer for this week’s CITHH piece, I stepped up, as I’ve always been a fan of the site, and I’ll take every chance I can get to talk prospects with just about anyone. My idea for the piece was to give a little overview of how the minor leagues work, from a developmental standpoint, and what to look for when checking out a game or even just looking at minor league box scores. The minor leagues have long been underreported on, but that seems to be shifting, and all fans should at least have some idea of how the farm system works, in my opinion.

The first important thing to note is that the farm system serves two goals: provide prospects to the big league team (Chase Utley, Ryan Howard, Cole Hamels, etc) and provide ammunition for our smuggy GM to plunder the big league roster of other teams (Halladay trade, Oswalt trade, etc) to acquire MLB ready talent. Big market teams (like the Phillies) do more of the latter, as they generally do not have the luxury of plugging in multiple rookies and letting them learn on the job. Series Preview: Philadelphia Phillies (22-11) at Florida Marlins (20-13)

May 9th

Posted by Meghan P. in Posts

1 comment


The Phillies will kick off a eight game road trip starting with a three game set against the Florida Marlins at Sun Life Stadium. The series will also mark the return of Joe Blanton to the starting rotation after missing two starts due to an elbow injury. In April, the Phillies and Marlins spilt their shortened series 1-1.

GAME ONE: Joe Blanton (0-1, 5.92) at Javier Vazquez (2-2, 6.39)
(Monday, May 9th 7:10 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

Blanton will make his first start since April 23rd after missing two starts due to an impingement in his right elbow. Blanton has a 5-2 career record against Florida with a 3.12 ERA with 50 strikeouts in 49 innings of work. While he has yet to face Florida yet this season, the Marlins are batting .220 against him with 22 hits in 100 at bats, with nine for extra bases. He has yet to allow a home run to a current Marlins hitter. Vazquez is coming off a rough start against St. Louis where he lost a four run lead; lasting just five and two-thirds innings while allowing six runs (four earned) on eight hits. He earned no decision in a game he started against the Phillies in April; lasting just five innings while allowing three runs on five hits with four walks and just one strikeout. Batting .309 against Vazquez, the Phillies have 76 hits in 246 at bats, with 29 for extra bases including 11 home runs.

GAME TWO: Roy Halladay (5-1, 2.19) at Josh Johnson (3-1, 1.68)
(Tuesday, May 10th 7:10 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

Halladay and Johnson will meet again for a May game; the last time the two aces met under these circumstances, Halladay threw the 20th perfect game in MLB history. Halladay is coming off another strong outing against Washington; going seven innings and allowing just two runs on six hits with 10 strikeouts. He missed the Marlins during their visit to Citizen’s Bank Park in April and has a 4-2 record with 2.30 ERA for his career. Their current lineup is batting .255 against him with 24 hits in 94 at bats with just four hits for extra bases. Johnson is coming off of his worst outing of the season, allowing five runs on eight hits while striking out seven and walking four batters in seven innings of work. In 70 innings, Johnson has a 5-3 record against the Phillies 3.34 ERA. Batting .261 against Johnson, the Phillies have 40 hits 153 at bats with just eight for extra bases. Ryan Howard is batting .310 against Johnson with 9 hits in 29 at bats, including two doubles and two home runs totaling for seven RBIs.

GAME THREE: Cliff Lee (2-3, 3.69) at Ricky Nolasco (3-0, 3.23)
(Wednesday, May 11th 7:10 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

Lee is coming off a loss against Atlanta in which he struck out 16 while allowing three runs on nine hits in seven innings of work. It was Lee’s fourth double-digit strikeout game of the season. Pitching 11 innings against Florida, Lee has a 1-0 record with 4.09 ERA with 10 strikeouts. While Cliff Lee has not faced the majority of the Marlins lineup, in 62 at bats the Marlins have 23 hits with nine for extra bases. John Buck has the hot bat against Lee with 12 hits in 34 at bats with three doubles and three home runs with six RBIs and five walks. Nolasco earned no decision in his last start against Washington; allowing just two runs on six hits with 11 strikeouts in seven innings. In 64.3 innings of work, Nolasco has a 6-3 record against the Phillies 3.78 ERA with 50 strikeouts. The Phillies are batting .238 against Nolasco with 30 hits in 126 at bats with 14 hits for extra bases. Howard has Nolasco’s number with 5 hits in 23 at bats including a double and three home runs with six RBIs and six walks.

Series Preview: Atlanta Braves (18-15) at Philadelphia Phillies (21-9)

May 6th

Posted by Meghan P. in Posts

No comments


Coming off a three game sweep of the Washington Nationals, the Phillies will complete  their ten game homestand against the Atlanta Braves in a three game series.  Both the Phillies are Braves are shuffling their rotations for this series. Roy Oswalt returned back from a personal leave of absence from the team, only to suffer another setback with a sore back; Kyle Kendrick will make a spot start in his place.  To face Kendrick, the Braves have called up one of their top pitching prospects in Julio Teheran. Carlos Ruiz again will most likely miss the series with the disabled list still a possibility for the catcher.  In a mid-April visit to Turner Field, the Phillies won their first series against the Braves 2-1.

GAME ONE: Derek Lowe (2-3, 3.72) at Cliff Lee (2-2, 3.66)
(Friday, May 6th 7:05 PM — The Comcast Network, 1210 AM)

Lowe has pitched at least six innings in the majority of his starts this season, and has allowed fewer three or fewer runs in six of his starts, but the Braves are only averaging 1.4 runs in games which Lowe starts.  In his last start against St.Louis (his first start since his DUI arrest) Lowe went five innings while allowing four runs on six hits with five strikeouts and two walks.  He lost his start against the Phillies in April, going seven innings and allowing just two runs on seven hits with only two strikeouts.  The current Phillies are batting just .197 against Lowe with 42 hits in 213 at bats, with just 9 of those hits for extra bases.  Raul Ibanez has the hot bat against Lowe with 6 hits in 40 at bats with two home runs and six RBIs with two walks.  For his career, Lowe has a 7-4 record against the Phillies with a 3.08 ERA. Lee is coming off a loss against the New York Mets in a game the Phillies offense decided not to show up.  Lee allowed eight hits and one run with two walks in seven innings of work.  In his start against Atlanta in April, Lee lasted just three and a third innings and allowed six runs on ten hits in his worst outing of 2011.  Lee has a 1-2 lifetime record against Atlanta with a 6.98 ERA in 19.1 innings of work.

GAME TWO: Julio Teheran (0-0, 0.00) at Kyle Kendrick (1-2, 2.08)

Teheran will make his Major League debut against the Phillies after a late decision to call him up for the spot start Friday afternoon.  He is ranked number five overall in Baseball America’s prospects. He began this season with the AAA Gwinnett Braves; he is 3-0 with a 1.80 ERA, and has allowed just six runs in five starts this season.  Kendrick will make his first start of the season after several relief appearances so far in 2011.  He has one relief appearance against Atlanta this season; in one and two thirds innings Kendrick allowed a hit and a walk.  The current Braves are batting .256 against Kendrick with 32 hits in 125 at bats with 11 for extra bases.  Chipper Jones has the hot bat against Kendrick with a .389 average and seven hits in 18 at bats, including three doubles and two home runs with six RBIs and three walks.

GAME THREE: Jair Jurrjens (3-0, 1.52) at Cole Hamels (4-1, 2.66)
(Sunday, May 8th 8:00 PM — ESPN, ESPN Radio, 1210 AM)

Jurrjens is having a solid 2011 for the Braves going at least six innings in each of his starts.  In his latest outing against Milwaukee, Jurrjens went seven and two thirds innings allowing just two runs on seven hits with four strikeouts and no walks.  It will be his first start against the Phillies this season.  In 59.2 innings of work, Jurrjens has a 3-3 record against the Phillies with 2.56 ERA with 35 strikeouts. Against Jurrjens, the Phillies are batting .203 with 28 hits in 138 at bats, with 10 hits for extra bases.  Hamels is coming off another stellar start; Tuesday against Washington, Hamels threw a complete game allowing just one run on five hits.  Hamels himself hit a triple and single and struck out six Nationals on the night.  Against the Braves this season, Hamels threw seven shutout innings, allowing just four hits and a walk while striking out eight batters.  The Braves have a .243 average against Hamels with 50 hits in 206 at bats, with 23 for extra bases. Brian McCann has a .600 average against Hamels with 12 hits in 20 at bats with six doubles, a home run, and 11 RBIs with five walks.

UP NEXT: The Phillies will embark on a nine game road trip where they will face the Florida Marlins, Atlanta Braves, and the St.Louis Cardinals.

Series Preview: Washington Nationals (14-14) at Philadelphia Phillies (18-9)

May 3rd

Posted by Meghan P. in Posts

1 comment

The Phillies will continue their 10 game homestand with a three game set against the Washington Nationals. It will be the first visit to Citizens Bank Park for former right fielder Jayson Werth, who signed a seven-year 126 million dollar contract with Washington last offseaon. In their first face off in April, the Phillies won the series 2-1 at Nationals Park.

GAME ONE: Livan Hernandez (3-2, 3.23) at Cole Hamels (3-1, 3.13)
(Tuesday, May 3rd 7:05 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

In his last start Hernandez allowed three runs on seven hits over eight innings against New York. He has a 10-9 record against the Phillies with a 3.60 ERA with 116 strikeouts. In his April start against Philadelphia, it was Hernandez who had the sole victory in the series going six and two-thirds innings, while allowing just one run on seven hits with six strikeouts. Hamels will make his first start against Washington during this series, after going seven innings in his last start against Arizona; he allowed three runs on four hits with eight strikeouts and a walk. He is 3-0 with a 1.55 ERA in his last four starts.The Nationals are batting .274 against Hamels with 20 hits in 73 at bats, with 13 hits for extra bases. Adam LaRoche has the hot bat against Hamels with a .333 average going four for 12 with a double and three home runs. Lifetime against Washington, Hamels has a 9-3 record with a 2.73 ERA with 125 strikeouts.

GAME TWO: Jason Marquis (3-0, 2.62) at Vance Worley (1-0, 0.00)
(Wednesday, May 4th 7:05 PM — Comcast Sports Net, 1210 AM)

Marquis was masterful in his last outing against San Francisco (and Tim Lincecum) in a complete game shutout in which he struck out seven while allowing five hits and no walks. While Marquis has yet to face the Phillies this season, he is 5-7 against them for his career with a 5.34 ERA with 56 strikeouts in 97.2 innings pitched. The current Phillies are batting just .188 against Marquis with just 32 hits in 170 at bats, just six of those hits are for extra bases. Worley was a welcome surprise in his first start of 2011 against New York. Going six shutout innings, Worley allowed just two hits with five strikeouts and four walks. He has not faced the bulk of the Nationals lineup, facing only Rick Ankiel, Ian Desmond, and Danny Espinosa, who are batting .250 against Worley. Desmond has the sole hit against Worley. In Lannan’s start against the Phillies in April he earned the loss; going six innings while allowing two runs on six hits with three walks and three strikeouts. Against Lannan the Phillies are batting .291 with 51 hits in 175 at bats, with just 14 for extra bases, including six home runs. Lannan again will be looking for his first victory against the Phillies; he is 0-9 with a 5.80 ERA with 38 strikeouts. Halladay is coming off a complete game against New York (the 60th of his career); he allowed just one run on seven hits while striking out eight. Against Washington this season, Halladay threw a complete game, in which he allowed two runs on six hits while striking out nine and walking two batters. Against Washington, Halladay has a 9-1 career record with a 1.96 ERA with 73 strikeouts in 91.2 innings pitched. The Nationals are batting .236 against Halladay with 43 hits in 182 at bats, with ten of those hits for extra bases.

Patriotic Game Recap: Mets 2, Phillies 1

May 2nd

Posted by Liz in Game Recaps

No comments


Today’s playlist:

I keep forgetting that there was a baseball game last night.  Fourteen innings, over four hours, and only three runs scored total.  It was that kind of game.

Phillies pitching continued to be fantastic, keeping the offense in the game until the end.  A combination of six pitchers allowed only two runs.  Cliff Lee went seven innings of one run ball, and was followed by Michael Stutes, Antonio Bastardo, Ryan Madson, Danys Baez, and Kyle Kendrick.  Baez went two innings and allowed just one hit and no walks.  Kendrick allowed one run on four hits in three innings of work.  Walks continue to be a bit of a problem for him, but he did yeoman’s work last night.  The loss will technically be hung on him, as he allowed the go-ahead run in the fourteenth.  That seems unfair to me.  I’m not a huge Kendrick fan, but since his extra innings blow-up last month, he’s pitched really well.  Cliff allowed one run, and Kendrick allowed one run.  The Mets had a one run lead, which was not insurmountable.  They kept the team in the game, which is their job.  Pitching did not lose this game.

The loss should be hung squarely on the shoulders of the offense.  The only Phillie with more than one hit was Brian Schneider.  Victorino, Polanco, Howard, Francisco, and Orr had one hit each.  And that’s it.  Fourteen innings, seven hits, one run.  I’m not sure which is more embarrassing, that line, or the Mets line of fourteen innings, fourteen hits, and two runs.  They’re both pretty unsightly.  And I’m not even going mention Ibanez, who couldn’t hit water if he fell out of a boat.  Ryan Howard batted in the Phillies lone run of the night, a single in the eighth.

Until the seventh inning, the game was pretty run of the mill.  In the bottom of the seventh, everything turned surreal and stayed that way until the game ended seven innings later.  The bases were loaded with Schneider at the plate.  On the 2-2 pitch, Schneider checked his swing, or so he thought.  The home plate umpire called it a ball, but an appeal was made to third base umpire Lance Barksdale.  Barksdale said Schneider went around, and called him out on strikes.   Charlie Manuel didn’t seem to like that call very much, and immediately ran on to the field to argue with Barksdale.  Of course, you can’t argue ball/strike calls, and Charlie was ejected from the game.  Between the eighth and ninth innings, the big news broke, and the announcers practically ordered everyone watching to change the channel to news.  The news spread around the stadium, with fans chanting “USA! USA! USA!”

And then, of course, my favorite moment of the game.  In the bottom of the fourteenth inning, Cole Hamels picked up a bat and stepped into the on deck circle.  With two outs, Cole stepped into the batters box.  The crowd, already significantly riled up, started chanting “COLE COLE COLE!”  He struck out to end the game, but frankly, I didn’t care.  It delighted me to no end that Cole would come into the game to pitch the fifteenth, even though there wouldn’t be a fifteenth.

Where was Ross Gload, you may ask?  According to all of the beat writers, Gload was not with the team due to the death of his grandfather.  With Oswalt still with his family in Mississippi and Chooch unavailable due to back pain, the Phillies played last night with only 22 of 25 players on their roster available.  Quite the opportune time for a game to go into extra innings, no?

Truth be told, I don’t care all that much about this particular Phillies loss.  I mean, I don’t like it when the Phillies lose, but they won the first two games of the series.  And all things considered, New York can take this one.

The Phillies have a day off today, and then they take on the Nationals.  With Oswalt not expected to rejoin the Phillies until Thursday, David Hale of the Wilmington News Journal says that Cole Hamels will take the ball on Tuesday, Vance Worley on Wednesday, and Roy Halladay on Thursday.  The off day is significant, because it means that despite the changes, no one will be pitching on short rest.
