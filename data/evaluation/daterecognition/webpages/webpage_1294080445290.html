Chrysler Recalls 40,131 Vehicles, Including 2010 Dodge Challenger and Dodge Charger

Published May 17, 2010

    4 Ratings
    Just the Facts:
    • Chrysler is recalling 40,131 2010 vehicles due to a faulty ignition switch module.
    • The defect "could result in the potential for unintended vehicle movement and could increase the risk of a crash," says NHTSA.
    • Chrysler 300, Dodge Challenger, Dodge Charger, Dodge Ram, Jeep Commander and Jeep Grand Cherokee are all on the list.

    WASHINGTON — Chrysler is recalling 40,131 2010 vehicles, including the Chrysler 300, Dodge Challenger, Dodge Charger, Dodge Ram, Jeep Commander and Jeep Grand Cherokee to fix a faulty ignition switch module. The defect "could result in the potential for unintended vehicle movement and could increase the risk of a crash," says the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration.

    "These vehicles may have been built with a wireless ignition node module exhibiting a binding condition of the solenoid latch," said NHTSA in its recalls summary of the problem. "The result of the defect could lead to a condition where the key may be removed from the ignition switch module prior to placing the shifter in park. This could result in the potential for unintended vehicle movement and could increase the risk of a crash."

    Chrysler said it is "not aware of any confirmed accidents or injuries related to this issue," in other paperwork filed with the federal government.

    Chrysler and Dodge dealers will replace the module for free. The recall is expected to begin in July. Owners may contact Chrysler at 800-853-1403.

    Inside Line says: If you own one of the aforementioned 2010 Chrysler vehicles, have it checked out ASAP with your dealer. — Anita Lienert, Correspondent
