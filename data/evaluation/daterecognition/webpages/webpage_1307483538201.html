Bush Tax Cut Offer Favors Higher Earnings

January 21st, 2011 by News World Report

Protection of the Bush tax cut deal proposed by the Obama administration this week has centers on tax cuts for the rich. But there’s something for everyone in the mix. The greater you make the greater you save is how it works. Ironically, the package turns out to be a form of government stimulus derided by Republicans as they prepare to assume greater power in Congress. With any luck, if all goes as planned the Bush tax cuts can keep people from being forced to apply for a pay day loan.

The Bush tax cuts package

The Bush tax cut deal will cost the federal government nearly $900 billion dollars over the next 2 years. Individuals who live comfortable without the additional cash can be getting g a lot of the cash. The benefits will roll into 2011 and will benefit the middle class. Some of the benefits consist of a tuition tax credit, a federal joblessness extension, a payroll tax holiday and a child tax credit. Ending the Bush tax credits would mean that the middle class taxpayers would be bringing home less to their families each month. About $200 less a paycheck would be taken home. Americans in the lower-income would have seen a payroll tax. It would have gone up to 15 percent from the 10 percent it is now.

A lot more tax cuts for the rich

Everyone benefits from the Bush tax cut deal. It would help somehow anyway. The wealthy get the majority of the benefits though. The payroll tax holiday takes 2 percentage points off the current 6.2 percent taken out of salaries. Someone making $100,000 would get a tax cut ten times bigger than somebody making $10,000. Census data shows the average United States family earns about $52,000 a year. About $1,180 could be saved by the Bush tax cut extension for average homes in 2011. That is 2.3 percent of income being saved. Families earning from $200,000 to $500,000 would save about $7,500. About 6.2 percent of income could be saved by any person making $1 million or more which is about $129,000.

The cash is invested too

Broken down into Republican and Democratic wishes, the Bush tax cut package favors the latter. Tax cuts for the rich will cost $120 billion. That is just out of the $900 billion. Tax breaks suggested by the Obama administration take $450 billion from the total. The middle class would get about $360 billion for the Bush tax cut extensions. The idea is likes by economists. They think it is brilliant. There has also been a revision of estimates for economic growth and employment. This was shown on the news. Research groups in Washington are pretty looking forward to the Bush tax cut deal. They say a lot more jobs can be created with the bill than anything else.
