Friday, March 18, 2011

Montana Budget Bill Would Decline Federal Meaningful Use Funding

Montana Gov. Brian Schweitzer (D) is calling on state lawmakers to adjust a GOP-led budget package (HB 2) to allow the state to accept federal funding from the meaningful use incentive payment program, the AP/Helena Independent Record reports.

Under the 2009 economic stimulus package, health care providers who demonstrate meaningful use of certified electronic health records can qualify for Medicaid and Medicare incentive payments (AP/Helena Independent Record, 3/17).

Details of the Bill

Montana's spending package, which passed the state House on March 14, would deny authority for the state Department of Public Health and Human Services to accept and distribute meaningful use funds. Montana is the only state to take such action, Healthcare IT News reports.

Arguments For, Against Funding

Montana health care providers would be eligible for roughly $35 million in meaningful use payments (Merrill, Healthcare IT News, 3/17). According to the governor, the meaningful use funding would create jobs and reduce waste in health care.

Republicans said that EHRs have not been tested thoroughly and could spawn privacy worries.

According to Rep. Don Roberts (R-Billings), an oral surgeon, widespread access to patient data could compromise patient privacy (AP/Helena Independent Record, 3/17).

Next Steps

A state Senate committee is slated to review the budget package on Friday (Healthcare IT News, 3/17).

It is unlikely the measure will be finalized until later in April.

Schweitzer has not ruled out a veto of the legislation (AP/Helena Independent Record, 3/17).
