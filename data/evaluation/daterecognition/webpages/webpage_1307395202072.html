Redding crews ready for storms

  • By Record Searchlight staff
  • Record Searchlight
  • Posted January 15, 2010 at 3:36 p.m.
  • Email
  • Discuss
  • Share »
    • Digg
    • Facebook
    • LinkedIn
    • Tweet this
  • Print
  • A
  • A
  • A

With at least a week of wet and windy weather forecast to begin Sunday, city of Redding officials say crews are ready to provide sand bags, clear storm drains and repair power lines.

Sand bags will be available 24 hours a day at Fire Station 4 on South Bonnyview Road and at the city corporation yard on Viking Way off Old Oregon Trail, north of Highway 44. Residents must fill the bags and limit themselves to 25.

Officials are urging residents to avoid downed power lines. Call 245-7000 to report a snapped line.

City officials are also asking motorists to watch out for localized street flooding during downpours. Ponds may be deeper than they look, stalling motorists.

Residents should clear storm drains of leaves and debris to avoid flooding. Call 224-6068 to report a clogged drain.

Residents should also use battery-powered flashlights and lanterns rather than candles during any power outages, keep portable generators and barbecues outside, avoid connecting generators to household plugs and approach blacked out traffic lights with caution, officials say.
