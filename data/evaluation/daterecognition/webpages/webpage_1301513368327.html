Freedom for Indian if poppy 'does not grow'

By
  • Eman Al Baik
Published Tuesday, March 08, 2011

Picture only for illustrative purpose (FILE)

An Indian passenger who arrived from the UK on his way to India is still behind bars as the court is yet to determine if the poopy seeds he possessed were chemically treated or not.

The Dubai Court of Appeal has ordered an investigation to find if the poppy seeds confiscated from FR are growable.

He was sentenced by Court of First Instance to 10 years in jail followed by deportation for smuggling and possessing growable poppy seeds.

FR said he had bought the seeds from a well-known company that sells branded spices to take home to his mother for cooking purposes.

His lawyer Saeed Al Ghailani told the court. “It is very well known that the UK is a country that fights drugs. So how do you expect it to sell growable poppy seeds?

The lawyer defended by saying that he suspected the authenticity of the criminal evidences report because the pack had clear mention of the content and it read that the product is chemically treated. “This means that the seeds have been exposed to high temperatures and contained added preservatives. This clearly means they are not growable. The Criminal laboratory cannot be an agricultural expert,” Al Ghailani told the court.

“My client who has been living in the UK as a programmer was not hiding the 48.8gms of poopy seeds. It was clearly visible in his luggage. I ask the court to acquit my client as the accusation is baseless,” defended Al Ghailani.

The court adjourned the case until next week to hear the criminal evidences’ expert to clarify the contradiction of the statements in the report and that mentioned on nthe pack, which proves the seeds were chemically treated and not growable.

The Court of First Instance based its verdict on the criminal evidence report which said that the confiscated poppy seeds were growable.
