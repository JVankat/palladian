A Production Proposal

    "The Evolution Movement" is part two of a multi-media theatrical experience that delivers an innovative vision for the future of mankind. This vision is inspired by the true story of the discovery of a miraculous new source of energy. This energy is presented, and the new world that comes with it, by "surfing the web” of collective consciousness. Using a mix of media, scientific fact, dance, soliloquy and cultural touchstones this 60 minute project has the objective to raise awareness about free energy. 

 

    I am a professional theatrical lighting designer developing my artistic voice and original work around the evolution of consciousness and man’s untapped potential. I am inspired to combine the magic of the theatre with the mystery of man’s aptitude to create. The living mystery of the imagination and its role in creating change began to develop as a philosophy for me in 2004 as I embarked on a voyage to discover what might be the human agenda. This journey has distanced me from cultural, political and religious beliefs even as I understand deeply how these things have shaped our world. I have come to believe that man’s potential is undermined by these cultural and common belief systems. Limiting our consideration limits our imagination.

    This production aspires to manifest thoughtful changes to our collective consciousness, and our manifest reality, by working to establish a heart-coherent wave in part one and introduce a vision for the future in part two. There is distinct evidence that sustained emotion, coupled with an intention can enact change to our inner and outer environments. This means we can manifest change by feeling good and thinking good thoughts about a good idea. 


    I produced part one, “The Language of Light” in 2008. This theatrical dance production incorporated innovative techniques involving sound, light and vibration to create what Noetic science calls “a heart-coherent state of appreciation” in the audience. It has been found that when large numbers of people intentionally create heart-coherent states of care, love, compassion and appreciation they will generate a consistent standing wave that can offset the current planetary-wide wave of stress, discord, fear and incoherence. “The Language of Light” provides a good feeling by sharing through popular music and dance; the energy of mans “light within”. We explore what animates man’s spirit through dance and using the concept of the seven Chakra energy centers.  The end of part one culminates in the creation of our heroine, a human named Astrea.


    Part two; “The Evolution Movement” provides the vision and an intention. Astrea discovers, in a true ‘Dan Brown’ style of sourcing unusual materials, a miraculous new energy that is produced through the harmonic mixing of simple ingredients to create a “heartbeat” of unending, clean energy. The discovery of the heartbeat energy means that we will rebalance our waters, our air, our planet and our blood while providing a free, clean, unending source of power for everyone. Securing a healthy global future requires that we preserve freedom of spirit, and that technology and values are based on scientific guiding principles without prejudice. “The Evolution Movement” shows that these scientific guiding principles also have a dimension of spirit that animates them. The heartbeat energy is created by the same means that the heartbeat sparks to life inside each human being. This is energy that requires a certain PH and a certain harmonic to come alive. The story of this new energy hopes to serve as a catalyst for expanding man’s understanding of a magic that may be available to all.


    “The Evolution Movement” is set in an office/laboratory and becomes a theatrical interpretation of “surfing the web”. The story is told through a series of vignettes, video used as backdrops to dancers, audio speakers installed in Harlequin costumes and the use of a few cultural icons as commentators (in their own words and voice). There are three-dimensional projection surfaces and many areas on the stage/office that lighting will be have an interesting and mystical effect.  Exploring the creases of the Internet and counter-culture subjects such as The New World Order, The Mayan Calendar, Illuminati, and Time Wave Zero, we begin to understand our own reflection, our sovereign hearts and the truth of what we give our attention to can become real.


            The dancers/speakers tell us the story of the energy through a type work I am calling “re-combining”. This work takes aspects of different speeches, songs and visual images and re-combines them, putting new emphasis on the message. A vision for the future and lessons from the past are explored using the words of Terence McKenna, John Lennon and Martin Luther King among others.  As an artist I enjoy spectacle, deep color, heartfelt messages and rock-n-roll in my work. My biggest challenge in “The Evolution Movement” is to present the content in a fluent, fast, understandable manner that intrigues while it informs. I would like to work with a director, a video production artist, and a choreographer to help me shape the content and delivery of this performance.


            “The Language of Light” was my first original theatre work, featuring 57 dancers from all levels of learning and resulted in a beautifully realized production of considerable scope. The second part allows me to thread my voice and story into a smaller more intimate work using the stage, still to create interesting pictures and surprising moments, but also to tell a good story without the use of traditional storytelling methods. The second part has fewer scenic and costume requirements and will depend more on video projection and dialogue between actors. Expectation is to produce next fall in Aspen, CO and then to tour the production to two other Colorado cities.


    I am an independent artist that has many resources and physical backing for my work. I live in an area that supports the idea of sustainable energy and innovative thinking. I propose to do this work as soon as possible as I believe it is timely and necessary for the next step of our evolution. This production is very human-centric, contrasting with institutional and cultural modes of thinking. This experience of reality and its inhibiting parameters is the enemy of our transcendence as evolving beings. I believe that without a vision, without a map, we may continue to wander in the darkness and never find our way to create the new world.
