Libya
Still current at: 30 March 2011
Updated: 29 March 2011


This advice has been reviewed and reissued with amendments to the Travel Summary and the Safety and Security - Political Situation section (update on areas where clashes are occurring). The overall level of the advice has not changed. We advise against all travel to Libya. We advise British nationals in Libya to leave.

Travel Summary

  • We advise against all travel to Libya.
  • In accordance with UNSCR 1973, Allied forces began airstrikes on specific targets in Libya on Saturday 19 March.  There were further airstrikes on the evenings of Sunday 20 March through to Sunday 27 March including in and near Tripoli, Benghazi, Ajdabiya, Sebha, Zuwara, Zawiya, Sirte and Misurata, which may continue.  We urge any remaining British nationals in Libya to leave as soon as they can safely do so. You should not rely on further assisted evacuations.
  • For British, including dual nationals, remaining in Libya, we recommend that they keep a low profile and be aware of the possibility of retaliatory measures from pro-Qadhafi forces or groups as a result of the international intervention.  You should stay indoors wherever possible and avoid political gatherings and demonstrations.  If you are not in Tripoli, do not travel there.
  • There have been armed clashes involving pro-Qadhafi and opposition forces in many parts of Libya, including around Ras Lanouf, Sirte, Misurata, Zintan, Zawiyah, Bin Jawad, Burayqah, and Ajdabiya and reports of gunfire in the capital, Tripoli.  Clashes are continuing, particularly in and around Bin Jawad, Sirte, Misurata and Zintan.  The situation across Libya is unpredictable and areas of conflict can change rapidly.  Areas that have previously avoided conflict can also quickly destabilise.
  • There have been a number of reports of foreigners (including journalists) being harassed, arrested and detained in Libya, particularly in government-controlled areas.
  • We believe that the vast majority of British nationals who want to leave have now left Libya, through commercial means, Government charters and military evacuations. However, the FCO in London, and British Embassies and High Commissions in the region, continue to offer advice to British nationals in Libya, including those wishing to leave.  
  • We advise British nationals in Libya to register with us by calling +44 (0)20 7008 5395 between 08:30 and 18:00 (UK time) Monday to Friday so we can advise them of any exit options that arise. Outside of these hours British nationals requiring assistance should call  +44 (0)20 7008 1500.
  • In light of the deteriorating situation in Libya we have temporarily suspended the operations of the British Embassy in Tripoli. The Turkish Government will now temporarily represent British consular interests in Libya.  The Turkish Embassy in Tripoli can be contacted on: +218 21 340 11 40 / 43 / 44.
  • If you have already left Libya, or you know of British nationals who have recently departed Libya by other means, please call us on +44 (0)20 7008 5395 so we know you are safe and do not need to make further enquiries.

    Options for leaving Libya:

         By Air:
  • Following UN Security Council Resolution 1973 a No Fly Zone is in force over Libya.  Libyan airspace is closed to all traffic and all flights out of Libya are suspended.

    By Sea     :
  • There are no confirmed scheduled boats from any ports in Libya. 
  • Shipping options can arise at short notice. We advise British nationals in Libya to register with us by calling +44 (0)20 7008 5395 between 08:30 and 18:00 (UK time) Monday to Friday so we can advise them of any exit options that arise.

    By Land:
  • We understand that roads to the Tunisian and Egyptian land borders are open. We have had reports of violent incidents along these routes so proceed with extreme caution. These routes may close in the near future.

    Travel Documents:
  • Proof of British nationality: British nationals requiring an Emergency Travel Document should call us on +44 (0) 20 7008 5395.
  • Dependents of British Nationals - Visa Waivers 
  • Urgent humanitarian, compelling and compassionate cases for visa waivers for the dependents of British nationals in Libya will be considered by UKBA. Contact us on +44 (0)20 7008 5395. 
  • All other applicants should apply at the designated visa posts of Rome and Istanbul and usual rules will apply.  For more information visit the Worldbridge Service website or call 39-02-4070-8218 (Rome) or 0212 340 4472 (Istanbul).  Please note these calls will cost US$14.

     Onward flights departing Malta

  • British nationals who have arrived in Malta from Libya wishing to travel on to the UK should call the British High Commission in Malta on 00 356 23232411/2.
