UPDATE: 12 arrested outside Lebanon Valley Speedway

July 22, 2010 6:38 AM
Chris White

WEST LEBANON -- Seven people were arrested Monday night during the three-day monster truck event at Lebanon Valley Speedway, according to state police.

James Presz, 27, and Jesse Bolio, 20, both of Pittsfield, Mass., were charged with Unlawful Possession of Marihuana following a traffic stop on Route 20.

Nicholas LeClair, 22, of Pittsfield, Mass. has been charged with Criminal Possession of a Controlled Substance (oxycontin) in addition to pot possession. Patrick Warger, 21, of Pittsfield, Mass. is charged with Criminal Possession of a Controlled Substance (suboxone) along with pot possession.

Kimberly Bucier, 25, of Pittsfield, Mass. was arrested for Driving While Intoxicated and Felony Driving While Intoxicated under Leandra's Law after leaving the scene of an accident with a 5-year-old in the car. Bucier had a BAC of .12%, police said.

Michael Sullivan, 26, of Pittsfield, Mass. was arrested for Driving While Intoxicated after a traffic stop on Route 20. Sullivan had a .14% BAC, police said.

Matthew Wichmann, 34, of Lenox, Mass. was arrested for Driving While Intoxicated. Wichmann had a .16% BAC -- twice the legal limit, according to police. 

 

Another five were arrested Tuesday night.

Three people were arrested for Unlawful Possession of Marijuana: Justin Randall, 28, of Becket, Mass., Nathan Danford, 21, of Hinsdale, Mass. and Arthur Lewis, Jr., 49, of Becket, Mass.

Jaimie Garcia, 19, and Jessica Garcia, 21, both of Ghent, were arrested for Disorderly Conduct and Resisting Arrest. Police said the two sisters got into a fight in the parking lot of the Lebanon Valley Speedway.

Powers Humphrey, 68, of North Chatham, was arrested for DWI after he struck another vehicle from behind in the Speedway parking lot, then continued driving on Route 20, police said. Humphrey had at a .16% BAC.

Shane Holden, 19, of Pittsfield, Mass. was arrested for DWI following a traffic stop on Route 20 where he had a .10% BAC, police said.
