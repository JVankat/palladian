    Man gets life for 1990 rape, kidnapping of UF student

    Joe Byrnes/Staff Photographer
    Buy photo
    Kelvin Miles, left, sits with his attorney, Joshua Silverman, during a hearing in circuit court in Gainesville on Wednesday.

    By Joe Byrnes
    Staff writer

    Published: Wednesday, December 16, 2009 at 5:52 p.m.
    Last Modified: Wednesday, December 16, 2009 at 5:52 p.m.

    Kelvin Miles, who has a long list of violent felonies to his name, was sentenced Wednesday to two consecutive life term for the armed rape and kidnapping of a University of Florida sorority member nearly 20 years ago.

    A jury last month found Miles, 48, guilty, years after he was linked to the crimes through a DNA match in a state database.

    The rape occurred on the morning of Jan. 13, 1990. The UF student was opening the door to her car outside her sorority house on Panhellenic Drive when the attacker, armed with an 8- to 10-inch knife, forced his way into the car. After binding, blindfolding and raping the young woman, he left her at another location tied up with tape over her mouth and trapped on the floor of her car.

    Circuit Judge Ysleta W. McDonald cited the injuries to the victim, who testified Wednesday, and the rapist's extensive criminal history as reasons for exceeding the sentencing guidelines of 17 to 40 years in prison. Prosecutors had sought but failed to label Miles a habitual offender to prevent his leaving prison after a few years because of the gain time allowed under Florida laws back in 1990, which could have allowed a prisoner sentenced to 40 years to leave prison after nine.

    Assistant State Attorney Bill Ezzell said the life sentences got around that issue of prison gain time.

    "He should now - for lack of a better term - he should die in prison," Ezzell said after the hearing. That, he added, was the only just sentence.

    "I think that the only way I could sufficiently put this," Judge McDonald said, "is that I would find it absolutely inappropriate for me to do less than anything to try to keep you behind bars forever. I think you do not deserve to be out in our community or anyone's community. ... I don't know if that does anything with gain time, but it's certainly all that is in my power to do."

    Miles did not speak in court to express remorse because he is appealing the convictions, said his attorney, Joshua Silverman. The attorney argued for a sentence less than the guidelines, citing Miles' clean record while in prison for 19 years, describing the rape as an unpremeditated "crime of opportunity," pointing out that Florida is now much stricter with sex offenders, and noting the state's delay of several years in matching Miles' DNA to the crime and bringing it to trial.

    "There's no reason to believe that Mr. Miles cannot be rehabilitated," the defense attorney said. "He has been in prison a very long time." Silverman said Miles previously was slated to leave prison in 2012. The Department of Corrections Web site gave a release date of Sept. 18, 2011.

    From the witness stand, her voice cracking with emotion, the victim told of years of emotional distress caused by the rape. She married the man she had been dating at the time, she said, and though that marriage has lasted, the rape hurt the relationship and hindered trust.

    "It made me feel like I was in a position of depending on somebody," she said. "I wondered if anybody would be interested in hanging around and having to deal with the mess that came along with me."

    Though she sought counseling off and on, she did not often share her distress and still hasn't told her children about the rape, she said.

    "I immediately tried to protect the people I cared about from having to feel the things I felt," she said.

    The rape made her fearful sometimes and guarded and interfered with her ability to be intimate, she said.

    "It's not the kind of thing you want to have to say to your husband, that you think about something awful when he touches you," the rape victim said. The crime haunts her still in the form of troubled dreams, sleepless nights and unreasonable fears.
