Witness Describes Slain Journalist's Final Moments

Posted: 12:11 pm PDT March 22, 2011

OAKLAND -- An elderly Oakland man testified Tuesday that he saw a man wearing a black jacket and a black hat shoot journalist Chauncey Bailey three times in downtown Oakland the morning of Aug. 2, 2007.

Speaking through a Cantonese interpreter, the man said he was standing outside the Hong Fook Adult Day Health Care Center at 275 14th St. at about 7:10 a.m. that day because he was waiting for its doors to open so he could work at his job as a caretaker there.

The witness, who asked that his name not be disclosed, said he saw the man in dark clothing shoot Bailey twice from a range of about three feet near the corner of 14th and Alice streets. Afterward, the shooter took a few steps away, then came back and shot Bailey once more, he testified.

The shooter then ran to a white van parked nearby and got into the front passenger seat, and the van drove away, the witness said.

The elderly man was the second witness in the trial of former Your Black Muslim Bakery leader Yusuf Bey IV and associate Antoine Mackey, both 25, who are charged with three counts of murder for the deaths of Bailey and two other men in Oakland in the summer of 2007.

The man said he couldn't identify the shooter because he is nearsighted and the shooter's face was hidden by his hat.

Former bakery handyman Devaughndre Broussard, now 23, confessed in 2009 that he was the person who shot and killed Bailey, 57, who was the editor of the Oakland Post, but said Bey had ordered him to carry out the shooting because Bey wanted to stop Bailey from publishing an article about the bakery's financial problems.

The bakery was undergoing bankruptcy proceedings at the time and closed its door permanently later in 2007.

Broussard pleaded guilty in 2009 to two counts of voluntary manslaughter for fatally shooting both Bailey and another victim in the case and was promised a 25-year state prison term in exchange for his testimony against Bey and Mackey.

According to police reports and testimony at previous hearings in the case, Bailey was shot as he was walking from his apartment near Lake Merritt to the Post's office on 14th Street near Franklin Street.

Bailey had just had breakfast at the McDonald's restaurant at 14th and Jackson streets when he was shot a block away near 14th and Alice streets.
