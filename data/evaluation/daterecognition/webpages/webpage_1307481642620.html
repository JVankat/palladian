What is a Star Note?
A star note is a United States bank note that has an asterisk (*), or star, after the serial number.
The Bureau of Engraving & Printing inspects currency for printing errors prior to releasing notes into general circulation.
When notes are discovered that have been printed incorrectly (such as having the serial numbers upside down, etc.),
misprinted "error notes" are replaced with star notes because no two bills within a certain series can be produced with the same serial #.
They are used to maintain a correct count of notes in a serial number run.
By their nature, star notes are more scarce than notes with standard serial numbers and as such are widely collected by hobbyists.
