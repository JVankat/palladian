Postcard from the Edge
gregWRUBELL
June 7th, 2010 @ 10:27pm
Follow Cougar Tracks | Twitter: @gregwrubell | Facebook: Cougar Tracks

Well, I have to say I've done a great job of totally ignoring work and actually taking a real vacation--no blog entries in 10 days. I thought I would be magnetically drawn to the keyboard by some mystical force that has had me in its grip for the last three years, but it's amazing what true relaxation can do for you, and Hawai'i is about as relaxing as it gets.

*******

Last week, the local sports columnists here were fully expecting the University of Hawaii's WAC "nemesis" to be bolting the conference by today, but as we all know, Boise State is now on the Mountain West Conference's back burner, for the time being.

The MWC's decision to forgo a decision on expansion makes perfect sense given the current cross-country climate. If it may only be a matter a couple of weeks, why not let a few cards get played elsewhere before determining whether the MWC might be part of a bigger picture shake-up.

Notre Dame and/or Missouri and Nebraska may make decisions that set in motion a multi-conference free-for-all, just as it's possible that bluffs get called and practically everyone stays put. As WAC Commissioner Karl Benson put it: "The poker playing that is going on I think is unprecedented."

*******

I ran into former BYU DB Scott Johnson and his family here on the beach last week, and I've spotted numerous BYU shirts and hats on my early morning runs and other travels.

*******

For those who have been to Hawai'i, you might be interested to know that the venerable Honolulu Advertiser published its final edition on Sunday. The Advertiser has been bought out by and merged with the Honolulu Star-Bulletin, creating the new Honolulu Star-Advertiser, which published its first edition today.

In this era of newspaper close-downs, it bears noting that KSL's print media partner the Deseret News is one of few newspapers actually growing and thriving as both a print and online entity.

*******

Numbers of the day: 3.2 and 9.8

The average number of wins for the University of Washington and BYU, respectively, over the last five seasons.

*******

Did you know?: when BYU plays at Florida State on September 18th, the Cougars will be facing a Mark Stoops-coached defense for the fourth time in five years.

As defensive coordinator at Arizona, Stoops and his defensive units held BYU to 13 points in 2006 (UA win), 20 points in 2007 (BYU win) and 21 points in 2008 (UA win). Stoops left his brother Mike's staff in Tucson after last season to join FSU as Jimbo Fisher's new DC.

By the way, BYU scored 14 points in beating Bob Stoops' Oklahoma Sooners last season, so it seems that Stoops= stops when the Cougars are involved.

*******

I'm pumped up for the World Cup starting at the end of the week. Soccer fans are already well-acquainted with Nike's epic pre-World Cup spot, and if you kind of like the music accompanying the commercial, then you have to check out the original version of the tune.

I must admit that I wasn't too up-to-speed on my early 70s Dutch rock, so the band "Focus" and their song "Hocus Pocus" (of course) were news to me. But now that I've heard the tune, it has grown on me, and I'll be iTuning it once I get back to the mainland. If you like a killer guitar riff combined with yodeling, yammering and some rock flute, then "Hocus Pocus" will be right in your wheel-house. You can thank me later.

Aloha!

*******
