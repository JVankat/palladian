Milwaukee County

Walker favors successor without any baggage

By Steve Schultze of the Journal Sentinel

Nov. 12, 2010 |(30) Comments

Related Coverage
  • Walker vetoes $7.3 million in tax-levy spending from budget
  • Walker favors successor without any baggage

Milwaukee County Executive and Governor-elect Scott Walker said Friday he doubted that Tom Ament would follow him into the job Ament gave up in 2002 during the heat of the county pension scandal.

"It's an interesting story, but I don't think there's any real probability that he's going to return to this position," Walker told reporters.

Someone outside of politics would make the best choice as interim county executive, Walker said Friday.

He said he liked the example set by the appointment of Janine Geske, a former state Supreme Court justice - somebody without any "political baggage" - as interim county executive after Ament quit.

Walker made the comments in response to a Journal Sentinel story that said Ament had made overtures about winning back the job he quit nearly nine years ago rather than face a likely recall. Ament also mused about how he'd like the chance to restore his reputation by getting his old job back.

County Board Chairman Lee Holloway takes over as county executive when Walker quits at the end of the year and will make the interim executive choice. That person also must be confirmed by the County Board. A special election to fill out the remainder of Walker's term is expected to be held in the spring.

Holloway said he hadn't decided whom he will appoint.

"I'm not ruling him out or ruling anybody in," Holloway said. He has previously said he had someone in mind for the appointment who wasn't involved in government.

Ament has maintained a campaign account with $450,000, which likely would make him the best-funded of a group of potential candidates for county executive in the special election.
