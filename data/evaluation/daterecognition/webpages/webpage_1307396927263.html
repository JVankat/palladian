Apple To Recognize iPhone, Apple TV Revenue Over 24 Month Period; FY Q3 Margins Seen Slipping

By Eric Savitz

Apple (AAPL) plans to recognize revenue for iPhone and AppleTV sales gradually over a 24 month period, reflecting plans to gradually roll out new software products for both products free to customers, Apple CFO Peter Oppenheimer said on a conference call with analysts following its well-received March quarter results.

The company will recognize cash on sale, but revenue and costs will be recognized on a straight line basis over 24 months; the company will record deferred revenue on its balance sheet and then recognize it over time. The company also said it will recognize payments from AT&T (T) as received, but declined to give any details on what kind of payments those might be.

A couple of notes on the March quarter:

  • Macs were 56% of quarterly revenue; 36% year-over-year growth. Notebooks were up 79% YOY, with 59% of total Macs sold. Mac channel inventory was 3-4 weeks.
  • iPods and other music products were 44% of revenue; up 24% year-over-year.
  • The higher than expected margins were tied to a big decline in memory pricing.
  • Cash was up over $700 million to $12.6 billion.

On the June quarter outlook:

  • The company sees gross margin retreating to 32% in the quarter, with a rebound in commodity pricing, a recent repricing of the company’s displays, and expected mix shift to more lower margin products with higher sales to the K-12 market.

The company said it still sees long-term gross margins at 27%-28%, and that recent higher gross margins are not sustainable.

The company declined to give any sales data on the new AppleTV product.

After hours, Apple is up $5.44 at $100.79.
