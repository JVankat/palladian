Going public: One of the many ways to save earth
With inflation rate and pollution going up by the day, we must adopt some of the ways that can make life easy for the common man. Use of public transport as an alternative to the personal vehicles may prove to be an effective step.
CJ: Pratibha Prashar
 
Fri, Jun 27, 2008 20:15:40 IST
Views:
1326
   Comments:
0
Rate:  5.0 / 5 votes
Global News Online : 

"PLANET EARTH is going to end by 2012," this is the news being displayed by various TV channels these days in a very interesting manner. Some of the astrologers have even given a specific time for this to happen. If true, that means all of us have less than only four years left of our lives. I do not know how far this statement is correct but one very true fact is that earth is definitely heading towards disaster. There is no denying the fact that we have done more than sufficient amount of damage to mother earth and are still doing it.

Another current issue worldwide that affects our lives very strongly is the increasing inflation rate. One of the root causes of this is the continuously increasing price of petroleum products. But I do not think that any government has significant control over this issue. The fact is that we are depleting the reservoir of this non-renewable natural resource at an unusually high speed. Non-renewable refers to those natural resources, which cannot be regenerated like the renewable sources like water. It means once lost, we will never be able to get it again.

It seems to be really tough to live a peaceful life.

One method that may prove to be an effective tool to achieve the twin goals of controlling the unlimited use of petroleum products along with slowing down the speed of environmental degradation is a strong public transport system.

Recently, there was a news that Malaysian people have started a ’going public’ campaign as a measure to save fuel. People have either started walking or have opted for public transport rather than using their personal vehicles.

We, in India, are in dire need of such a system. Concentrating more on using public transport may prove to be a very successful method to achieve multiple targets with a single effort.

First and foremost, it will cut down the consumption of petrol and diesel by a significant amount. Similarly, it will reduce the amount of air pollution being caused by the harmful vehicular emissions. At the same time, it will also help in tackling the traffic menace as the number of private vehicles running on the roads would come down.

We do not have a strong and comfortable public transport system except in a few metros like Mumbai and Kolkata or some state capitals. In Mumbai, more than 88 per cent of the people use public transport -- either suburban trains or buses.

Kolkata, too, has a good transport system in the form of Metro train and local buses. In Delhi, more than 60 per cent people use public transport to travel. But the point is that the facility is almost nil in other big cities and towns. Even in the National Capital Region (NCR), the condition is not very impressive. As per the 2001 census, there are more than 35 cities in India with a population of more than a million and three of them have a population in excess of 10 million of it and keeping in mind the rate of population growth, this number is definitely going to increase. So we need a very robust and efficient method to provide mobility to the population.

Effective steps are required to improve the system so that people may get attracted to use of public transport rather than their personal vehicles.

Some of the steps that can be followed to make the situation better may be as follows:

  • The number of suburban buses and trains must be increased so that their frequency of running gets increased.
  • Maintenance of buses and trains (hygiene, wear and tear, capacity, etc) must be ensured so that people, especially the white collared workers, who generally avoid the public transport due to the inconvenience being caused by the huge rush and uncivil behaviour of the passengers, etc, may get motivated to use of public transport.
  • Strict law and order must be maintained to avoid anti-social elements and prevent any kind of criminal activities inside buses and trains.
  • Railway stations and bus stands (bus stops) need to be maintained and looked after properly.
  • Schools and colleges must arranged for strong transport so that the use of personal vehicles by staff and students can be restricted. Other organisations should also follow this.
  • Not only the Metro cities and the NCR but all state capitals and other important cities must also be supported with a strong public transport system.
  • In India, especially north India, people have a mindset that using public transport speaks low of one’s social status. This mindset has to be changed. Improving the public transport system may help in achieving this.
  • Public transport should prove to be a cheap, convenient and efficient alternative to personal vehicles rather than a struggle to get into a bus or train and a fearful ride to your destination.
  • We personally must try to use public transport as an alternative to personal vehicles. It may prove to be an effective way to cut down the transportation expenses.

Though all this will put extra financial burden on the government initially but once established, it will prove to be a good source of income to the state and Central government.

Government has taken some steps in this direction like the Delhi Metro, introduction of high capacity buses in Delhi, etc. But much more emphasis must be given on this issue and more such efforts are required in all parts of country to improve the present scenario.

Keeping in mind the rapid population growth rate, we must not forget that there is a limit up to which the roads can accommodate our cars and scooters. Next time try to catch a bus or a local train rather than going by your car or two-wheeler. Your pocket and nature, both will be at advantage.
