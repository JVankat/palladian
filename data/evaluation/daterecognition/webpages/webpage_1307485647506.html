Alfred, Ethan Henry

June 16, 2010
The Journal
Save |

NEW ULM - Ethan Henry Alfred, the infant son of Dean and Kathryn Alfred of New Ulm died Sunday, June 13, 2010 at the Children's Hospital in Minneapolis.

Funeral service will be 11 a.m. Thursday, June 17 atChrist the King Lutheran Church in New Ulm with Pastor Timothy Anderson officiating.

Visitation will be 3 - 7 p.m. Wednesday and 7:30 - 8:30 a.m. Thursday at the Minnesota Valley Funeral Home in New Ulm and will continue for one hour prior to the time of service at church on Thursday.

To leave an online condolence for the family or to sign the guest book go to www.mvfh.org.

Ethan is survived by his parents, Dean and Kathryn Alfred of New Ulm; a sister, Karlie Raymond of New Ulm; brothers, Jack Raymond and Ben Alfred both of New Ulm; grandparents, Harlyn Hanson of Hanska, Linda Hanson of New Ulm, Melvin and Dianne Alfred of New Ulm; great-grandparents, Linus and Angeline Krzmarzick of Sleepy Eye; aunts anduncles, Jon Hansonof Hanska,Quinten Hanson of Hanska, Laura and Robert Zapata of San Antonio, Texas, Ann and Baburaj Vellicolungara of Eagan, Rick and Carolee Alfred of New Ulm, Randy and Cindy Alfred of New Ulm; and by many cousins.

He was preceded in death by his great-grandparents, Harold and Sylvia Hanson, Percy and Irene Alfred, Joseph and Ardis Evers.

Ethan Henry Alfred was born Aug. 22, 2008 in New Ulm to Dean and Kathryn (Hanson) Alfred.

Ethan was faced with challenges from birth, being hospitalized all but 10 weeks of his life. Ethan was a warrior, courageous and strong overcoming many obstacles until he grew tired and is now at peace with the Lord.

Ethan managed to find moments of joy in a life filled with tremendous pain.

Ethan loved to be held by his caregivers, especially his mother. He loved his wet washcloth, his lullabies and just resting in his crib.

He will be dearly missed, cherished and forever loved.

In lieu of flowers, the family prefers memorials to the Optimist Club of New Ulm, Tim Orth Memorial Foundation, New Ulm Jaycees, and the Hanska Women of Today and Christ the King Lutheran Church.
