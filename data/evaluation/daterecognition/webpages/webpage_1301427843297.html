Mobile Stations Cut Cost Of Electric Car Charging Stations

By Jesse Emspak | September 9, 2010 5:07 PM EDT

A group at the Mitsubishi Electric Research Lab has proposed portable charging stations to offer electric vehicles to cut the cost of building infrastructure.

Zafer Sahinoglu, senior principal member of the research staff and his colleagues designed a system in which a high capacity battery - probably a big lithium ion or zinc-based cell - would be charged at a certain location at night. Since that is off-peak, the price for the electricity is less.

The battery could be loaded on a truck, and carted to a location where it is needed. Electric vehicles could pull up and charge just as they would at a conventional charging station.

The big advantage is cost, Sahinoglu says. The problem with current plans for charging stations is that they need to be built, just like gas stations do. There is also the associated infrastructure for the station such as power hookups. That costs money - sometimes hundreds of thousands of dollars.

On top of that, the most likely time people would use them is during the day, when the load on the local utility is already highest and power prices go up. "We could buy the power at the cheapest rate during off-peak hours, and still sell it during the day at a lower cost," Sahinoglu says. Charging up the batteries at night also evens the load on the utility.

An added benefit is that cars could even wirelessly transmit their battery levels to a central location, which could tell a battery provider where the need for chargers is greatest. That allows for smarter deployment - there is no need to send charging stations to places they aren't needed.
