THE ZILLIONS � Play! Zeuxis: Xight? Zeen...
Wednesday, 29 October 2008

(Speak & Spell/Inertia)

Putting the sophistication back into Australian rock. Thank God.

The Zillions are the current vehicle for Sidewinder founder Nick Craft. Play! is menagerie of introverted Australian rock and pop influences, made fresh with esoteric guitar tones and production. Opener Shade is a perfect radio track at just over two minutes. The urgent, syncopated drums drive the song forward quickly through short sections of searching, jangly guitar. Who Is Me? is even shorter, abandoning its middle eight and kicking straight back into the chorus. The modulating guitar is reminiscent of Kevin Shields, but the pressing, almost-melodic drumming keeps the song from becoming a cerebral mess. With subtleties thickly layered, the song never feels too short when it clocks in at 1:59. There are longer, more sparse tracks as well, and they too feel precisely weighted. Admittedly they don�t contain as much nuance under close scrutiny, though the sense of loneliness and desolation they afford proves less-is-more in this case. Unfortunately, the changes of pace highlight the sporadic nature of the record, and that�s its main failing. For while individual tracks are incredibly sophisticated in their songwriting, as an album Play! feels somewhat haphazard, and some songs suffer as interest falters. Still, this is an excellent release of timeless Australian rock.

JAKEB SMITH
