Gwyneth Herbert - Clangers And Mash

Review by Jack Foley

product

IndieLondon Rating: 4 out of 5

GWYNETH Herbert is an artist who enjoys redefining (and defying) our expectations.

A singer-songwriter with one foot in the jazz world and one somewhere in the future, Herbert has decided to come over all playful to mark the release of a new recording of Perfect Fit. Hence, the mini-album Clangers And Mash, a nine-track offering that allows the singer to collaborate with some of her favourite friends and artists… as well as underline her musical diversity.

Herbert says of the album herself: “I wanted to do something a bit different to mark the release of our new single, Perfect Fit, so I scrabbled around for a few backstage scrapings and asked a handful of my lovely and varied musician friends to present some Herbert tracks as they’d never been heard before.

“The result is Clangers and Mash: bleepy, tappy, pleasureful ear food to tickle your bellies. Tuck in.”

The ensuing feast is nothing you can have been anticipating. Sometimes it’s extremely nourishing, occasionally there’s the odd piece that doesn’t quite taste right, but the overall result leaves you extremely satisfied and more than a little hungry for more.

The main song, Perfect Fit, is an absolutely beautiful slice of kooky folk-pop that sounds as though it could be tailor-made for the soundtrack of a film like Juno. It’s an enchanting love song, beautifully delivered by Herbert’s lush vocals, and brimming with warm melodies.

It’s repeated a further twice on the LP, including a similarly charming and even more kooky version with The Voluntary Butler Scheme (who, like Herbert, rates among our favourite new artists).

But elsewhere, there’s also plenty to savour, including a darker slice of music making in the Polar Bear mix of My Mini & Me, which showcases an edgier side to the Herbert make-up and serves as a nice contrast to the happy themes of the main song.

Mandolin and simple whistling ushers in another of the LP’s heart-warmers: So Worn Out, while hand-clap beats and simple strumming accompany the beautifully delivered Petite Cacahuete (in French).

I wasn’t entirely taken with the Girl After Shower Remix of My Narrow Man (as it never really gets going, or makes the most of Herbert’s vocals), or the stripped back nighttime lullaby that is Midnight Oil. But even though these didn’t rate as favourites, Herbert is always doing something interesting with her songs.

Overall, though, this is a great listen and further compelling proof of why Herbert is one of the UK’s most treasured (and diverse) female artists.

Download picks: Perfect Fit (all versions), My Mini & Me, So Worn Out, Petite Cacahuete

Track listing:

  1. Perfect Fit (Radio Edit)
  2. My Mini & Me (Polar Bear Remix)
  3. So Worn Out (In The Bedroom)
  4. My Narrow Man (Girl After Shower Remix)
  5. Perfect Fit (Mr Solo & The Voluntary Butler Scheme Remix)
  6. Petite Cacahuete
  7. So Worn Out (Temper D Remix)
  8. Perfect Fit (Original Version)
  9. Midnight Oil (A cappella)
